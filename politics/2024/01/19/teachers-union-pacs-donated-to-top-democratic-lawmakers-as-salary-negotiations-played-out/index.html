Teachers union PACs donated to top Democratic lawmakers as salary negotiations played out

LAS VEGAS (KLAS) -- Amid fierce negotiations between the teachers union and the school district, a political action committee run by the head of the union doled out some $300,000 in donations to four Democratic state legislators in Las Vegas within one-to-two days of a major finance committee meeting, records from the secretary of state's office show.

The Interim Finance Committee, which handles budgetary items while the legislature is out of session, ultimately postponed its decision on approving new contracts for school employees at the Dec. 13 meeting.

But the timing is enough to get a strong reaction from the Clark County School District because the union's PAC gave $150,000 to Nevada Strong PAC, run by  Democratic Assembly Speaker Steve Yeager, on Dec. 12. The donating PAC, known as Strategic Horizons and run by Clark County Educators Association Executive Director John Vellardita, gave the other three Democrats their donations on the two days following the IFC's meeting.

The PAC run by Senate Majority Leader Nicole Cannizzaro, Battle Born and Raised Leadership, reported a $50,000 donation on Dec. 14. On Dec. 15, PACs run by Assemblywoman Shea Backus (Our Best Nevada) and state Sen. Rochelle Nguyen (Nguyening Leadership) received $60,000 and $40,000, respectively.

"If this is how our elected representatives make decisions, then public agencies, like school districts – whose finances must be transparent – stand no chance of shaping sound, student-centered policies," CCSD wrote in a statement. "The suspicious timing and nature of CCEA’s donations call for more transparency about how they operate and negotiate. Otherwise, Nevada taxpayers are the losers."

CCSD, whose entire statement appears at the bottom of this article, did not submit a plan for teacher raises at that Dec. 13 meeting. 

But Yeager – along with Cannizzaro and other Democrats – was an outspoken advocate for teacher raises. The two sides ultimately agreed on a new contract, giving teachers a 10% raise this year and an 8% raise next year.

"Legislative Democrats said from the first day of the 2023 legislative session that substantial teacher raises were a top priority to help districts address ongoing teacher vacancy in many of our schools," Yeager, who was traveling Thursday, said in a statement to the 8 News Now Investigators. "Our members are proud that as a direct result of legislation Democrats passed last year, teachers across the state are seeing historic pay raises as high as 20%."

Vellardita worded his statement – which appears in its entirety at the bottom of this article – more strongly.

"Any correlation of CCEA donations to participants of that meeting appears to be the view of conspiracy theorists," Vellardita, who has called for the resignation of CCSD Superintendent Dr. Jesus Jara, said in a statement. "As the 2024 election cycle marches on CCEA will continue to endorse and make donations to candidates that advance improving public education.”

A review of similar records from 2022 show similar – but smaller – donations to some of these elected officials.

The full CCSD statement:

The decision to defer our SB 231 request for funds for our Education Support Employees Association was unconscionable when, according to the rules at the time, our application was complete and included all required documents. The suspicious timing of these donations by CCEA leadership to legislative leadership and IFC members raises troubling questions about the relationship between CCEA, legislative leadership, and IFC members to arbitrarily delay our application and punish our support professionals who need these funds most. It is troubling that our lowest-paid employees could be victims of a legislative leadership game.

If this is how our elected representatives make decisions, then public agencies, like school districts – whose finances must be transparent – stand no chance of shaping sound, student-centered policies. The suspicious timing and nature of CCEA’s donations call for more transparency about how they operate and negotiate. Otherwise, Nevada taxpayers are the losers.

Full statement from CCEA Executive Director John Vellardita:

In every election cycle CCEA will support candidates for state office that advocate for improvement in public education. The 2024 election cycle is no different. CCEA did not request funds at the IFC meeting. Any correlation of CCEA donations to participants of that meeting appears to be the view of conspiracy theorists. As the 2024 election cycle marches on CCEA will continue to endorse and make donations to candidates that advance improving public education.
