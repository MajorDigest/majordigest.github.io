Ballots en route for special election for 20th Congressional District

BAKERSFIELD, Calif. (KGET) -- In the coming week, some voters in Kern, Kings, Fresno and Tulare counties will be getting another ballot for a special election.

This special election is to fill out the remainder of former Bakersfield Congressman Kevin McCarthy's term, which would've ended January 2025.

The special election is separate from the March 5 presidential primary. So, how did we get here?

Last December, McCarthy resigned from Congress mid-term, leaving his office vacant until January 2025. To temporarily fill that vacancy, Gov. Gavin Newsom called for a special election.

With this in mind, there are some key election dates fast approaching.

Again, there's the regularly scheduled March 5 presidential primary, for which voters should have already received a ballot. Two weeks later, on March 19, there's the primary special election.

Following this, there could be a runoff election on May 21 with the top two vote getters, if no candidate gets 50% (+1) of the votes in March.

"We have our March 5 ballot and that's our normally scheduled election," said Kern County Auditor-Controller-County Clerk Aimee Espinoza. "The 20th district is a special election, and so the only race on that ballot is going to be the race for the 20th Congressional District."

"If you're not sure if you live in the 20th District, you can go out to our website and you can look at who your representatives are, and you put in your address," Espinoza added.

Ballots for the special election have been mailed out. Espinoza said voter should contact elections if they don't receive the ballot by the end of next week.

The special election ballot will look the same as any other election ballot, other than that there's only one race on this one. The return envelope will be orange for color coding purposes.

You can mail back these ballots or drop them off at any of the 19 county drop boxes.

The sample ballot and candidate statements will all be included in the ballot packaging. Everything you need for the special election will be included in the ballot wrap.

Espinoza emphasized only those registered to vote in the 20th Congressional District will be receiving and be able to participate in this special election. She added that about half of Kern's registered voters live in CD-20.

For those who want to vote in this election and have a ballot sent to them, the last day to register to vote is March 4.

After this deadline, voters will have to register in person at their county election offices or at a poll site on Election Day.
