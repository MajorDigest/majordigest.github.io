CD-20 debate sparks some moments of tension, talks on national and Central Valley issues

BAKERSFIELD, Calif. (KGET) -- Thursday night, four candidates running to replace Kevin McCarthy in California's 20th Congressional District took to the debate stage in the 17 News studio.

Ten wide-ranging topics were discussed, and while candidates didn't really address one another during the debate, targeted criticism came during one-on-one interviews after we went off air.

A notable moment of tension was between the two Republican candidates regarding recent endorsements.

"It's very clear, after we received the endorsements of two of the most popular candidates in the Kern County area, who are actually friends of Mr. Fong who endorsed me, Sheriff Donny Youngblood and Shannon Grove, Mr. [Kevin] McCarthy pulled a political favor so that [Vince Fong] could have the endorsement of Mr. Trump," Tulare County Sheriff and CD-20 Republican candidate Mike Boudreaux said during the debate.

To which Bakersfield Assemblymember Vince Fong responded, "Well it's not a political favor. I'll put my endorsements locally over anybody's."

These candidates, considered the top two Republicans in the race, referring to recent endorsements that caused them further tension and split Kern County's GOP.

"Donald Trump doesn't even know Vince Fong. I don't take away from the fact that he received it. But he received it as a political favor," Boudreaux added in a post-debate interview with 17 News.

Fong and Boudreaux on the right; local educator Marisa Wood and security officer Andy Morales on the left, were the four candidates who qualified for the debate Thursday night.

There were topics specific to the Central Valley: "And quite frankly, I believe that our farmers shouldn't be charged one dime for water," as Boudreaux said, as well as national issues: "We should not be spending another dime in aiding Israel and their genocide in Gaza," as Morales said.

Top-of-mind for candidates were immigration and border security.

"We need physical barriers, we need technology, we need to give the border control flexibility, we need more border control, we need to end catch and release," Fong said.

Wood also acknowledged the need to resolve the border crisis but noted safety measures must be upheld for those "seeking asylum."

Boudreaux answered, "I do not believe that we should spend one more taxpayer dime going to Ukraine, but I do believe we should support our strongest and most supportive ally in Israel. But combining that into our border is a mistake."

And Morales said, "Rather than waste the money on sending aid to other countries, how about we just expand resources to our courts?"

The congressmember hopefuls also weighed in on the tying of border security to foreign aid.

For Democrats? It was no aid to Israel.

17's Jenny Huh: "You said all hostages should be released. Is that the first step in calling for a ceasefire?"

Marisa Wood: "The first step is a ceasefire. I'm non-negotiable on that."

For Republican: "If they begin to get overtaken and need our military, then yes, our military would need to help Israel... Boots on the ground," Boudreaux said.

And on key Central Valley issues like crime, candidates also had a lot to say.

17's Jim Scott: "Have progressive policies gone too far?"
Vince Fong: "Absolutely."

While Republicans pushed for being tougher on crime and accountability, Democrats argued tackling root causes was the way to go.

As Marisa Wood said, "High levels of poverty and crime... without acknowledging that, all the accountability pieces mean nothing."

We are just 11 days away from the March 5 primary election in California.

Remember, these are just four of 11 candidates running for this open seat in Congress.

Source: Jenny Huh, KGET 17, NBC
