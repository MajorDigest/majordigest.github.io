Chula Vista City Council District 4: Meet the candidates

CHULA VISTA, Calif. (FOX 5/KUSI) -- Six candidates are running for Chula Vista City Council's District 4 in the March 5 primary election.

Delfina Gonzalez, Christine Brady, Cesar Fernandez, José Sarmiento and Rudy Ramirez are all vying to replace former Councilmember Andrea Cardenas, who is also on the ballot even following her recent resignation on Feb. 19 as she faces multiple felony charges, including money laundering. Cardenas resigned from her seat on the council one day before she appeared in court and was faced with an additional felony charge: grand theft.

Cardenas’ vacancy for the current term will be declared Feb. 27, at which point council has 45 days to appoint a new councilmember for the remainder of the current term. If no one is appointed, that seat would remain vacant until November.

District 4 covers the southwest section of Chula Vista.

Who are the candidates?

Christine Brady

Brady is an engineer, educator and CEO. Her priorities include reestablishing fiscal responsibility, providing a definitive and humane solution to homelessness, creating opportunities for economic development and creating new arts, culture, educational and recreational programs to serve the youth of West Chula Vista. Find out more about Brady's views on her website and by watching her interview with FOX 5 News below.

Cesar Fernandez

Fernandez is a teacher and Chula Vista Elementary School board member. His priorities include "better jobs, safer streets, and a fair shake for Southwest Chula Vista." Fernandez's endorsements include San Diego County Board of Supervisors Chair Nora Vargas, State Sen. Stephen Padilla, Rep. Juan Vargas and former Chula Vista Mayor and Assemblymember Mary Casillas Salas, among others. Find out more about Fernandez's views on his website.

Delfina Gonzalez

Gonzalez is a union member, educator and small business owner. Her priorities include fiscal responsibility, safe neighborhoods and economic empowerment. Find out more about Gonzalez's views on her website and by watching her interview with FOX 5 News below.

Rudy Ramirez

Ramirez is a metal fabrication business owner who was a former member of the Chula Vista City Council between 2006 and 2015. His priorities include public safety, helping the homeless and building more cooperation and communication between the community and police. Find out more about Ramirez's views on his website and by watching his interview with FOX 5 News below.

José Sarmiento

Sarmiento is a bank manager. His priorities include public safety and homeless prevention. Find out more about Sarmiento's views on his website and by watching his interview with FOX 5 News below.
