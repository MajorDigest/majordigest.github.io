CD-20 special election: Where Fong, Boudreaux support is strongest so far

BAKERSFIELD, Calif. (KGET) -- As results from the March 19 special election continue to trickle in, Republican Assemblymember Vince Fong maintains the lead districtwide with 41% of the votes.

Remember, the AP has projected Fong will advance to the May runoff election.

In that runoff, he'll likely face Republican Tulare County Sheriff Mike Boudreaux, currently in second place with about 26% of the vote, or Democrat Marisa Wood, in third place with about 23% of the votes.

"I think the campaign was very aggressive," said Central Valley political strategist Tal Eslick, who supports Fong for Congress. "It was pretty well funded. I'm not sure what would have changed other than fewer candidates that would have provided the ability for him to get to the majority."

The campaign of discussion is that of Fong.

Endorsed by former President Donald Trump and longtime mentor Kevin McCarthy, Fong is considered a top-tier GOP candidate in the race to replace McCarthy in Congress.

But name ID and high-profile endorsements still may not have been enough to secure 50%+1 of the votes in the special election, Eslick noted.

If any of the nine candidates gets that majority no runoff is needed. They would win outright.

Eslick added that appears unlikely in this multi-candidate field.

"We are prepared for any candidate," Fong told 17 News on Tuesday after polls closed. "We don't know what the results are going to be [of the special election], but in a nine-person field, we are prepared to run against anybody."

So far districtwide, Fong leads Republican Tulare County Sheriff Mike Boudreaux by 15 points.

It appears the two Republicans -- as the top two vote getters -- will face off in a May runoff, just as they'll be advancing to the November general election together for the full two-year term in the House.

Fong is leading in Kern, his hometown, as well as Fresno County; Boudreaux is leading in Tulare -- his home base -- and Kings County.

"The biggest hurdle is going to be bringing who I am to the Kern County area," Boudreaux said in an interview Tuesday night. "I'm very well-known in Tulare, Kings and Fresno County, that's not the hurdle."

On the likelihood of a runoff election, Eslick added, "It's sort of funny and sad that voters in this district are going to have the privilege and the burden of voting with largely the same candidates four times in one election year."
