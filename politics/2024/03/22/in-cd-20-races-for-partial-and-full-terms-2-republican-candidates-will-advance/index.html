In CD-20 races for partial and full terms, 2 Republican candidates will advance

BAKERSFIELD, Calif. (KGET) -- March has been busy for voters in the 20th Congressional District with back-to-back elections.

It looks like Republicans -- Vince Fong and Mike Boudreaux -- will advance in their bids to fill the remainder of Kevin McCarthy's current term in Congress and for the next full term as representative of CD-20.

The Fong campaign declined an interview in studio Thursday, but 17 News asked Fong a few questions during his special election watch party Tuesday.

"You have two great, Republican candidates, both of which have different backgrounds and different styles of leadership," said Tulare County Sheriff Mike Boudreaux in an interview with 17 News Thursday morning.

That is what the race to replace Kevin McCarthy in Congress has come down to, said the sheriff, one of top two vote getters in both the March 5 primary election and March 19 special election for CD-20.

Boudreaux will face Republican Assemblymember Vince Fong, first in a May runoff special election to fill the remainder of the term McCarthy left early, then the November general election for a full two-year term.

"I never thought that we would be in the number one position because we have a lot of work to do," the sheriff said. "We have a lot of water to carry uphill quite frankly."

With Kern County being Fong's home base and making up 50% of the district's votes, Boudreaux noted his focus is on voters here.

17's Jenny Huh: "And you don't deny that persuading those people may be hard?"

Mike Boudreaux: "Very difficult. I think it'll be difficult out of loyalty to Vince, and I respect that loyalty."

He added, "I need to be down here, I need to make sure people see me, I need to shake hands, so they're comfortable with me ... The biggest challenge is people meeting me down here. Wanting to shake hands and learn a little bit about me when there's obviously an established group of people here that have put their support behind Vince."

Though, Boudreaux clarified, he and Fong were never on bad terms.

"As I got into this campaign, Vince was not allowed to be in the campaign," Boudreaux said. "He was told by the Secretary of State you can't run. Hence the reason I'm here. I didn't get into this race to push against Vince or McCarthy or anyone else."

Fong is now in the midst of an ongoing lawsuit on whether he can run for Congress, since he's also on the ballot for State Assembly.

"There's no [legal] limbo," Fong said Tuesday night. "We are going to be 100% successful."

Boudreaux emphasized the ongoing litigation is a big reason why he, a top-tier GOP candidate, needs to remain in the race -- in case Fong is disqualified and cannot pursue a term in Congress.

Boudreaux noted he hopes if he becomes the leading candidate, Fong could support him, and that while he hopes the best for Fong, the law reads Fong should not be allowed to run.

And negative political advertisements, some by the Fong campaign and some by groups supporting Fong, put a dent in what Boudreaux had predicted would be a gentleman's race.

17's Jenny Huh: "Did you anticipate there to be such a split coming into this race?"

Mike Boudreaux: "You know, I did. I did because we have Republican voters who are frustrated. They're tired of what they've always had."

"Always had," in reference to the long political reign of Kevin McCarthy and his local Kern County supporters.

"Kevin McCarthy is his friend, I wouldn't have expected it any other way," Boudreaux said of McCarthy's endorsement of Fong for Congress.

The sheriff also noted he hopes to unite all voters behind him, districtwide and regardless of political party.

When asked how he'd earn the votes of Democrats, who may not see eye-to-eye with him on issues like immigration, the Second Amendment, abortion and support for former President Donald Trump, Boudreaux answered he has a track record of working across the aisle and that Republicans and Democrats can find middle ground on various topics.

Boudreaux now has the endorsements of fellow CD-20 candidates Kyle Kirkland, David Giglio and T.J. Esposito, who've altogether gotten over 9,000 votes districtwide.

In a statement, Kirkland said in part, "I know Mike will represent District 20 with the same authenticity, hard work, and dignity he has shown throughout his career and in this campaign."
