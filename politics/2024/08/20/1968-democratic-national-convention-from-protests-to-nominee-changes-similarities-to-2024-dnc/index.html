1968 Democratic National Convention: From protests to nominee changes, similarities to 2024 DNC

BAKERSFIELD, Calif. (KGET) -- The Harris-Walz campaign secured the Democratic nomination via an online vote early this month.

So, while this convention is expected to be more of a celebration, many are drawing parallels to the DNC of 1968 -- remembered for the disorderly last-minute candidate nomination, fist fights on the convention floor and violent protests against the Vietnam War.

Kern County's own Eddy Laine attended that convention 56 years ago.

He said the '68 convention, which took place from August 26-29, was supposed to be a big happy party of unity but turned into a disaster in and out of the convention hall.

"The idea was an orderly convention and that things would go well, the Democratic process would work, it didn't work at all," he said.

Then-20-year-old Eddy Laine recalled the Democratic National Convention of 1968, in one word, as raucous.

As a college sophomore working part-time to save up to attend both the DNC and RNC, what was his in? He said he was lucky enough to stumble upon a team of Chicago Mayor Richard J. Daley supporter.

"Well heck, I wanted to go to the convention, so how can I go? Sit down with us and go. So, I was given a Daley hat and a pass," Laine said.

But what awaited him was a party divided.

"The average Democrat didn't want Hubert Humphrey, and there they were stifled," Laine said. "Then on the streets, you had peaceful protests being suppressed by tanks?"

"So, the overall effect for the party and the country was just one of shock. What's going on with the Democrats?" Laine added.

Disorder like last-minute changes to the top of the ticket is also what faces this year's DNC, as it returns to the Windy City for the first time since 1968.

"I don't know of any convention since '68… where the nominee wasn't sewed up ahead of time, and it wasn't just a production," Laine reflected.

President Lyndon B. Johnson -- much like President Biden -- announced he would not seek reelection just months before the convention, instead endorsing Vice President Hubert Humphrey.

Humphrey became the Democratic nominee but lost to Republican Richard Nixon.

And the Democratic delegation was split among competing options like senators Eugene McCarthy and Robert F. Kennedy, who like his brother John F. Kennedy, was assassinated.

Following that death, there were efforts to draft another Kennedy brother -- Ted Kennedy.

"Now, Ted Kennedy, with his brother just killed, he didn't want to be the nominee, but there were delegates who wanted to draft him," Laine explained.

He continued, "When [President] Johnson backed out, [John F.] Kennedy came in, and Kennedy and McCarthy then fought through the primaries... Humphrey was already running as the rubber-stamped machine. But Kennedy thought he could persuade the party bosses to be the party nominee. But of course, that all ended when he was assassinated."

Back then, it was the Vietnam War. Now, it's the Israel-Hamas War, with thousands already protesting for a permanent ceasefire.

"There were thousands, mainly young people protesting the war in Vietnam," Laine stated. "They cleaned out the park with all sorts of what we would now say are not acceptable, put a whole bunch of people in jail. It was bloody."

Laine also recalled what happened inside the convention hall -- and it was no less rowdy.

"The tension, the hollering, the fist fights in the convention hall were astounding," Laine said. "Mayor Daley had tanks sent down the streets of Chicago, and this was the very same time Russia was invading Czechoslovakia, so the parallel was chilling."

He added, "Who would've thought there'd be tanks on the streets when he did this?" referring to the many 'Welcome to Chicago' posters that put on a warm, friendly environment in the tension-growing city.

"For decades now, both parties, especially because of '68, both parties have been very careful to choreograph their conventions, so it's a slick show," Laine concluded.

Only the next few days will tell if history will repeat itself.
