SF Giants sign former Cardinals All-Star shortstop, onetime trade deadline target

PHILADELPHIA — As they continue to seek to shore up their middle infield, the Giants agreed to terms with a former All-Star shortstop who was recently designated for assignment.

Paul DeJong, the former Cardinal, will join the Giants on Wednesday after signing a major-league deal, a league source confirmed to the Bay Area News Group. The move, first reported by USA Today’s Bob Nightengale, is pending a physical and has not been publicly announced by the club.

DeJong, 30, was a target of the Giants leading up to the Aug. 2 trade deadline, a source said, but was ultimately sent from St. Louis, where he spent the first six-and-a-half years of his career, to the Toronto Blue Jays.

Batting .233/.297/.412 for the Cardinals, DeJong was enjoying his best offensive season since being named an All-Star in 2019 but recorded only three hits in 44 at-bats — with 18 strikeouts and no walks — after joining the Blue Jays. Toronto DFA’d him over the weekend when its starting shortstop, Bo Bichette, returned from the injured list.

DeJong can serve a similar stopgap role for the Giants, who recently placed Brandon Crawford on the injured list for the third time this season with a left forearm strain. He will likely take the roster spot of Johan Camargo, who managed to go from minor-league free agent to the top of the Giants’ shortstop depth chart and the sixth hole in their lineup in the span of a couple weeks.

Crawford, whose batting average had dipped to .194 due to a 1-for-28 slump before landing on the IL, underwent an MRI that showed a mild strain.

“I don’t know if that means he’s going to be out, you know, seven days, 10 days,” manager Gabe Kapler said before Tuesday’s game in Philadelphia. “But it means obviously that we’re going to be without him for a little bit of time.”

While DeJong slugged 74 homers during his first three big-league seasons, including 25 while finishing as runner-up for Rookie of the Year in 2017, he has been a below league-average hitter ever since 2020, with just 41 homers combined over the four seasons since.

DeJong has almost exclusively played shortstop, where he is considered an above-average defender.

Lacking depth up the middle from the start of the season, the Giants’ needs have only become greater due to injury and underperformance.

Young players such as Brett Wisely and Casey Schmitt have struggled to hit major-league pitching. Top shortstop prospect Marco Luciano is sidelined after straining his hamstring at Triple-A after getting a cup of coffee in the majors. And Thairo Estrada, while averting the worst-case scenario, was just hit by a pitch in similar fashion to the one that broke his hand and cost him a month on the IL.

“The depth will get stronger as we add back to the roster,” Kapler said.

Meanwhile, the Giants opted for DeJong over Tyler Fitzgerald, another young shortstop who has been on a tear at Triple-A Sacramento.

There will many 40-man moves in the coming days and weeks. San Francisco must create space for Kyle Harrison, set to make his debut Tuesday, and DeJong, as well as Mitch Haniger and John Brebbia, who both began rehab assignments this week and aren’t far off from being activated from the 60-day IL.

Notable

— The Giants and A’s also pulled off a rare post-deadline trade and an ever rarer swap between the Bay Bridge rivals. San Francisco sent LHP Sean Newcomb, a onetime top prospect with Atlanta, in exchange for IF/OF Trenton Brooks, who was batting .299/.405/.529 (a .934 OPS) for the A’s Triple-A affiliate. Neither player appeared on a 40-man roster this season or spent time on an MLB injured list, allowing for the post-deadline trade. The clubs haven’t pulled off a swap of major league players since Darren Lewis was sent to Oakland for Ernie Riles in 1990.

— RHP Ryan Walker rejoined the club, beaming with the pride of fresh fatherhood. His wife, Alexis, safely delivered their first child on Saturday — a girl, Rylie, who was born healthy without complications, weighing 6 pounds, 10 ounces, despite arriving a month early. RHP Sean Hjelle and OF Heliot Ramos were optioned to Triple-A Sacramento to clear space for Walker and LHP Kyle Harrison, who was officially added to the roster for his debut.
