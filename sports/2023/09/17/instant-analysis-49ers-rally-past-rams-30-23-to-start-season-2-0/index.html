Instant analysis: 49ers rally past Rams 30-23 to start season 2-0

INGLEWOOD, Calif. – Sure, Brock Purdy will agonize over three overthrows that spoiled potential touchdowns on the way to the 49ers’ 30-23 victory Sunday.

He took solace, though, in these key stats: No interceptions, no turnovers, no loss to mourn.

Los Angeles Rams counterpart Matthew Stafford could not do the same. The 49ers intercepted Stafford twice to pull away from a 17-17 halftime tie and take a 2-0 record into Thursday’s home opener against the New York Giants.

“Obviously today it came down to not turning the ball over. We knew that going into it,” Purdy said. “For myself, it’s, ‘I have to make plays for my team,’ but, at the same time, protect the ball more than anything, let our defense play and see what happens.

“That was the name of the game today.”

Second-half interceptions by cornerbacks Isaiah Oliver and Deommodore Lenoir led to Jake Moody field goals to help doom the scrappy Rams, who’ve lost nine straight regular-season meetings in this intrastate rivalry, which again attracted a pro-49ers crowd to SoFi Stadium.

“What sealed it and allowed us to separate ourselves were those two turnovers we got,” coach Kyle Shanahan said. “One, Demo awesome job jumping in, and Isaiah with the other off a tip. And we didn’t turn it over.”

Don’t underestimate that last sentence.

Purdy has not been intercepted this season (54 passes). Last season, he yielded just four interceptions in 262 attempts (18 touchdowns), including the playoffs. He’s gone six games (and 182 passes) without getting intercepted, dating to the third quarter of a New Year’s Day overtime win in Las Vegas.

“Everyone is less shocked now when he makes great plays and good decisions. That’s just who he is. That’s part of winning football games,” running back Christian McCaffrey said. “It’s a four-quarter fight. He did a great job taking care of the ball, always does. Keep it up.”

Not counting last December’s win over the Dolphins in relief of Jimmy Garoppolo, Purdy is 7-0 in regular-season starts. Sunday, he passed for 206 yards (17-of-25) and ran for a 1-yard touchdown as the first half expired to pull the 49ers into a 17-17 tie after managing a two-minute drill with key completions to Deebo Samuel and Jennings.

Purdy initially was credited in the fourth quarter with an 11-yard touchdown pass to Deebo Samuel until a scoring change correctly identified the pass as a lateral. Samuel powered and deftly maneuvered his way through defenders on the left sideline to put the 49ers ahead 27-17 with 11:28 left.

Samuel is one of three receivers Purdy overshot earlier in the game. Instead of a 39-yard touchdown strike to Samuel late in the third quarter, the 49ers settled for a 57-yard field goal from Moody, the longest of his two-game career and the 49ers’ longest since David Akers’ 63-yarder in the 2012 opening win at Green Bay.

Purdy also had third-down overthrows to Brandon Aiyuk and Jauan Jennings. Were those a matter of Purdy not knowing his own strength after his March 10 elbow surgery?

“No, I don’t think so,” Purdy answered. “We’ve hit those deep balls at practice. It comes down to that when the bullets are flying, make accurate throws, don’t overthrow, don’t underthrow, and be on point. That’s what you have to do as a NFL quarterback and I take that on myself.”

“The main thing he did today was just protect the ball,” Shanahan said. “Besides those three misses, which none were easy, he took care of the football, didn’t give them a chance to touch it, and when he was under duress, he was smart.”

Linebacker Fred Warner praised Stafford for taking what the 49ers defense was giving him for quick, short throws. He completed 22-of-25 passes (205 yards) until Oliver intercepted a pass off Kyren Williams’ hands. That first turnover of the game staved off a potential go-ahead score by the Rams.

Moody’s 57-yard field goal followed, and after Warner notched the 49ers’ only sack of Stafford on the day, Samuel delivered his first touchdown of the season (and eighth in nine career games against the Rams).

“I’ve gotten used to it with him,” Shanahan said of Samuel’s style. “That was the look we wanted, which usually means it gets six yards. Then Deebo has a knack for getting a lot more than that.”

Nick Bosa didn’t sack Stafford but did hit him three times, and Bosa closed in on the veteran quarterback on Lenoir’s fourth-quarter interception.

This wasn’t a cakewalk like last Sunday’s opener, when the 49ers rolled the host Pittsburgh Steelers 30-7. Then again, that’s back-to-back games with 30 points. Shanahan said their weekly scoring potential is high.

“I feel that way every year,” he said. I’m extremely irritated when we don’t score. You expect to score. That’s how you try to attack and what our players expect.”

The Rams’ only second-half points came on fourth-quarter field goals by Brett Maher, the final of which left no time on the clock to attempt an ensuing onside kick and last-ditch rally.

Rams defensive star Aaron Donald had no tackles, no sacks and just one hit on Purdy.

“We commit everything to (stop) him, because if you don’t, he wrecks everything,” Shanahan said.

Bosa remains without a sack through two games, but he had three quarterback hurries this outing and felt he improved. As for his heavier workload with only a few days to rest before facing the Giants, Bosa said he is not concerned about his conditioning.

“I needed a couple of games to get my body into football shape,” Bosa said. “Now that I’m through two and had a pretty good output, I’m only going to be up from here.”

For the second time in as many games, the 49ers scored on their opening possession, which McCaffrey capped with a 14-yard run. That 75-yard march was mostly stress-free, until Aiyuk appeared to injure his shoulder on a third-down conversion to the 22-yard line but remained in the game although rookie Ronnie Bell rotated in with him. McCaffrey finished with 116 yards on 20 carries.

For a second straight game, the 49ers appeared to escape without any significant injuries.  Cornerback Ambry Thomas (knee) exited in the second quarter, and a corresponding move saw the 49ers insert Oliver at nickel back to keep Lenoir at right cornerback. Thomas returned late in the third quarter to replace Lenoir, who was evaluated for a possible concussion and ultimately cleared.
