Bay Area News Group high school football predictions: Week 5, 2023

It’s that time of the week again — 16 more picks as we head into the fifth week of the high school football season.

My advantage has widened over the two others in the Bay Area News Group’s all-in-fun competition — Mike Lefkow and Joseph Dycus — after I went 10-6 in Week 4.

Lefkow finished 9-7. Dycus went 7-9.

Dycus is eight games back in the season standings, and Lefkow is five games out of first.

As was the case last week, Dycus can make up ground if he has a good week. He went against yours truly in five of the 16 games on this week’s list, choosing Logan over Heritage, Palo Alto over Menlo-Atherton, Bellarmine over Valley Christian, Folsom over De La Salle and (yes) Milpitas over California.

Lefkow and I agreed on every game but two: He picked Castlemont over Mt. Diablo and St. Ignatius over Riordan.

In this week’s video, I lost track of the games we were picking. Oops. My apologies to Logan and Heritage and Lincoln and Silver Creek. Our picks are below.

In our pre-picks roundtable, we gave our thoughts on De La Salle-Folsom and the impact games such as Campolindo at Monte Vista on Friday will have in the North Coast Section Division I selection and seeding process.

It wasn’t mentioned in the video, but Lefkow notes that league champions automatically qualify for the playoffs. Campo could lose Friday and still win the Diablo Athletic League’s Foothill Division.

If you want just the predictions, keep scrolling.
