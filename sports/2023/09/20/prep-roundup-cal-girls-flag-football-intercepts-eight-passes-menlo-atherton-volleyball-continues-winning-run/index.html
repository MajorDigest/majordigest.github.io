Prep roundup: California girls intercept eight passes in flag football game; Menlo-Atherton volleyball continues winning run

Girls flag football

California 34, Foothill 18

The Grizzlies’ defense picked off an astounding eight Foothill passes on Tuesday night, which included two pick-sixes. Janie Trottier had two interceptions, and Emma Perry, Olivia Horton and Priya Mistry each had one. Nadia Sherman, who had two picks, and Lauren Grgurina each returned one interception for a touchdown. 

Offensively, Grgurina also threw for one touchdown and caught another. Kylie Tabor passed for two touchdowns, and Olivia Young and Mary Dilling each caught a TD.

California coach Frank Grgurina said his team was supposed to play Benicia in the second game of the night, but due to poor air quality caused by wildfires near the Oregon border, the game was called off. Grgurina told the Bay Area News Group that it would be rescheduled for a later date.

San Ramon Valley 32, Livermore 6

In the first game of the night, Onessa Yee did it all for the Wolves in a dominant victory over Livermore. She threw one touchdown pass to Samara Sinohue, ran five times for 53 yards and a touchdown, and caught two passes for 16 yards and another TD. 

Senior quarterback Kaitlyn Bray threw a touchdown to Ella Gunderson, who herself had three receptions for 42 yards. Madi Alberti led the team with three receptions for 60 yards, Llianna Marge caught one pass for 28 yards and Kate Herrera had two receptions for 13 yards. Kyla Fogelman ran the ball four times for 31 yards. 

San Ramon Valley 20, Monte Vista 0

SRV’s defense dominated its second matchup, with coach Chad Cochran naming defender Sydney Gann the player of the game. During a four-play goal-line stand, Gann had two pass breakups to keep Monte Vista off the board. Hania Bowes, the younger sister of star SRV basketball player Sofia Bowes, also returned an interception for a touchdown.

Bray completed 14 passes for 123 yards and two touchdowns, Yee catching one and Gunderson (78 yards) catching the other for one of her six receptions. Sinohue had four receptions for 50 yards, and Yee rushed five times for 38 yards. 

San Ramon Valley improved to 8-2 with the two victories. 

Girls volleyball 

No. 2 St. Francis 3, Archbishop Riordan 0

The Lancers were dominant against another West Catholic Athletic League opponent, this time defeating Riordan. However, Riordan coach Bob Greene said he was proud of his team’s effort, telling Bay Area News Group, “We really battled in the second set.” St. Francis won 25-10, 26-24, 25-17. 

It took five sets to decide a winner in a matchup between the two best teams in the East Bay Athletic League, with Foothill winning 25-13, 24-26, 25-16, 24-26, 15-12. Senior outside hitter Paige Bennett had 29 kills and 14 digs, and junior outside hitter Kaycie Burdick had 17 kills and 14 digs. Senior setter Katie Salonga had 61 assists and 10 digs as Foothill improved to 17-1, and Amador Valley dropped 14-4.

No. 8 Burlingame 3, Half Moon Bay 0

Burlingame won 25-14, 25-23, 25-19 to triumph in its ninth consecutive victory and improve to 4-0 in Peninsula Athletic League Bay Division play. Junior outside hitter Ella Duong had 12 kills, and senior outside hitter Morgan Toomey was just behind her with 11. Sophomore middle blocker Ava Scatena has seven kills .

Half Moon Bay still had several standouts. Junior outside hitter Kayla Andrew had five aces, and juniors Amelia Ortolan and Gabby Harrison combined for 11 kills. Senior outside hitter Ava Cardiel had eight digs, and Kayla Andrew and junior libero Kate Billington each had seven digs. 

No. 9 San Ramon Valley 3, Monte Vista 0

San Ramon Valley rebounded from losses to St. Francis and Archbishop Mitty with a victory over Danville rival Monte Vista by winning 25-19, 25-18, 25-14. Junior outside hitter Lucy Chertock had 10 kills, four assists, an ace, one block and 13 digs. Senior outside Maddie Connelly hitter had 10 kills, one dig and two blocks, and senior setter Audree Vasconi had 10 assists, two aces and three digs. SRV improved to 12-4 and 5-1 in EBAL play, and Monte Vista dropped to 10-7, 3-2.

No. 11 Menlo-Atherton 3, San Mateo 0

The Bears rolled to their seventh consecutive victory behind contributions from numerous standouts. Junior Meki Maile had five aces and eight digs, and senior Samantha Lin had four aces. Junior Dani Eline had 11 kills and one ace, and junior Duru Ruacan and senior Aria Sokol combined for 15 kills. M-A won 25-14, 25-23, 25-6 to improve to 8-4 overall and 4-0 in league play.

James Logan coach Matt Guzman won the 250th game of his career in a sweep over league rivals Moreau Catholic. Sophomore outside hitter McKenzie Anderson had eight kills and 11 digs, and seniors Hannah Issenman and Dylan Gutierrez combined for 13 kills and five blocks. Sophomore setter Leann Jung had 13 assists, six digs and two aces. Senior libero Julianna Bondoc had 12 digs and two assists as the Colts improved to 19-2.

Encinal 3, Bishop O’Dowd 2

Encinal slipped past Bishop O’Dowd in what O’Dowd coach Nova Bramed called “a competitive match.” She said a trio of seniors were very impressive. Middle blocker Every Borjon, outside hitter Lisa Gerardi and libero Thea Hill all stood out for the Dragons. Encinal (13-8) rallied from a 2-1 deficit, beating O’Dowd (3-11) 25-14, 21-25, 22-25, 25-22, 15-9.  

Miramonte 3, Las Lomas 1

Miramonte improved to 7-3 with a home victory over Diablo Athletic League rival Las Lomas 25-15, 23-25, 25-14, 25-21. Freshman Sena Hans had a fantastic game, as did senior opposite hitter Hannah Rush and senior libero Nicole Tuszynski. After losing their first two games, the Matadors have now won seven of eight. 

Aragon 3, Carlmont 1

Aragon lost the first set but then cruised in the final three to win 23-25, 25-14, 25-17, 25-21. Aragon improved to 10-9 on the season, while Carlmont dropped to 11-8. Junior outside hitter Sophie Rubinstein had 31 kills and 16 digs for Aragon. Senior libero Kathleen Suayan had 21 digs and five assists, and senior Hunter Kwan had 49 assists and 10 digs as a setter. 

Deer Valley improved to 12-10 after defeating Bay Valley Athletic League opponent Pittsburg 3-2 (19-25, 26-24, 20-25, 25-20, 15-11) at home. Deer Valley coach Steve Evans said junior Elon Smith, senior Megan Lee, junior Fetty Te’O and senior Mischa Reyes were among the team’s top performers on Tuesday night.

The Yellowjackets improved to 13-4 after an easy home victory over San Leandro in West Alameda County Conference play. Senior middle blocker Preslie Yates had two blocks, three aces and seven kills, and senior outside hitter Elle Walker had eight kills, five digs and two aces. Junior libero Semaj Manuel had 13 digs and two aces, and senior outside hitter Marlo Blalk had three aces, five kills and four digs.
