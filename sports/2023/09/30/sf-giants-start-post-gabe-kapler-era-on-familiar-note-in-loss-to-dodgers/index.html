SF Giants start post-Gabe Kapler era on familiar note in loss to Dodgers

SAN FRANCISCO — The post-Gabe Kapler era started the same way his tenure ended: in defeat.

With interim skipper Kai Correa at the helm, the Giants’ bats were bleak as ever against Dodgers starter Lance Lynn, while Los Angeles’ powerful lineup lit up San Francisco rookie starter Keaton Winn for a trio of home runs (Will Smith, Freddie Freeman, J.D. Martinez).

Final score: 6-2.

1) No .500 finish

It was an inevitability that became a reality Friday night: the Giants will finish 2023 with a worse record than in 2022.

That’s the kind of stagnation that can get a manager fired, and guess what — it did.

The Giants needed to sweep the Dodgers this weekend to avoid securing a losing record, but the homer barrage against San Francisco’s rookie starter meant that dream died in the first game of the series. They have finished better than .500 only once — in their 107-win 2021 — in five seasons under Farhan Zaidi.

Good news: They can’t finish any worse than Zaidi’s first season (77-85 in 2019). Not so good: They need to win these last two to avoid finishing further below .500 than in any season since. Maybe worse: Even if they accomplish that, they need the Padres to lose their last two to avoid their first fourth-place finish since Zaidi was hired.

2) Thanks to Willie Mac

Before the game, the Giants honored Thairo Estrada with the prestigious Willie Mac award.

It’s voted on by their peers in the clubhouse and is given to the Giants player who embodies the “competitive spirit, ability and leadership” of Hall of Famer and franchise icon Willie McCovey. The ceremony acted as an interesting juxtaposition against the backdrop of managerial upheaval.

The four previous honorees still with the club — Brandon Crawford, Mike Yastrzemski, LaMonte Wade Jr. and Wilmer Flores — joined Estrada on the field.

It should come as no surprise that Estrada has taken after Flores, whom he has described as a big brother. On a day where clubhouse questions reigned supreme in the wake of their manager’s firing, Estrada and Flores put their heads down, went to work and led by example, as they did every other day this season.

Estrada went hitless in four at-bats, but the other Willie Mac winners played a role in both the Giants’ runs.

After Wade and Yastrzemski drew back-to-back walks to lead off the bottom of the first, Flores won a 10-pitch battle with a line drive single in center field that drove Wade home from second.

In the third, Flores drove a solo shot just over the left field wall, his 23rd of the year, five more than anyone else on the roster.

In a microcosm of the Giants’ season, Flores’ pair of hits were their only ones of the night. The rest of the lineup combined to go 0-for-25.

3) Making history

The Giants’ decision to hand the interim reins to Correa was routine; the bench coach is second-in-command in the dugout and often next man up in these scenarios.

But, in this case, it was significant when Correa met Dodgers manager Dave Roberts at home plate for the lineup exchange.

It was the first game in MLB history that featured two managers of Asian descent.

Correa hails from Hawaii and carries Japanese lineage. Roberts is the son of a Black father and Japanese mother.
