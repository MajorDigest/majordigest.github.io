Rejuvenated LeBron James enters 21st NBA season eager to chase title with Lakers

EL SEGUNDO — For Lakers star LeBron James, the decision to return for a 21st NBA season wasn’t one he made hastily.

After contemplating his own basketball mortality in a way that’s rarely seen publicly after the Lakers were swept in last season’s Western Conference finals, James needed time.

Time to not only refresh the 38-year-old body that has accumulated 65,747 combined NBA minutes in the regular season and playoffs – the second most in league history behind only Kareem Abdul-Jabbar (66,297) – but also his mind.

James used that time the way many around the NBA do during the offseason: putting the focus on his family.

It was while James was coaching his younger son’s (Bryce) AAU team alongside former Lakers teammate Rajon Rondo during the Nike EYBL Peach Jam in early July that James felt he was getting out of what he referred to as a “funk.”

“Just seeing the love and just seeing the – they just play for the love of the game,” James said during the Lakers’ preseason media day on Monday. “And being around all of those kids with our AAU program, they were just out there competing and just wanting to win. It had nothing to do with social media clicks or money or fame or none of that stuff. They were just playing to be able to be able to put themselves in a position to possibly win Peach Jam. Just seeing that kind of got me going a little bit.”

Not too long after that, James publicly announced he’d be back for at least one more season.

“I had a conversation with my family, [a] conversation with myself,” James said. “Getting inspired once again from my kids. And then I feel like I got a lot more in the tank to give.”

A tank that needed to be refilled after telling reporters in late May that he had “a lot to think about” when it came to his future with basketball.

“At that moment … I was exhausted. I was tired. Mentally, I was in too many different places,” James said. “And that’s what drew that comment. Because that’s just how I felt at the moment. But I’m happy to be returning for another season and helping this team, hopefully, get this team to the Finals.”

“I mean, I feel different. I’m not a 21-year-old, that’s for sure. Feels a little different getting out of bed every day. But as far as my energy level, I feel pretty good.”

To the Lakers’ brass, how James has prepared for the 2023-24 season is no different from what they’ve seen from him before.

“It’s staggering for a player who has 20 years under the hood already and is preparing for 21 like he’s a rookie,” Lakers general manager Rob Pelinka said last week. “He’s been doing 6 a.m. workouts. Probably been in our building as much as any player this offseason.

“Any team LeBron’s played for, it’s been pretty uniform that his work sets the tone. There has been nothing but an increase in seeing that here. That really puts a spirit in our entire group to see him preparing that detailed and working that hard.”

James said “a lot” of his offseason was also spent rehabbing the right foot which he tore a right tendon in, resulting in a 13-game absence from Feb. 28-March 24. He played the final eight regular-season games and all 16 of their playoff games, but he was clearly limited physically.

“My foot has been reacting very well on my offseason workouts,” James added. Looking forward to seeing how it reacts during training camp and all preseason going into the regular season. Excited to see where I am as it stands.”

He doesn’t know what his future will look like beyond where this Lakers’ season takes him.

James, a three-time Olympian and two-time gold medalist who hasn’t played for Team USA since 2012, expressed interest in playing for the U.S. in the 2024 Paris Olympic Games but didn’t make a commitment.

“We’ll see what happens,” James said. “As far as physical toll, I don’t know, we’ll see how I feel at the end of the season. But from the players that we have here that I can think of off of the top of my head that could fill that roster up, I don’t think it would be too much of a physical toll. But we’ll see, we’ll see what happens.”

As for whether there’s any part of James that could see this being the last season he plays, he remained unsure.

“Um, I don’t know. I don’t know,” James said. “I’m happy right now. I’m excited. I’m looking forward to [Tuesday] and getting training camp going and getting going. But I don’t know what the end of this road looks like, or at the end of the season.”
