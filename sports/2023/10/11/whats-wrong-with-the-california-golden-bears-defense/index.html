What’s wrong with the California Golden Bears’ defense?

BERKELEY — Cal defensive coordinator Peter Sirmon has a new title these days.

“I’m the suggestion coach right now,” he said.

He figures he has earned the moniker after the Bears experienced something they’ve never faced in his six seasons on coach Justin Wilcox’s staff.

Twice in the past three weeks, Cal has allowed more than 50 points in a game — 59 at Washington and 52 at home against Oregon State. Opponents reached the 40s a few times against the Bears but never 50.

Until now.

“When things are going good there’s not a lot of people who offer suggestions,” Sirmon said. “Right now, giving up some points the last three weeks . . . a lot of suggestions are coming across the desk. Sometimes they’re great suggestions.”

If only it were that easy.

The Bears (3-3, 1-2) are in just Week 2 of their five-game gauntlet against Pac-12 rivals ranked in the AP Top-25. Cal visits No. 16 Utah (4-1, 1-1) on Saturday afternoon at Rice-Eccles Stadium, where the Utes have won 17 in a row and 28 of 29, all before capacity crowds.

The Bears lost 35-0 to Utah in 2019, the last time they ventured to Salt Lake City, and haven’t won there since the week before JFK’s assassination in 1963.

Maybe the Bears’ visit this week is well-timed because Utah has played all season so far without star quarterback Cam Rising, who is still recovering from a complicated knee injury he sustained in the Rose Bowl. He may be ready to play — no one is saying — but with his understudies running the offense the Utes have scored just one offensive touchdown in each of their two conference games.

The Bears have enough to worry about fixing their own problems on defense.

One of the Pac-12’s elite defensive teams just a couple of years ago, Cal is 10th in scoring defense (30.7) in the conference and eighth in total defense (381.5 yards).

Sirmon concedes to having emotions that range from frustration to anger about how things have unfolded recently. Mostly, he said it provides an opportunity to evaluate every aspect of the team’s preparation, including scheme and personnel.

“What we’re doing right now leaves a lot to be desired,” he said. “We’re scoring some points (on offense) and for us to not be able to stop people is disappointing, to say the least.”

Wilcox agreed, saying,  “It is frustrating. We thought we would perform better. Ultimately, the tape is the resume and that’s where we are right now. So we’ve got to do something about it.”

Wilcox said the Bears have to create more pressure on the quarterback, defend the run better and win one-on-one battles against receivers in the secondary. In other words, almost everything needs a tuneup.

The numbers say Cal is not making the kind of splashy defensive plays that can change games.

They have zero sacks in three Pac-12 games and the numbers in the losses to UW and OSU are stark: No sacks, one fumble recovery, one interception, one pass breakup, two quarterback hurries.

Partly as a result, the Huskies and Beavers tore up Cal with 600 passing yards and 10 touchdowns. Quarterbacks Michael Penix Jr. and DJ Uiagalelei combined to complete 76 percent of their attempts against the Bears, each going 19 for 25.

In their other four games the Bears racked up solid numbers: seven sacks, six fumble recoveries, six interceptions, 17 pass breakups and 11 quarterback hurries.

Things won’t be easier if Jackson Sirmon — Peter’s son and the senior All-Pac-12 inside linebacker — is unavailable after leaving the Oregon State game in the second half with an undisclosed injury. Wilcox has labeled him day-to-day.

In any case, lineup changes are possible, Wilcox said. “It’s been a competitive week,” Sirmon said. “We’ll explore every option.”

But wholesale changes, in either personnel or scheme, aren’t the answer, he believes.

“I think we have to continue to press forward,” Sirmon said. “We need to believe in what we’re doing and we need the young men to believe in the techniques they are capable of performing.”

JAYDN OTT UPDATE: Wilcox said running back Jaydn Ott, who left the OSU game in the third quarter due to injury, is probable to return at Utah. Ott leads the Pac-12 and is seventh nationally, averaging 111.6 yards rushing per game.
