Bay Area high school football roundup 2023: Best of Week 8 action

Welcome back to the Bay Area News Group’s high school football roundup.

Here, you’ll find all the details from the weekend’s action in this news organization’s coverage area, which encompasses teams that play in leagues based primarily in Alameda, Contra Costa, San Mateo and Santa Clara counties.

Check back every Saturday during the season for scores, highlights and top performers, updated throughout the day.

If you have not already, please subscribe here for complete digital access all season long. Your contributions keep us going.

On to the roundup … 

Ranked teams

No. 2 De La Salle 33, No. 3 San Ramon Valley 27, OT

The showdown between the best teams in the North Coast Section lived up to the pregame hype, and then exceeded it, as the schools went into overtime after San Ramon Valley’s rally from down 15 points in the final four minutes of regulation. SRV quarterback Luke Baker rushed for 181 yards and threw for 204, and Derrick Blanche scored the winning touchdown in an unforgettable game that kept De La Salle’s 32-year unbeaten streak against NCS opponents alive. Darren Sabedra was in Danville and has the full story here.

No. 4 Pittsburg 54, Freedom 0

Pittsburg built a 48-point lead at halftime en route to routing host Freedom in Bay Valley Athletic League play. Marley Alcantara completed 9 of 14 passes for 114 yards and four touchdowns to lead the way as the Pirates improved to 7-0 overall and 2-0 in the league. Jamar Searcy, Jadyn Hudson, Bobby Shaw and Anthony Bookout were on the receiving end of Alcantara’s TD passes. Searcy finished with two TDs and the Pirates also got rushing touchdowns from Rolph Mosley, Jr. and Elijah Bow. Freedom fell to 1-6, 0-2. Next Friday, Pittsburg will be at home to play Heritage and Freedom will travel to Antioch. – Darren Sabedra

No. 6 El Cerrito 47, Pinole Valley 0

El Cerrito ran its winning streak in Tri-County Athletic League Rock Division games to 17 after a comfortable victory over Pinole Valley. Guardian Harper, a 185-pound EC defensive back, rushed the passer like a player twice his size, ending the night with four sacks and one forced fumble. Gauchos quarterback Michael Vanhook completed all five of his passes for 168 yards and three TDs. WR Jayvon Bolds caught two passes for 51 yards and two TDs, and Washington State commit Kamani Jackson reeled in a 30-yard TD. Running back Micah Avery rushed for three touchdowns, and Ziare Eastman ran for one. El Cerrito’s showdown against Salesian at the end of the season could decide the league championship. The Gauchos (7-0, 2-0) play at Hercules on Friday, and Pinole Valley (2-5, 0-2) travels to De Anza on Saturday. — Joseph Dycus

No. 7 California 21, No. 16 Monte Vista 14

California played well defensively to slip past Monte Vista in a thrilling EBAL Mountain Division game. Running back Julian McMahan put Monte Vista on top at the end of one quarter with a six-yard TD run. California tied the score in the second quarter when Derek Trottier recovered a backward Monte Vista pass and ran it in for a touchdown. Luke Taylor gave Cal High a 14-7 lead on a wide receiver reverse in the third quarter, and Saydi Abdul-Kareem scored on a 35-yard TD run to put the Grizzlies up 21-7. McMahan, who rushed for 156 yards on 29 carries, cut Cal High’s lead to seven points in the fourth quarter, but the Mustangs could not finish the rally. California (5-2, 1-0) plays host to San Ramon Valley on Friday, and Monte Vista (4-3, 1-1) travels to Clayton Valley Charter. — Joseph Dycus

No. 8 McClymonds 56, Castlemont 0

McClymonds opened its Oakland Athletic League schedule with a rout in a rematch of last year’s Silver Bowl. Defensive end Troy Hill returned a fumble 51 yards for a TD, picked off a pass 62 yards for another touchdown and had three sacks. Tajir Golden ran 12 times for 146 yards and three touchdowns and caught two passes for 92 yards and one TD. John “Sharky” Tamale had nine carries for 88 yards and one TD, and Redmani Albert ran for 58 yards and one TD. Castlemont (2-4, 0-1) will play Oakland on Friday, and McClymonds (3-2, 1-0) will play Oakland on Oct. 27 after a bye week. – Joseph Dycus

No. 9 Los Gatos 55, Mountain View 2

Six different players scored first-half touchdowns as the Wildcats improved to 5-2 overall and 2-0 in the Peninsula Athletic League Bay Division. Quarterback A.J. Minyard threw first-half TD passes to Boxer Kopcsak-Yeung and Jaylen Thomas, Jay Mallugari blocked a punt that Max Thomas returned 8 yards for a score, and Shane Timmons, Scott Garwood and Kayden Bakhtiari added rushing touchdowns as Los Gatos built a 41-0 lead at halftime. Zane Frey and Andrew Sandoval rushed for TDs in the second half to make it eight players in all to reach the end zone for Los Gatos. The Wildcats appear headed for a showdown with Wilcox on Oct. 27 to decide the Bay title. Mountain View lost its third straight after a 3-1 start. All three defeats have been to Bay opponents, putting the Spartans in danger of missing the Central Coast Section playoffs. Five Bay Division teams will receive automatic berths, but one will have to hope for one of five at-large berths handed out by the CCS seeding committee. Los Gatos led 48-0 before Mountain View got on the scoreboard with a fourth-quarter safety. – Mike Lefkow  

No. 14 St. Ignatius 15, No. 10 Valley Christian 14

St. Ignatius’ second consecutive trip from San Francisco to Santa Clara County seemed destined for the same fate as last week’s visit to St. Francis when Valley Christian scored two touchdowns to take a 14-0 lead with 11:10 left. But SI staged a late rally to grab the lead and survived the West Catholic Athletic League game when Valley Christian missed a 48-yard field-goal try as time expired. Soren Hummel’s pass to Pierce St. Geme on a two-point play accounted for the decisive points. Jarious Hogan scored the touchdown that made it 14-13 from 4 yards out. Hummel’s 21-yard TD pass to Hogan cut the margin to 14-7. Uchenna Eke’s 38-yard TD run in the third quarter gave Valley Chrisitan the lead. Joseph Stephens’ 23-yard run early in the final quarter made it 14-0. Valley also got an interception from Joseph Patterson. Next Friday, Valley (5-2, 2-2) will visit St. Francis and SI (4-3, 2-2) will face Sacred Heart Cathedral in the Bruce-Mahoney game at Kezar Stadium. – Darren Sabedra

No. 11 Wilcox 41, Burlingame 7

Wilcox dominated on the ground with eight players combining for 384 yards rushing. Elijah Walker paced the Chargers with 141 yards and three touchdowns on nine carries. Jamontay Amataga added 64 yards rushing and two touchdowns. Brayden Rosa picked up 51 yards on five carries and one TD. Wilcox (5-2, 3-0 PAL Bay) plays next at Sacred Heart Prep on Saturday afternoon. Burlingame (3-4, 1-2) hosts Los Gatos on Thursday. — Glenn Reeves

No. 12 St. Francis 44, Sacred Heart Cathedral 22

Kingston Keanaaina scored three touchdowns on runs of 41, 35 and 22 yards to lead St. Francis. Aaron Knapp threw two touchdown passes to Grant Righellis to give the Lancers a 14-0 lead. SHC came back to draw even at 14-14 on a 3-yard run by Jayden Crane and an 18-yard pass from Michael Sargent to Obryan Flanigan. St. Francis then scored the next 23 points in the game on two Keanaaina runs, one by Daniel Swank and a 29-yard field goal from Christopher Han. Sargent’s 26-yard TD pass to Griffin Merrit closed out the scoring for the Irish. Sione Tuiileila and Jackson Cahoon had interceptions and Asher Kolodziej “Did a great job of pushing the pocket and putting pressure on their quarterback,” St. Francis coach Greg Calcagno said. St. Francis (4-3, 3-1 WCAL) hosts Valley Christian and SHC (0-7, 0-4) plays St. Ignatius on Friday. — Glenn Reeves

No. 13 Campolindo 35, Northgate 14

Northgate gave Campolindo an early fight as the Broncos, in search of their first victory of the season, led 14-7 late in the second quarter. But Campo evened the score with 32 seconds to go before the break and outscored Northgate 21-0 in the second half to stay undefeated in the Diablo Athletic League Foothill Division. Timothy Daugherty, whose first touchdown gave Campo a 7-0 lead, put his team in front for good when he crossed the goal line from the 1 to make it 21-14. Sebastien Antonios-McCrea’s 1-yard run widened the margin to 28-14 and Daugherty’s fourth TD of the game pushed the advantage to 21. Josh Tsai and Luke Bonardi intercepted passes for Campo. The Cougars also got 78 yards in 12 carries from Mack Fisher and four catches for 62 yards from Joe Revelos. Campo improved to 5-1-1, 3-0. Northgate is 0-7, 0-4. Next Friday, Campo visits Las Lomas and Northgate travels to College Park. – Darren Sabedra

No. 19 Menlo 19, No. 18 Hillsdale 13

Hillsdale and Menlo were both 6-0 and tied for first place in the PAL De Anza Division when they met Friday afternoon at Menlo. Coming off a shutout win over Palo Alto, Menlo had a 19-0 lead at halftime. But the Fighting Knights of Hillsdale made a game out of it in the second half before falling short as Menlo ended up celebrating a narrow victory. Mikey McGrath passed 166 yards and Jack Freehill threw for 105 to lead Menlo’s shared QB position. Glenn Reeves has the complete recap here.

No. 20 Liberty 41, No. 24 Antioch 40

Sophomore RB Jaxon Bell scored his third touchdown of the game with less than a minute left and William Geer booted the extra point to break the 40-40 tie in a wild one at Liberty. The Lions (5-2, 2-0 Bay Valley Athletic League) rallied twice in the second half, scoring two touchdowns to go in front 34-24 after trailing by three points at intermission. Antioch (5-2, 1-1) stormed back to lead 40-34 before Bell crashed over from the 1 and Geer added the PAT, ending the Panthers’ four-game winning streak. Bell rushed for 139 yards, giving him 1,126 yards for the season. He has scored 20 touchdowns. Quarterback Sage Robertson passed for 188 yards and three TDs, with all three six-pointers going to Ryan McKendry, who had eight catches for 163 yards. Antioch QB Larenzo Mayfield may have been the most spectacular player on the field. He passed for 156 yards and three touchdowns, and rushed for 175 yards. Javion Smith had four receptions for 152 yards and a pair of scores for Antioch. The teams combined for about 900 yards in total offense. – Mike Lefkow  

No. 22 Christopher 45, Santa Teresa 13

Leading 21-13 at halftime, Christopher heated up in the second half on its home field in Gilroy on Thursday and went on to defeat Santa Teresa to improve to 7-0 overall and 2-0 in the Blossom Valley Athletic League’s Mt. Hamilton Division. Jaxen Robinson completed 11 of 18 passes for 164 yards and three touchdowns. The Cougars also got 96 yards and two TDs in 11 carries from William Rizqallah. Ray Waller rushed for one TD. Amari Bluford caught two touchdown passes and Evan Vernon grabbed one as Christopher remained tied for first with Live Oak, a team it hosts on Nov. 3 to close the regular season. Santa Teresa had won its previous three games. The Saints fell to 3-4, 1-1. Christopher visits Lincoln-San Jose next Thursday. Santa Teresa travels to Live Oak on Friday. The Saints will probably have to win two of their final three games to make the Central Coast Section playoffs. The top four finishers in the Mt. Hamilton automatically qualify. – Darren Sabedra

No. 23 Las Lomas 41, College Park 0

The Knights (7-0, 3-0 DAL Foothill), off to their fastest start since beginning the 2019 season with eight straight wins, led 21-0 at the half. Sophomore QB Dylan Thomas threw four touchdown passes, increasing his total to 14 thus far this season. Kaden Lagaya had two TD receptions and added a rushing touchdown. Landon Gilmore and Daniel Downes caught the other two TD tosses from Thomas. College Park suffered its fifth straight defeat after beginning the season 2-0. – Mike Lefkow 

No. 25 Sacred Heart Prep 27, Menlo-Atherton 21, OT

Ravu Savali scored on a 5-yard run in overtime to give SHP the win in a matchup between Atherton rivals. M-A had sent the game to overtime on a 5-yard touchdown pass from Thomas Scott to Owen Van Loben Sels in the final minute of regulation. Savali also scored Sacred Heart Prep’s first touchdown. QB Jack Scully threw two TD passes, to Jack Gartner and to Kyle Chai. M-A’s Kareem Franklin scored on a 91-yard fumble return and Scott, inserted into the game after Xander Eschelman suffered a hip injury early in the first quarter, threw a touchdown pass to Alek Marshall. “Both sides played with tons of heart,” M-A coach Chris Saunders said. “A very intense game.” Sacred Heart Prep (4-3, 2-1 PAL Bay) hosts Wilcox on Saturday. Menlo-Atherton (2-5, 0-2) is at Mountain View on Thursday. – Glenn Reeves

East Bay

Alhambra 49, Mt. Diablo 47

Alhambra quarterback Beau Blau accounted for six touchdowns, Mt. Diablo running back Herschel Turner rushed for 299 yards, and a halftime adjustment changed the game for Alhambra in a wild showdown for the top spot in the Diablo Athletic League Valley Division. Joseph Dycus has the story from Martinez here.

Berkeley 35, Washington-Fremont 10

The Yellowjackets outscored host Washington-Fremont  21-0 after the first quarter, setting up what could be a showdown against Hayward next Saturday for the MVAL/WACC Mission Division crown. Both teams will enter the contest in Berkeley at 2-0 in league play. Berkeley is 4-3 overall, Hayward is 5-2. Deontae Faison passed for three touchdowns and ran for one as the Yellowjackets won their third in a row. Washington is 3-4, 1-1. The score after one quarter was 14-10. Berkeley scored on a pair of pass plays from Faison to converted WR Jalen Harris, a two-star recruit with offers from Cal and San Jose State. The Huskies got a touchdown from QB Esean Johnson and a field goal from Arnav Pophale. From the second period on it was all Jackets. Faison scored on an 8-yard run to make it 20-10 at the half, then he threw a 62-yard TD pass to Kamren Bell to increase the lead to 28-10 after three quarters. A 51-yard pick-6 by Damonte’ Smith concluded the scoring – Mike Lefkow

Bishop O’Dowd 57, Moreau Catholic 20

Bishop O’Dowd left no doubt about this one as the Dragons, playing on the road in Hayward, roared to a 41-14 halftime advantage and never looked back in an MVAL/WACC Foothill Division game. Devin Wilson, a 6-1, 200-pound junior, threw for 352 yards on 19-of-30 passing and six touchdowns. Miles Dixon was Wilson’s top target. The senior caught nine passes for 170 yards and five TDs. He also forced a fumble on defense. Deji Ajose added seven receptions for 123 yards and a TD. Evan Garrison led the O’Dowd defense with nine tackles, two sacks, three tackles for loss and a fumble recovery. Kyle Turner also was productive, finishing with five tackles, two tackles for loss, a forced fumble and a fumble recovery. Jalen Arnold, Jayden Bryant and Andrew Placido scored TDs for Moreau. On Friday, O’Dowd (4-3, 2-0) visits Castro Valley and Moreau (3-4, 1-1) travels to San Leandro. – Darren Sabedra

Clayton Valley 41, Foothill 29

The Ugly Eagles, playing with star receiver Daven Amos healthy for the first time this season, led 28-0 early in the second quarter. Amos, who has been slowed by a hip injury, caught his first two touchdown passes of the season, including a 19-yard reception from QB Tyson Lovett that put the Uglies up 28-zip one minute into the second period. Amos also caught a 10-yard touchdown from Lovett in the third quarter that made it 34-7. Justin Underwood had two rushing touchdowns for Clayton Valley (3-4, 1-1 EBAL Mountain) and Dion Lew-Beverly got the scoring started with a 60-yard kickoff return. Hayezon Jackson had a long touchdown run for the Eagles in the first quarter. The Eagles play their next two games against EBAL rivals Monte Vista and California, and those contests could determine whether they receive a berth in the North Coast Section playoffs. Foothill (1-6, 0-2) didn’t go quietly despite the large early deficit. The Falcons trailed by only two scores, 34-22, when QB Erik Olsen tossed his second of three TD passes with four minutes left in the third quarter. But Underwood’s 2-yard run early in the fourth period allowed the Eagles to go back on top by three scores at 41-22. In addition to three touchdown passes, Olsen scored on a quarterback sneak for Foothill. Brayden Brown, Graham Zander and Nathan Bodensteiner were on the receiving end of Olsen’s TD tosses. – Mike Lefkow

Hayward 38, Encinal 0

Mateaki Paongo ran for 80 yards and a touchdown to lead a diverse Hayward attack that overwhelmed Encinal in an MVAL/WACC Mission Division game. Maurice Hall ran for 79 yards and a TD on two carries, and sophomore Jordyn Wilson ran for 19 yards and a TD. Senior quarterback Kennedy Braziel ran for 35 yards and a touchdown and also completed 8 of 12 passes for 173 yards and two touchdowns. Eusebio Goncalves and Dillion Lobo each caught a TD, and Emmanuel Sega led the team in receiving yards with 63 on three catches. The Famers held Encinal to 91 yards rushing, according to Hayward coach Virgil Hart. Encinal (1-6, 0-2) plays host to Irvington on Friday, and Hayward travels to Berkeley on Saturday in a matchup of the division’s two best teams. — Joseph Dycus

James Logan 42, Castro Valley 7

Quarterback Jonathan Craft, who transferred to Logan from Valley Christian before this season, threw four touchdown passes and ran for a fifth in the Colts’ victory. Logan has won three in a row after starting the season 0-4. They are 2-0 in the MVL/WACC Foothill Division and tied for first with Bishop O’Dowd. The teams play Oct. 27 at Logan, and that game will have large playoff implications for Logan. If the Colts beat O’Dowd and capture the Foothill, it would receive an automatic berth in the NCS Division I playoffs. Otherwise, the Colts likely will be on the outside looking in when the postseason begins. Kemani Blackmon caught two TD passes and Jeremy Craft, Jonathan Craft’s older brother, grabbed one. Castro Valley fell to 2-5, 0-2 and will likely need some help to make the NCS playoffs in Division II. – Mike Lefkow

Kennedy-Richmond 30, St. Patrick-St. Vincent 29

Senior Deondre Prim was a one-man show in Kennedy’s stunning upset against host St. Patrick-St. Vincent. Prim made himself right at home on the Bruins’ turf, scoring all four Eagles touchdowns and two of their three 2-point conversions in the come-from-behind win. Kennedy (6-2, 1-1 TCAL Stone) trailed 29-16 going into the fourth quarter. Prim, playing quarterback, scored both touchdowns in the period on keepers, then added the 2-point conversion that put the Eagles up by the final margin with 7:07 left in the game. In all, Prim rushed for two TDs, passed for another six points and recovered a fumble for a touchdown. He also had nine tackles, two interceptions and forced a fumble. “I’ve been here for nine seasons,” Kennedy coach Greg Marshall said. “That was probably the best performance I’ve witnessed.” Probably? The victory should put Kennedy in pretty good shape to make the North Coast Section playoffs in Division VI. The Eagles still have games left with Vallejo and Richmond, but should do no worse than 1-1 in those. SPSV (5-2, 1-1) also is in good shape for the playoffs in Division VII. But the loss to Kennedy likely hurt its chance to win the TCAL Stone. The Bruins finish the regular season at St. Mary’s-Berkeley on Nov. 4, and the Panthers are the highest-rated team in the division, according to the calpreps.com computer. – Mike Lefkow 

Salesian 55, De Anza 13

The Pride led 27-0 at the end of one quarter and De Anza was never able to get closer than three scores after that. Marco Turqueza led Salesian, running for three touchdowns and passing for another score. Salesian improved to 6-1 in all games and 2-0 in the TCAL Rock Division. The Pride has three remaining games, all against Rock opponents, with El Cerrito standing in the way of a nine-win regular season. The Pride and Gauchos play on Nov. 4 at Salesian. De Anza fell to 1-6, 0-2. – Mike Lefkow

South Bay/Peninsula

Aragon 36, Milpitas 20

Ivan Nisa rushed for close to 200 yards and scored two touchdowns to lead Aragon (6-1, 3-0 PAL Ocean). Sean Hickey ran for a touchdown and passed to Josh Jacobs for another. Pau’uvale Hokafonu also scored on the ground. Milpitas had a 20-15 lead at halftime. “They’re a good team,” Aragon coach Steve Sell said. “I don’t want to play them again.” Aragon, which had 255 yards rushing and 118 passing,  plays at Terra Nova on Thursday. Milpitas (4-3, 1-2), which had 169 yards rushing and 70 passing,  visits San Mateo on Friday.  – Glenn Reeves

Branham 43, Piedmont Hills 7

Branham, playing at home on Thursday, built a 21-7 halftime advantage on its way to a blowout victory in BVAL Santa Teresa-Foothill Division play. The Bruins (5-2, 2-0) fell behind 7-0 before Jimmy Scibetta’s 10-yard TD catch from Jack Lewis evened the score in the opening quarter. After going ahead 14-7, Branham widened the cushion to two touchdowns when Lewis connected with Elias Antillion from 54 yards out. Lewis threw for five touchdowns. Preston Conrad caught two of them, covering 80 and 45 yards.  Antillion also had two TD receptions. Piedmont Hills fell to 2-4, 0-2. Next Friday, Branham will play host to Independence and Piedmont Hills will be at home to face Pioneer. – Darren Sabedra

Carlmont 48, El Camino 3

After starting 0-5, Carlmont has begun league play with two consecutive blowout victories. Braeden Kumer rushed for four touchdowns as Carlmont routed El Camino in a PAL El Camino Division game. Carlmont defeated Santa Clara 56-28 last week. Christian Booth started the scoring with a 34-yard touchdown, and Juan Olvera scored a rushing TD with less than a minute to go in the first half. Nate Long also rushed for a Carlmont TD. On Friday, Carlmont (2-5, 2-1) plays at Woodside and El Camino (0-7, 0-2) plays host to Los Altos. — Joseph Dycus

Capuchino 42, San Mateo 35, OT

Capuchino, playing at home in San Bruno, stopped San Mateo near the goal line in overtime to improve its record to 7-0 overall and 3-0 in the PAL Ocean Division. Cap had taken the lead on the first possession of OT when Brandon Arceo threw a screen pass to Issa Keishk from 10 yards out for a touchdown. The finish spoiled San Mateo’s rally after the Bearcats (3-4, 1-2) charged back from a 35-14 deficit. Gabe Buenrostro led San Mateo with 160 yards and three touchdowns in 10 carries. Next Friday, San Mateo will be at home to play Milpitas and Capuchino will play host to Sequoia. Darren Sabedra has more details here.

Cupertino 38, Jefferson 12

Shen Hernandez-Ong ran for two touchdowns and passed for two touchdowns – to James Xue and Malachi Perez – as Cupertino remained undefeated in PAL Lake Division play with a lopsided win at home. The Pioneers also got a 28-yard interception return for a touchdown from Jaidyn Sinclair. Victor Silva had two sacks, a strip sack and three tackles for loss, and Anthony Silva kicked a 28-yard field goal to complete the scoring. Cupertino (5-2, 4-0) has a first-place showdown at home on Friday against South San Francisco. Jefferson (1-6, 1-3) visits Saratoga on Friday. – Darren Sabedra

Del Mar 35, Yerba Buena 0

Del Mar’s defensive front controlled the line of scrimmage in a BVAL West Valley Division game. Angel Nolasco and Julian Reyes combined for three tackles-for-loss, a sack and a fumble recovery. DT Ayden Ayala had two TFL and an interception and DT Alex Estrada had four tackles in the backfield plus a sack. Adrian Hernandez returned a kick for a touchdown. Quarterback Andre Latimore had 230 total yards to go along with three rushing touchdowns and a 20-yard TD pass to top target Jacob Schwoob. On Friday, Del Mar (4-3, 4-0) plays host to Hill and Yerba Buena (1-6, 0-4) will be at home to face Evergreen Valley. — Joseph Dycus 

Leigh 17, Willow Glen 7

Willow Glen struck first in this BVAL Santa Teresa-Valley Division game as Cedeno Chavez threw a 26-yard TD pass to Spencer Bartlinsk to give the visitors a 7-0 advantage in the opening quarter. Cam Tchir’s 28-yard field goal later in the period made it 7-3. Tyler Donaldson’s 22-yard touchdown pass to Carter Williams late in the half gave Leigh a 10-7 lead at the break. Donaldson’s 38-yard scoring pass to Shayan Shariat stretched the advantage to 17-7 in the third quarter as Leigh improved to 5-2 overall and 2-0 in the league. Willow Glen is 4-2, 1-1. Donaldson finished 14 of 24 passing for 189 yards. He also ran for 87 yards in 17 carries. Shariat ran 13 times for 65 yards and caught two passes for 63 yards. Carter Williams had three receptions for 51 yards. Next Friday, Leigh will be back at home to face Overfelt and Willow Glen will play host to Sobrato. – Darren Sabedra

Live Oak 44, Leland 13

The Acorns continued on their path toward a perfect regular season with an easy road win. Live Oak (6-0, 2-0 BVAL Mt. Hamilton) fell behind 7-0 two minutes into the contest after fumbling away its first possession, then scored the next 37 points. The Acorns appear headed toward a Mt. Hamilton Division showdown versus Christopher on Nov. 2 at Live Oak in what will be the regular-season finale for both schools. Regardless of the outcome, the two teams are shoo-ins for the Central Coast Section playoffs. On Thursday, Live Oak scored six touchdowns. Quarterback Luke Newell threw three TD passes, Josh Gagni ran for 154 yards and two scores, and Luke Richey caught four passes for 70 yards and a pair of touchdowns. Christian Hauge had four receptions for 92 yards and a TD, and junior RB Dominick McKinney raced 96 yards for the game’s final score. Gagni is up to 1,058 rushing yards for the season and has scored 14 touchdowns. Timmy Blocker was credited with a third-quarter safety that made it 30-7 Live Oak. The Acorns amassed 482 yards of total offense and the defense had five sacks. Luke Whitson had both TDs for Leland (1-6, 0-2), which has lost six in a row since a season-opening win. The junior came into the contest with only two carries for minus-2 yards in the Chargers’ previous six games. – Mike Lefkow

Overfelt 46, Westmont 21

The Royals received big offensive production from several players: Javier Mendoza (15 carries, 196 yards, two touchdowns), Duron Hall (12 carries, 123 yards, one TD), sophomore Lorenzo Garcia (8 carries, 87 yards, one TD) and QB Gabriel Martinez, who completed two of three passes for 127 yards and a touchdown. Taylor Gordon was on the receiving end of both of Martinez’s completions. Walter Smith made eight tackles and an interception playing safety. Offensive line won this game and controlled the line of scrimmage with physicality,” Overfelt coach Carlo Maningo said. Overfelt (4-3, 1-1 BVAL Santa Teresa-Valley) plays at Leigh on Friday. Westmont (4-3, 1-1) hosts Mt. Pleasant on Thursday. – Glenn Reeves

Prospect 35, San Jose 0

All five Prospect touchdowns came on the ground, three by Tommy Lewit and two by Ahmad Almaini. Lineman Zach Allard, LB Riley Roberts and CB Jordan Sharpe were standouts defensively, contributing to the shutout. James Perez had an interception. “We made good halftime adjustments to take advantage of what they were giving us defensively,” Prospect coach Rick Esparza said. Prospect (6-1, 4-0 BVAL West Valley Division) plays at James Lick while San Jose (2-5, 2-2) hosts Gunderson on Friday. — Glenn Reeves

Silver Creek 28, Pioneer 0

Trajan Sinatra completed 12 of 17 passes for 165 yards for Silver Creek, which scored seven points in each quarter. Dominic Roman caught four of Sinatra’s passes for 75 yards and a touchdown. Alonzo Hernandez was the leader on the ground with 65 yards on 12 carries and two touchdowns. Gabriel Lim also had a rushing touchdown. Dominic Lim and Mathias McClain had sacks, James Arriaga picked off two passes and Joshua Rodriguez had one. Silver Creek (5-2, 2-0 BVAL Santa Teresa-Foothill) hosts Gilroy on Thursday. Pioneer (2-5, 0-2) plays at Piedmont Hills on Friday. – Glenn Reeves

Sobrato 48, Mt. Pleasant 6

Another huge game by Noah Taylor, the leading rusher in the CCS, helped lead the Bulldogs to a victory in BVAL Santa Teresa-Valley play. Taylor rushed for 268 yards and four TDs on 12 carries. He had TD runs of 42, 48, 10 and 59 yards. QB Braden Hughes threw a 73-yard touchdown to Parker Hughes and a 60-yard TD to Ryan Hankins. On Friday, Sobrato (4-3, 1-1) travels to Willow Glen. Mt. Pleasant plays at Westmont on Thursday. — Joseph Dycus

Woodside 35, Fremont-Sunnyvale 15

Woodside moved into a three-way tie with Fremont and Carlmont atop the PAL El Camino standings with a big win at home on Thursday. Evan Usher led Woodside with 140 yards and two touchdowns in 21 carries. Oliver Lyssand added 54 yards and a TD in six carries and caught a 51-yard pass. Mason Furtado ran for 56 yards and a TD in four attempts and caught a 14-yard TD pass. Furtado also played a big role defensively. Daniel Torre and Cal Adasiewicz each intercepted a pass for Woodside. The Wildcats scored the first two touchdowns before Alexander Solarez’s 6-yard run cut the margin to 14-7 heading into halftime. Henry Buenrostro’s 54-yard TD pass to Solarez brought Fremont to within 21-15 with five minutes left in the third quarter. But Woodside added two more touchdowns to put the game away. The Wildcats improved to 5-2 overall and 2-1 in the league. Fremont fell to 5-2, 2-1. Next Friday, Woodside will play host to Carlmont and Fremont will be at home to face Santa Clara. – Darren Sabedra
