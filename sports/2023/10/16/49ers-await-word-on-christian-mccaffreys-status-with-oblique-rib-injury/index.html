49ers await MRI results on McCaffrey’s injury, give updates on Samuel, Williams

The 49ers were awaiting word on medical testing Monday to determine the status of running back Christian McCaffrey, who left the game after an injury to his rib cage area in a 19-17 loss to the Cleveland Browns.

“He’s in MRIs right now so we’re still waiting on the imaging,” coach Kyle Shanahan said in an afternoon conference call with local writers.

The good news is that left tackle Trent Williams (ankle) and wide receiver Deebo Samuel (shoulder) received “day-to-day” designations, meaning they’ll probably miss some practice time but have a chance of facing the Minnesota Vikings.

The 49ers (5-1) have an additional day to rest up, with a road assignment against the Vikings (2-4) awaiting on Monday Night Football.

Once the 49ers get a diagnosis on McCaffrey, they can determine whether he can be fitted with protective gear and be given pain medication in order to play or if he will be better served by missing one or more games.

Quarterback Brock Purdy played quarterback with a broken rib last season against Seattle, but McCaffrey is put in the direct line of fire far more often.

“Obviously it’s a tougher position because you get hit a lot more,” Shanahan said. “We’ve just got to see how it reacts and exactly what’s wrong with it too because we haven’t got that back yet. We say oblique/rib, hopefully it’s not both.  We’ve got to wait and see for sure.”

If not, the 49ers will move forward with a combination of Jordan (J.P.) Mason and Elijah Mitchell. Mason has been the No. 2 back of late with Mitchell missing two games with a knee injury during practice and returning last week.

Mason had five rushes for 27 yards including an 8-yard touchdown run against Cleveland.

“We’ve got two really good players there and I know Ty Davis-Price is waiting for his opportunity also,” Shanahan said. “Hopefully, Christian will be good to go this week and if not we’ve got a group of backs we can rely on.”

McCaffrey, who didn’t miss a game last season with either Carolina or the 49ers and was a workhorse for the first five games of 2023, rushed 11 times for 43 yards Sunday and caught three passes of nine net yards, including a 13-yard touchdown on a shovel pass from Purdy to open the game.

It was the 15th consecutive game that McCaffrey scored a touchdown. In the 49ers’ last two games, Dallas and Cleveland both keyed on McCaffrey and were successful in slowing him down as he gained 94 yards on 30 carries.

Television cameras caught McCaffrey attempting to stretch his torso while sitting on a bench in the third quarter. He returned to the game and had a 6-yard run which was wiped out by a penalty. He left the game and was headed to the locker room by the end of the quarter.

The 49ers aren’t sure when McCaffrey was injured.

“He mentioned about four possible plays he could have done it on,” Shanahan said.

McCaffrey leads the 49ers with 553 yards on 100 carries, followed by Mason (21 for 107 and two touchdowns), Samuel (18 for 95) and Mitchell (18 for 49). Davis-Price has played in just one game, carrying six times for 21 yards late in a blowout win over Dallas.

Samuel was the first to depart, playing just nine snaps and rushing two times for 11 yards. He was on the bench before the end of the first quarter and ruled out at halftime, watching the rest of the game from the sidelines. He was injured on an 8-yard run on the first play from scrimmage.

“He just took a shot on the shoulder. It didn’t look overly big or anything,” Shanahan said. “It just connected right. If you watch him, the next eight plays, you could see the feeling was starting to go because he couldn’t use his left arm.”

With Samuel out, Ray-Ray McCloud played a season-high 44 snaps on offense, the most he’s had since playing 38 against the New York Giants when Brandon Aiyuk was out with a shoulder injury. McCloud was targeted just twice, catching one pass for four yards and had 23 yards rushing on two jet sweeps.

Jauan Jennings also had a season-high 33 snaps, catching two passes on four targets for 26 yards.

Williams injured his right ankle around the same time as Samuel departed, with Jaylon Moore coming in for two snaps. Williams returned to the game and finished it but left in an orthopedic walking boot.

Shanahan said the ankle sprain wasn’t a “high” sprain which typically takes a few weeks to heal.

Safety and core special teams player George Odum was also “day-to-day” with a quad contusion, and Shanahan said linebacker Dre Greenlaw, who missed the game with a hamstring strain, is expected to practice this week.

Offensive players were lamenting being “behind the sticks” too much of the evening, and the 49ers’ 3-for-12 performance on third down went a long way toward explaining why Purdy was 12-for-27 for 125 yards and his first interception in 248 passes.

“We got in some negative situations where we were second-and-long and third-and-long and with that pass rush and the tight coverages, that makes it really tough on a quarterback,” Shanahan said. “Just getting in those bad situations made Brock’s job a lot tougher.”

Instead of operating on third-and-medium or short, seven times the 49ers had a third-and-10 or worse, including a third-and-23 and a third-and-19. Overall, their average third-down play required 11 yards to get a first down.

The only conversions were a 2-yard run by Purdy on third-and-2, an 8-yard run by Ray-Ray McCloud on third-and-8 and a 9-yard completion to Jennings on third-and-2.
