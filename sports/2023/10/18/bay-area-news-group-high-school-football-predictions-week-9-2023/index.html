Bay Area News Group high school football predictions: Week 9, 2023

Welcome back to another round of the Bay Area News Group high school football picks competition, an all-in-fun activity that gets everyone ready and/or fired up for the weekend ahead.

We pick 16 more games this week, highlighted by a couple of Top 10 matchups: No. 3 San Ramon Valley at No. 7 California on Friday and No. 5 Archbishop Riordan at No. 1 Serra on Saturday.

SRV is coming off a gutsy overtime loss to De La Salle. California has been one of the pleasant surprises of the season.

Joseph Dycus and I took SRV.

Mike Lefkow?

“I am going to go with California,” Lefkow said. “After coming off a week like that, I think San Ramon is a great team. Don’t get me wrong. But I think they could be just a little bit flat for this game. California, they are a very good football team, and I think they want this game real bad. I think they’re still smarting from the Pittsburg game (38-35 loss last month).”

My reaction: “Wow.”

Serra, meanwhile, was a unanimous pick by the three of us.

Me: “I just think there is too much talent on defense. That’s where it’s going to be won. Riordan will score some points. But I think Serra will end up winning. Will it be a running clock? (pause). It’s going to be close.”

Dycus: “Riordan is a good team. There is no doubt about it. But they’re beating their WCAL opponents by 15 points. Serra is beating their opponents by 42 points. Maybe in a year or two, this will be a much more even matchup. But I think Serra wins by … I don’t think it’s a running clock, but I think it’s probably 20.”

Lefkow: “There is not going to be a running clock. Serra might win this game by 34 points. But that still wouldn’t be a running clock.” (Running clocks start with the point differential at 35 points in the fourth quarter). “I think Riordan is good enough to stay within shouting distance of Serra.”

In the picks race, Dycus and I went 11-5 last week. Lefkow finished 10-6. Lefkow trails me by eight games in the season standings. Dycus is 17 games out of first.
