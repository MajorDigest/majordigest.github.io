Prep roundup: Miramonte, Washington-Fremont girls water polo, Pioneer flag football among Wednesday’s winners

Girls water polo

Miramonte 10, Acalanes 9

In another thriller between two of the Bay Area’s premier water polo teams, Miramonte avenged a one-goal loss at home to Acalanes last month with a one-goal win over its Lafayette rival on Wednesday night in Diablo Athletic League Foothill Division play.

The Orinda school improved to 18-1 overall and 4-1 in the league. Acalanes fell to 15-4, 3-2.

Ally Larsen and Bea Hearey each scored three goals and Tali Stryker and Alison Sagara added two goals apiece to lead the Mats.

Goalkeeper Eliana Bottene saved 11 shots for Miramonte.

Acalanes led 2-0 after the first quarter and 6-5 at halftime. Miramonte scored three goals in the third quarter to take an 8-7 advantage into the final period.

Miramonte closes out league play next Wednesday at Las Lomas. Acalanes will play host to Campolindo next Wednesday in its league finale.

Washington-Fremont 15, James Logan 9

Leading 8-7 in the third quarter on the road against rival Logan, Washington went on a 7-1 run to take command as the Huskies finished Mission Valley Athletic League play undefeated.

They will be the No. 1 seed in next week’s league tournament.

Ava Ellsworth and Shristi Bandyopadhyay each scored four goals for Washington. Bandyopadhyay also drew three ejections and a five-meter.

Goalkeeper Ava Huggins had 10 saves and Sarah Gilbert finished with five assists as Washington finished the regular season 16-4, 6-0.

Logan is 13-7, 4-2.

Madeline Kuhlmann scored three goals for Logan.

Mission San Jose 10, Moreau Catholic 5

Mission San Jose honored its seniors on Wednesday night, a group that began their high school water polo careers during the spring 2021 COVID season and will finish with the best regular season in school history.

The Fremont school is 18-4.

Kira Clark, the team’s star utility player, scored seven goals against Moreau, giving her a program record 277 for her career.

The core group of seniors also includes goalkeeper Marissa White, sprinter Ozzy Lim, Sharvi Verma, defensive specialist Audrey Lau, Priya Voleti, Lucina Tonoyan, outside shooter Veda Jadaprolu, Mishika Agarwal and Vrindha Moka.

Another key player, Sarina Cortes-Morales, joined the team as a junior last season.

Verma had two goals on Wednesday and White had one from beyond the middle of the pool to beat the halftime buzzer.

Northgate 15, Clayton Valley 6

Sophia Weise finished with six goals and Chloe Pappas had seven steals and four goals to lead Northgate to a victory at home over Clayton Valley in Diablo Athletic League Valley Division play.

The Broncos improved to 14-2, 5-0. Clayton Valley is 5-12, 2-3.

“I’m always very proud of my team, especially in games like this where everybody has an opportunity to play,” Northgate coach Jane Bergez said in an email. “Overall, everybody had a great game and we are very excited for our tournament this weekend.”

Tori Malsom had three goals, three steals and two assists for Clayton Valley. Kai Leigh Hernandez and Maya Canada combined for 11 saves against what Clayton Valley coach Ernie Minglana called a “barrage of shots from Northgate.”

Pioneer 7, Willow Glen 5

Pioneer closed out the Blossom Valley Athletic League regular season with a victory at home in San Jose over Willow Glen behind three goals from Katherine Peres and two goals each from Jenna Salles-Cunha and Ava Ries.

Anna Murphy scored one goal and Alexandra Murphy had 16 saves.

For Willow Glen, Evangeline D’Souza and Isabelle Greunke had two goals apiece.

Pioneer (16-6, 2-2) finished third in the league standings, behind first-place Leland and runner-up Leigh.

Sacred Heart Prep 12, Menlo School 3

SHP got balanced scoring to cruise at home past Menlo School on Senior Day.

Five Gators — Charlotte Summe, Vivian Golub, Natalia Szczerba, Cal McElhinney and Kiernan Hogan — scored two goals apiece to lead the way as SHP improved to 17-3 overall and 7-0 in the West Bay Athletic League.

Kenya Cassidy had two goals for Menlo.

Boys water polo

James Logan 8, Washington-Fremont 7, 3 OT

Jacob Campa’s third goal of the match was the winner as he fired a shot past the goalkeeper in triple overtime to lift James Logan to a dramatic Senior Night victory at home over rival Washington in Mission Valley Athletic League play.

Campo also scored in the first overtime session as Logan finished the regular season 13-7 overall and 5-1 in the league. The Colts were second in the league standings, one game behind Newark Memorial.

Washington is 7-8, 4-2.

Sacred Heart Prep 12, Menlo School 6

Andrew Spencer made the most of his Senior Day, scoring five goals and adding an assist and a steal, to lead the Gators to a victory over rival Menlo School in WBAL play.

SHP is 17-6, 7-0.

The Gators also got two goals apiece from Tyler Hogan and Nelson Harris.

Jackson Coleman and Teddy Meeks each scored twice for Menlo, which slipped to 7-11, 2-3.

Leland 16, Gilroy 4

Leland continued to build momentum toward the postseason as the Chargers scored five goals in the second quarter to erase an early one-goal deficit and went on to prevail over Gilroy.

Luke Raimondi had four goals, four assists and four steals, Max Buran finished with five goals and two assists and Louis DeScioli added two goals, four assists and four steals to lead the San Jose school.

“I thought the boys played really well tonight,” coach Sam Raimondi said. “They played solid defense today that really slowed the Gilroy offense. This was a great tune-up game before some important games this weekend and our end-of-season BVAL league championship games next weekend.”

Leland improved to 14-6. Gilroy is 11-4.

Mission San Jose 17, Moreau Catholic 4

Vinny Yao, one of six MSJ seniors honored in the team’s final regular season home match, scored seven goals to help the Warriors improve to 14-10.

Justin Ho added three goals and Nick Zhang had two.

Ho and Zhang also were recognized on Senior Night, as were driver Elton Yu, hole set Kaidinh Tran and goalkeeper Doniv Vinod.

Tuesday

Los Gatos 18, Harker 14

In a tight match in its home pool, Los Gatos got numerous contributions to turn back Harker as the Wildcats improved to 22-1.

Ryan Madsen and Liam Amburgey each scored four goals and Mathijs Dirven and Connor Sullivan added three goals apiece to propel the Wildcats, who led 6-4 after the first quarter, 8-7 at halftime and 12-10 going to the fourth quarter.

Jake Walton scored one goal for Los Gatos, which visits Mountain View on Saturday.

Harker is 13-7.

Girls volleyball

St. Patrick-St. Vincent 3, St. Mary’s-Berkeley 1

SPSV captured the Tri-County Athletic League championship, avenging two regular-season losses to St. Mary’s with a 25-21, 25-20, 14–25, 25-20 victory in the league final.

Dior Duru had the match-winning kill for the Bruins (32-8).

Cierra Ray, Olivia Butcher, Himani Bakshi and Danica Pagtakhan also made important contributions for SPSV, said coach Sal Paja, who added, “We worked our way to get to the finals to play them again and win league.”

St. Mary’s is 19-11.

Castro Valley 3, Bishop O’Dowd 1

Senior outside Avery Bland and sophomore Tessa Smith led the Trojans in kills as Castro Valley defeated Bishop O’Dowd 25-21, 17-25, 25-15, 25-20 to stay alone atop the West Alameda County Conference Foothill Division standings with one match to go.

Castro Valley (19-9, 10-1) visits Alameda on Thursday.

If the Trojans win, they will host a North Coast Section playoff match “for the first time in a very very long time,” coach Maggie Del Grande said.

O’Dowd fell to 12-20, 5-7.

Head-Royce 3, Bentley 0

In the Bay Counties League-East semifinals, Head-Royce prevailed 25-11, 25-12, 25-10 behind big hits from Melina McKinney and solid serving from Hailey Hilsabeck and Kalia Krakowski. Libero Natalie Kwon also had a nice match with her passing from the back.

Head-Royce (25-3) advanced to the final on Friday against College Prep or St. Joseph-Notre Dame.

Tuesday

No. 8 Menlo-Atherton 3, No. 7 Burlingame 2

Menlo-Atherton improved to 12-0 in PAL Bay Division play with a 25-20, 13-25, 25-19, 19-25, 15-12 nail-biter of a victory over Burlingame.

Daniela Eline (15 kills, 13 digs, two blocks), Duru Ruacan (15 kills, three aces, six digs) and Carley Gracia (13 digs, three aces) led M-A, as did Kiela King (19 digs, one ace), Sienna Morales (eight blocks, six kills) and Aria Sokol (five kills, three aces).

M-A is 18-6 overall. Burlingame slipped to 22-5, 10-2.

Girls flag football

Pioneer 27, Notre Dame-San Jose 0

Abbie Mackintosh had herself a day. The senior caught three touchdown passes and a two-point conversion and intercepted three passes to help lead Pioneer to a victory at home over Notre Dame-San Jose.

Jessica Tonegato added 105 yards rushing and a TD and Hailey Martinez, Tina Shaheen and Sara Maccioni pulled multiple flags and knocked down passes to prevent Notre Dame from scoring.

Pioneer improved to 9-3.

Menlo School 48, Sacred Heart Prep 0

What a difference a month makes. In September, Sacred Heart Prep edged Menlo School 2-0 in the girls version of the “Valpo Bowl.” The schools are next-door neighbors on Valparaiso Ave. in Atherton.

Wednesday, the Knights — guided by head coach John Paye and assistant Steve Young, both former 49ers quarterbacks — flipped the script in a huge way. They led 28-0 at halftime and cruised to the victory.

Sophie Housser returned an interception for a touchdown and pulled a flag to prevent a score. Ava Allen rushed for a TD and connected with Laila Young — Steve’s daughter — on a two-point play. Scarlett Bundy added a sack.

In the second half, Allen ran for a TD and threw a touchdown pass to Ellie Knoll.

Menlo School 24, Notre Dame-Belmont 19

In the second game on the day, Menlo edged host Notre Dame-Belmont as Krista Arreola scored on an interception return and Allen threw TD passes to Arreola, Maren Mulloy and Paige Miller.
