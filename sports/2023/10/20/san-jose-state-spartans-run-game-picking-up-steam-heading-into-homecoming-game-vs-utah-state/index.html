San Jose State Spartans’ run game picking up steam heading into Homecoming Game vs. Utah State

SAN JOSE — San Jose State has relied on the passing game as the catalyst of the offense during the Brent Brennan era, but the Spartans might have found a new identity running the football.

Saturday’s Homecoming Game at CEFCU Stadium (4 p.m.) could give their emerging rushing attack another opportunity to shine against Utah State defense that ranks in the bottom half of the Mountain West Conference in stopping the run.

“I believe once we get the opportunity to run the ball consistently, we can get the ball rolling,” said SJSU right tackle Jaime Navarro. “I think it’s really hard to stop all three of our running backs.”

Despite SJSU returning Chevan Cordeiro, a second-team All-Mountain West quarterback last season, coach Brent Brennan said he wanted to establish a more efficient run game this season. With a season-ending injury to wide receiver Justin Lockhart and tight end Sam Olson missing the last two games with an undisclosed injury, the Spartans (2-5, 1-2 MW) have relied on their running game more than maybe even Brennan expected

​​Senior Kairee Robinson has answered the call with a breakout season that might go down among the best ever for a SJSU running back.

Robinson is on pace to break SJSU’s single-season rushing touchdown record. Former Spartans Deoncé Whitaker and Johnny Johnson Jr. are tied for the record with 15, and Robinson currently sits at 10 with five regular-season games remaining.

Despite missing the second game of the season against Oregon State with a lower leg injury, Robinson has rushed for 471 yards at 6.5 yards per carry. The De La Salle product has an outside shot at becoming SJSU’s first 1,000-yard rusher since Tyler Ervin in 2015.

“Really I just hand it off and he does the work,” Cordeiro said after the Spartans’ 52-24 win over New Mexico last weekend. “It’s always good having the run game going. It opens up the pass and it makes my job a lot easier.”

Robinson is coming off the best game of his college career. The 5-foot-7 senior totaled 189 all-purpose yards and four touchdowns (three rushing and a receiving touchdown) against the Lobos. His career game earned him Mountain West Player of the Week on Monday.

Overall, SJSU has rushed for over 100 yards in five of the seven games played this season. The Spartans ran for a season-high 259 yards against New Mexico.

Quali Conley and freshman Jabari Bates have been solid compliments to Robinson.

Conley, a power back, rushed 123 yards and a touchdown against New Mexico. The Utah Tech transfer saw his touches dip since the start of the season, but continues to produce in short-yardage and goal-line situations.

Brennan and running backs coach Alonzo Carter have praised Bates’ ability to be versatile in the passing game as both a receiver in the backfield and as a blocker.

“We’ve been trying to figure out ways to get Jabari more touches to use his speed,” Carter said. “I think moving forward, he’ll earn even more playing time.”

Utah State is allowing 163 rushing yards per game and 4.09 yards per carry. The Aggies lost 37-32 to Fresno State last week and had little success slowing Bulldogs running back Malik Sherrod, who rushed for 131 yards and three touchdowns.

Similar production from any of their backs could go a long way for SJSU against the Aggies.

“We’re just trying to get this thing going,” Robinson said about the run game. “The first half of the season hasn’t gone the way we wanted it to, so hopefully this gives us a little momentum going into the second half of the season.”

Utah State quarterback Cooper Legas presents another challenge for SJSU’s defense.

Legas is the most efficient quarterback in the Mountain West, completing 66% of his throws to go along with 13 touchdowns.

When the Spartans played the Aggies last season, Legas played a crucial role in Utah State’s victory in Logan, Utah. He passed for 244 yards and a touchdown while leading the Aggies down the field on their final drive of the game in a 35-31 win.

“He’s got a great arm and he can throw on the move,” Brennan said. “Having a good player like they have in the last few years makes them really hard to defend.”

Saturday’s game will be the one-year anniversary of Camdan McWright’s death. McWright died in a traffic accident last season at the age of 18.

“We don’t forget what happened to Camdan,” Navarro said. “We just know we have to get right for him Saturday because he would do the same thing for us.”

  • The Spartans have a +6 turnover margin which is 17th in the nation and third in the Mountain West.
  • According to ESPN, SJSU has the second-ranked special teams unit in the country. The Spartans have blocked two punts (one for a touchdown), recovered three onside kicks and returned a punt for a touchdown.
