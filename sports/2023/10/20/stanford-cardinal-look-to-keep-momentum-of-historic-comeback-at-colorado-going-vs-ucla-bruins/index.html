Stanford football: Can Cardinal keep momentum of historic comeback going vs. UCLA Bruins?

Coming off the biggest comeback in school history, Stanford is looking for its first home victory — and consecutive wins — of the season when it faces UCLA on Saturday night at Stanford Stadium (7:30 p.m., ESPN).

The Cardinal (2-4, 1-3 Pac-12) was the talk of the college football world after overcoming a 29-0 halftime deficit at Colorado to win 46-43 in double overtime last Friday night. But coach Troy Taylor said he hasn’t noticed a difference during practice this week.

“Our guys have been the same,” Taylor said. “That’s been their strength, really. Every day they come in, they bring the same energy and the same focus. So I haven’t seen any difference, and that’s one of the things that I admire the most about them is they’re just always the same guys and giving the same effort and the same intensity.”

That same attitude – never too low, never too high – allowed them to pull off the fourth-largest comeback win in Pac-12 history, and the largest halftime deficit overcome in a win.

“It’s not a big speech, it’s not a huge adjustment,” Taylor said. “The thing I’m most proud of is there was no bickering on the sidelines, there were no people panicking in any respect. And so when you have that kind of attitude, mentality, you just keep playing and then all of a sudden something happens and you find yourself in the game. And so it wasn’t anything major. It’s just the little things and once you get a little momentum going and some confidence you got a chance.”

A big reason was the play of redshirt freshman receiver Elic Ayomanor, who had a school-record 294 receiving yards, all after halftime. Ayomanor and fellow receiver Bryce Farrell are the fastest players on the offense, according to tracking data, and he showed his speed with 97- and 60-yard scores. His third and final TD was a 30-yard catch in overtime when he trapped the ball on the helmet of star cornerback Travis Hunter.

It all came after he dropped a deep ball on a post pattern in the first half and was held without a catch.

“Confidence is pretty close to the most important thing that you’re going to have in anything you do,” Taylor said. “Games never go exactly how you want them to. Even when you win there’s going to be kind of ups and downs. So really proud that he continued to work through those things and showed the competitive greatness that we knew was inside of him.”

Taylor is now hoping to see that happen at home, where Stanford has lost to Oregon, Arizona and Sacramento State. No. 25 UCLA (4-2, 1-2) has lost its last two road games, though those were against No. 12 Oregon State and No. 14 Utah.

A big factor will be if Stanford can be balanced on offense. The Bruins have allowed a nation’s-best 2.3 yards per rush this season and are tied with Iowa, Oklahoma and Texas A&M for first overall with just one rushing touchdown allowed.

Stanford is ninth in the Pac-12 in yards per rushing attempt (3.7) and rushing TDs (eight) and has started three different running backs as it seeks a consistent threat in the backfield. Cardinal running backs had just two carries during its scoreless first half against Colorado, with E.J. Smith gaining a combined two yards.

The running game may be even more important since Taylor said tight end Benjamin Yurosek is day-to-day. The star tight end appeared to injure his shoulder in the second half against the Buffaloes. John Humphreys, who was projected to be Stanford’s top receiver, still isn’t ready to return after suffering an injury in the season opener.

The lack of weapons on offense is one reason why UCLA is favored by 17 points, despite Stanford’s momentum from the historic comeback. The Cardinal likely won’t be favored to win any of its remaining games, but it showed last week that it can pull an unlikely upset.

“It’s one of those things that you know you’ll remember your entire life,” Taylor said. “I’ve coached a lot and played in a lot of games and I don’t remember all of them, but you know that is one that I personally will remember, and just doing it with a bunch of guys that you really admire and love makes it really special.”

The Bruins and Cardinal have played every year except one since the end of World War II but this could be the last meeting for the in-state rivals for quite a while. UCLA is heading to the Big Ten next season and Stanford will be in the ACC.

UCLA has a 47-43-3 advantage in the rivalry after winning three of the past four meetings. Stanford had won 11 in a row before then.

“It’s all sad,” UCLA head coach Chip Kelly said. “I have feelings on the whole thing. I think that we should all still be together. Some people should be able to work that out.”

The Associated Press contributed to this report.
