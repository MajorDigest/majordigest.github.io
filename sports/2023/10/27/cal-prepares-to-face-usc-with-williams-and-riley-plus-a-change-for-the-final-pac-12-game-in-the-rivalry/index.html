Cal prepares to face USC with Williams and Riley, plus a change for the final Pac-12 game in the rivalry

BERKELEY — If Cal officials conduct roll call Saturday afternoon at Memorial Stadium, Bears fans may be disappointed when USC coach Lincoln Riley and Heisman Trophy-winning quarterback Caleb Williams raise their hands and respond, “Present,” after some doubt about their availability crept in earlier this week.

Better news for the home team is that Cal redshirt freshman quarterback Fernando Mendoza says he is more confident and better prepared for what will be his third career start — each one of them against a Top 25 opponent.

But absent from the proceedings will be one of Cal’s most beloved former players as the athletic department made a decision sure to confuse and rile the fan base. After 46 years, the Bears’ home matchup this season against the visiting Los Angeles school is not designated the Joe Roth Memorial Game.

As the Bears (3-4, 1-3) and 24th-ranked Trojans (6-2, 4-1) both try to snap two-game losing streaks in their final meeting before Cal heads off to the Atlantic Coast Conference and USC to the Big Ten, there is a lot to unpack here. Let’s get started:

Riley and Williams, the two most important Trojans, will be ready to go. Riley’s status seemed a bit unsure early in the week when he stayed home from practice for two days under doctor’s orders while fending off pneumonia.

He was back Wednesday and will bring a two-year record of 17-5 to town.

Williams, who came from Oklahoma to USC with Riley before last season, has been spectacular for the Trojans. But he totaled just one touchdown pass and three interceptions in losses to Notre Dame and Utah the past two weeks, prompting at least one analyst to suggest he should shut it down right now and await the NFL Draft, where he is projected as a possible No. 1 pick.

“The Heisman is a long shot, CFB Playoffs are even less likely, and he won’t play in the bowl game,” Fox analyst Emmanuel Acho wrote on social media. “The risk of playing FAR outweighs the reward. Business decision.”

Williams hasn’t left USC but is everywhere these days, including on TV commercials for Wendy’s and Dr. Pepper, fueling an NIL income stream supposedly in the neighborhood of $2.7 million.

According to Mike Florio of Pro Football Talk, Williams’ agents are spreading the word that he wants a financial stake in whatever NFL team drafts him. Problem is, that’s currently not allowed by league rules.

“There’s a lot going on with Caleb Williams,” said beat writer Ryan Kartje of the Los Angeles Times. “And it’s fair to wonder what his headspace is right now.”

After throwing two TD passes each in losses to Oregon State and Utah, Mendoza said the bye week gave him the chance to further settle into his role as starter.

“I would say that I’m an entirely different quarterback, as I have gained a ton of experience, playing against two of the top defenses in the Pac-12,” he said. “I’ve seen my development expedite and in a sense fast-forwarded. so I feel a lot more comfortable going into this game than the Oregon State game.”

Offensive coordinator Jake Spavital is encouraged by Mendoza’s play.

“I think Fernando has handled the circumstances pretty well,” Spavital said. “I think he’s really good at the second and third reads, which is an element that (had) been missing over the first five games.”

Not good enough, Mendoza added. “We didn’t win those games, so I was not up to par.”

Roth died at the age of 21 early in 1977 after privately battling melanoma through his senior season the previous fall. Within two months, the Cal athletic department announced plans to honor the All-American quarterback with the Joe Roth Memorial Game, a designation given to the annual home game against USC or UCLA.

The tradition was going to be altered next year when the schools no longer play in the same conference.

“As we prepare to make the transition to the ACC, we will be looking at starting a new tradition around honoring Joe Roth and his legacy,” Cal said in a statement. “In the meantime, we are excited to unveil a new version of the Joe Roth helmet on Saturday.”

An athletic department spokesperson, updating the statement, said Thursday night that the Joe Roth game will continue to be played in the future, but details are still to be worked out.
