Bay Area high school football roundup 2023: Best of Week 11 action

Welcome back to the Bay Area News Group’s high school football roundup.

Here, you’ll find all the details from the weekend’s action in this news organization’s coverage area, which encompasses teams that play in leagues based primarily in Alameda, Contra Costa, San Mateo and Santa Clara counties.

Check back every Saturday during the season for scores, highlights and top performers, updated throughout the day.

If you have not already, please subscribe here for complete digital access all season long. Your contributions keep us going.

On to the roundup …

Ranked teams

No. 1 Serra 43, No. 10 St. Ignatius 10

When Central Coast Section officials assemble Sunday to fill out the brackets for the playoffs, the No. 1 seed will be easy. Serra completed its second straight perfect regular season by running away from St. Ignatius. It was 22-3 after one quarter and 36-3 at the half. The Padres (10-0, 7-0 West Catholic Athletic League) have won 20 regular-season games in succession. Their last loss in such a contest was on Nov. 6, 2021, when they dropped a 44-21 decision to St. Francis. Serra got even three weeks later, beating the Lancers 16-12 in the CCS playoffs. Senior quarterbacks Maealiuaki Smith and Brooks Trimmer combined to throw five touchdown passes on Senior Night at Serra, much to the delight of baseball alums Barry Bonds and Dodgers outfielder James Outman, who were in attendance. Smith also had a rushing touchdown. Kyon Loud had two TD receptions, both from Smith. Padres coach Patrick Walsh was emotional while discussing the back-to-back regular-season perfectos. “From where we started to where we are now, if you had asked me 10, 20 years ago if we could do this, I would’ve said it would have taken a miracle,” Walsh said. “I’m thrilled and honored at what we’ve accomplished. So many hands have helped stir the pot.” — Mike Lefkow

No. 2 De La Salle 48, No. 17 Clayton Valley 0

De La Salle won its eighth consecutive game to close the regular season and shut out an opponent for the second time in a dominating, running-clock victory at home over Clayton Valley. De La Salle led 14-0 after the first quarter and 41-0 at halftime. Toa Faavae ran for 76 yards and a touchdown and passed for 107 and two TDs to help lead the way. The Spartans also got two rushing touchdowns from Dominic Kelley and a receiving touchdown from Derrick Blanche. De La Salle (8-2) is expected to be the No. 1 seed in the NCS Open/Division I playoffs that start next week. Clayton Valley (5-5) is among the teams hoping for one of the eight spots in that bracket. – Darren Sabedra

No. 3 San Ramon Valley 34, Monte Vista 0

Quarterback Luke Baker threw a pair of touchdown passes to Owen Scott and ran 2 yards for another score as the Wolves beat Monte Vista for the eighth time in nine games dating back to 2017. SRV also completed a 9-1 regular season and won the East Bay Athletic League Mountain Division championship with a 4-0 mark. Next stop for the Wolves will be the North Coast Section playoffs, where it will likely be the No. 2 seed behind De La Salle in Open/Division I. The Wolves’ only loss this season came in overtime against the Spartans. Baker’s 2-yard scamper started the scoring against Monte Vista, then he threw a 25-yard touchdown pass to Scott to make it 14-0. Austin Shelton’s 44-yard field goal increased the Wolves’ margin to 17-0 at the half. Marco Jones opened the scoring in the third quarter with a 30-yard pick-6, then Baker and Scott hooked up on a 76-yard pass play later in the period to make it 31-0. The two TD passes by Baker gives him 33 for the season. Shelton completed the scoring with his second three-pointer, this one in the fourth quarter. Monte Vista finished the season 4-6. – Mike Lefkow

No. 6 McClymonds 58, Fremont-Oakland 0

McClymonds shut out its fourth consecutive Oakland Athletic League opponent on Friday night to remain perfect in OAL play. John “Sharky” Tamale rushed for 208 yards, caught two passes for 68 yards, and scored four total touchdowns. Redmani Albert, who has played quarterback for the past few weeks, moved back to receiver and caught a touchdown while rushing for another. Isaac Espinosa completed all four of his passes for 127 yards and a touchdown, and also went 6 for 6 on extra points. Jayden Parker carried the ball six times for 72 yards and one touchdown, and DB Markus Thomas returned an interception 22 yards for a TD. McClymonds (6-2, 4-0) will play host to Oakland Tech to finish its regular season, and Fremont (1-8, 1-3) will welcome Castlemont. – Joseph Dycus

No. 7 Los Gatos 30, Menlo-Atherton 6

Los Gatos closed out its PAL Bay schedule with a comfortable victory over Menlo-Atherton. After going 8-2 with a 5-0 record in league play, the next stop for coach Mark Krail’s public school program will be the CCS Open/Division I playoffs. M-A will be one of the Bay’s five automatic qualifiers for the playoffs. Los Gatos running back Boxer Kopcsak-Yeung rushed for 152 yards and three touchdowns to go over 1,000 yards on the season. Los Gatos also scored on a trick play, where quarterback A.J Minyard threw a backward pass to Jaylen Thomas, who lobbed a 76-yard touchdown pass to Scott Garwood. On the defensive side of the ball for Los Gatos, Lyndon Bailey had a sack, Henry Masters had double-digit tackles and Theo Greenland forced a fumble. Menlo-Atherton dropped to 4-6. – Joseph Dycus

No. 8 St. Francis 28, Bellarmine 10

Victory did not come easily for the visitors. It was 7-7 at halftime, and St. Francis didn’t take the lead until the 6:45 mark of the third quarter when QB Aaron Knapp and TE James Jordan hooked up on a 60-yard touchdown pass. Knapp also threw a 71-yard TD to Perrion Williams in the fourth quarter. The Lancers finished the season 7-3 overall and 6-1 in the WCAL, good enough for second place behind Serra. St. Francis should be the No. 2 seed in the Open/Division I bracket when the CCS playoff matchups are announced Sunday. Bellarmine had a disappointing season under first-year coach David Diaz-Infante, going 2-8, 1-6. Knapp completed eight of 16 passes for 244 yards and the two long TD passes. The junior, who spent the off-season battling lymphoma, finished the regular season with 1,241 passing yards and 11 TD passes. More importantly, he is cancer-free. Kingston Keanaaina ran for 120 yards and one TD, giving him 1,300 yards and 16 TDs for the season. St. Francis compiled 392 yards of total offense. Bellarmine took a 7-0 lead in the first quarter on a quarterback sneak by Parker Threatt. But the Bells didn’t score again until Connor Tripp’s 41-yard field in the fourth quarter made it 21-10. Williams’ 71-yard TD catch completed the scoring. – Mike Lefkow

No. 11 Wilcox 60, Santa Clara 14

Wilcox continued its dominance of crosstown rival Santa Clara, cruising at home to a non-league victory that completed a 7-3 regular season for the Chargers and presumably a spot in the CCS Open/Division I playoffs. If the Chargers aren’t in that bracket, they’d be in D-II. Of the many highlights for Wilcox on Friday, Adrian Salinas’ 101-yard interception return for a touchdown and Elijah Walker’s touchdown runs of 80 and 66 yards stood out. Wilcox is 20-0 against Santa Clara in the MaxPreps era (2004-present). The Bruins finished 3-7. – Darren Sabedra

No. 12 Valley Christian 35, No. 19 Archbishop Mitty 0

Ekiah Mclemore and Marcel Leggett ran for two touchdowns apiece and Lovante Metcalfe, Remy Hernandez and Federico-Mario Cobarubio each had a sack as Valley Christian overpowered Mitty at Foothill College. The Warriors also got a touchdown pass from Jesse D’Anna to Kai Hamilton. One week after playing a tight game against juggernaut Serra, Valley led Mitty 21-0 after the first quarter and 28-0 at halftime as the Warriors finished the regular season 6-4 overall and 3-4 in the WCAL. They are expected to land in the eight-team Open/Division I bracket for the CCS playoffs. Mitty (5-5, 2-5) also is expected to be in the section playoffs, either in Open/Division I or Division II. – Darren Sabedra

Sacred Heart Prep 31, No. 14 Menlo School 19

Anthony Noto scored two touchdowns to help SHP win the Valpo Bowl and deny Menlo an undefeated regular season. Noto (13 carries, 84 yards)scored on a 30-yard run and on a 48-yard fumble return as the Gators built a 28-13 lead at halftime. Ravu Savali (13 carries, 67 yards) got the scoring started with a 4-yard run. Jack Scully (11 of 17, 162 yards) hit Jack Stevens with a 30-yard TD pass. For Menlo, Mikey McGrath passed to Harry Housser for one touchdown and ran 3 yards for another. Jack Freehill connected with Bradford Tudor on another Menlo TD. SHP is 6-4, Menlo 9-1 heading into the CCS playoffs.

No. 16 Campolindo 25, No. 15 Acalanes 24

Micah Parker scored on a 23-yard run with 2:53 left and Scott Lyon followed by catching a two-point conversion pass from Isaiah Ortiz to complete Campolindo’s late rally from 14 points behind. The Cougars tied Acalanes and Las Lomas for the DAL Foothill Division championship. Darren Sabedra has the full story here.

No. 18 California 48, Foothill 0

Cal High quarterback Jayden Macedo threw for four touchdowns and rushed for one TD in the Grizzlies’ rout of Foothill in EBAL Mountain play. California (6-4, 2-2) will find out if it is one of the eight teams to make the NCS Open/Division I playoffs on Sunday. Tight end Chase McGill scored his ninth touchdown of the season, and Nick Fasshauer, Brodie Bamer and Trevor Chacon all scored one touchdown apiece. Wide receiver Josh Calcagno caught two touchdowns. Foothill finished the season 1-8. – Joseph Dycus

No. 21 Liberty 52, Heritage 21

The Lions did what they had to do, building a 15-0 lead at intermission, then erupting for 37 points in the second half. Just whether that will be enough for an invite to the NCS Open/D-1 playoffs will be determined on Sunday. Liberty coach Mike Cable said Saturday it might come down to his team or Clayton Valley for the eighth and final playoff spot. Liberty finished 7-3 overall, Clayton Valley was 5-5. The Ugly Eagles played a considerably tougher schedule. Liberty’s only defeats were against Los Gatos, San Ramon Valley and Pittsburg, all very good teams. In the regular-season finale against visiting Heritage, sophomore Jaxon Bell rushed for 273 yards and three touchdowns, giving him 1,537 rushing yards and 25 TDs for the season. He’s also caught two TD passes. Senior receiver Ryan McKendry had eight catches for 116 yards and three TDs, lifting his season totals to 52 catches for 1,024 yards and 15 scores. Heritage, which got receiving TDs from Brooks Davis and Charlie Blaise against the Lions, appears to be on the playoff bubble for D-II. The Patriots went into the weekend as the No. 7 team in calpreps.com’s division rankings, but room has to be made for division champion Berkeley, and Dublin and Livermore were both 5-5 compared to Heritage’s 4-6 mark. The Patriots did have the toughest schedule of those four schools. – Mike Lefkow

No. 22 Las Lomas 45, Miramonte 7

Las Lomas secured a share of the Diablo Athletic League Foothill Division title after it defeated Miramonte. The Knights, Campolindo and Acalanes all finished 5-1 in league play. Las Lomas defeated Campolindo, Campolindo beat Acalanes, and Acalanes took down Las Lomas. Las Lomas will play in the NCS Division III bracket. Knights quarterback Dylan Thomas completed 10 passes for 170 yards and two touchdowns, with his favorite target Roman Mercado catching six passes for 120 yards and a touchdown. Sava Pouridis, Thomas, and Kaden Lagaya each ran for a touchdown. Miramonte (6-3, 3-3) will play in Division V. – Joseph Dycus

No. 24 Live Oak 28, No. 20 Christopher 24

Trailing 21-0 in the first quarter, Live Oak stunned host Christopher by completing an epic comeback that clinched the BVAL Mt. Hamilton Division title while handing the Gilroy school its first loss. Live Oak running back Josh Gagni ran for 136 yards, and Anden Rogers scored the game-winning touchdown with five minutes remaining. Glenn Reeves was in Gilroy and has the full story here.

Aragon 34, No. 25 Hillsdale 7

Sean Hickey threw for 260 yards and three touchdowns as Aragon rolled to a surprisingly one-sided win in the traditional rivalry game. Hickey’s scoring passes went to three different tight ends: Willie Faaumau, Aaron Pita and Max Thronson. Ivan Nisa rushed for 117 yards and two touchdowns. Both teams advance to the CCS playoffs. Amaziah Tanielu stood out defensively. Aragon (8-2, 4-1) tied for first place with Capuchino n the PAL Ocean, Hillsdale (7-3, 3-2) in a three-way tie for second with Palo Alto and The King’s Academy in the PAL Bay. “It’s possible we’ll play them again next week,” Aragon coach Steve Sell said. “I think we’ll end up in the same division. That would be nuts.” — Glenn Reeves

East Bay

Castro Valley 34, Tennyson 24

Conner Cooper ran for 68 yards and two touchdowns and had nine tackles (two behind the line of scrimmage) and forced a fumble to help lead Castro Valley to a season-ending victory over Tennyson in MVAL/WACC Foothill play. The Trojans also got 150 yards and two TDs passing — plus 50 yards rushing — from Cooper Matthews and three catches for 48 yards and a touchdown from Drew Leipold. Sevag Hakimian scored on a 16-yard run and Nathaniel Belli finished with eight tackles (three for loss), a forced fumble and a sack. Castro Valley improved to 4-6, 2-3. Tennyson finished 2-8, 1-4. – Darren Sabedra

Hayward 34, Washington-Fremont 20

Hayward’s dynamic quarterback Kennedy Braziel threw for two touchdowns and rushed for 93 yards and another TD as the Farmers held off Washington-Fremont in a MVAL/WACC-Mission Division game. Hayward finished the regular season 7-3 and could make the NCS Division III playoffs. Mateaki Pongo rushed for 117 yards and one TD, and Jordyn Wilson also rushed for a touchdown. Dillan Bui turned his one reception into a 29-yard touchdown. Washington ended the regular season 5-5. — Joseph Dycus

James Logan 42, Moreau Catholic 14

Logan coach Eddie Smith has sort of resigned himself to the same fate he has had in each of his first two experiences in the NCS playoffs – a first-round match against Open/Division I top seed De La Salle. The Colts assured themselves a playoff berth with a convincing win over Moreau Catholic on Friday night. It was their sixth win in a row, including a 5-0 mark in the MVAL/WACC Foothill, placing them one game ahead of Bishop O’Dowd in the division standings. But it’s what occurred during the non-league portion of the season that likely will make them a No. 8 seed in the playoffs. Logan went 1-4 against a tough schedule that included back-to-back losses to California and Antioch, two more of the 11 teams vying for spots in the eight-team Open/D-I bracket. Neither contest was close. Logan is playing well now. Quarterback Jonathan Craft threw four TD passes and ran for another score as the Colts built a 28-7 halftime lead against Moreau. The junior transfer from Valley Christian has thrown 16 TD passes this season. The Mariners (5-5, 3-2) shouldn’t have to worry about making the D-VI playoffs. The only question is whether they are one of the top four seeds and get a home game in the first round. They’re 5-2 since an 0-3 start, but one-sided losses to Logan and Bishop O’Dowd, the top two teams in the Foothill, won’t help their cause. – Mike Lefkow 

Piedmont 29, Newark Memorial 26

Sam Shelby scored the game-winning touchdown with three minutes left as the Highlanders rallied to beat Newark Memorial and capture the MVAL/WACC Shoreline championship. Piedmont and Kennedy-Fremont were both 5-1 in league play, but the Highlanders beat the Titans 35-29 two weeks ago. Shelby’s TD Friday night gave Piedmont a 27-26 advantage, then coach Jordan Seiden elected to go for the 2-point conversion. It was good, putting Piedmont up by three. “They have a very good kicker,” said Seiden, explaining that he didn’t want to give N-M placekicker Diego Arreola an opportunity to tie the game. Piedmont, 7-3 this season after dropping the varsity team last year after six games, is now headed to the NCS D-V playoffs. Seiden is hoping for a top-four seed, which means his team wouldn’t have to travel extensively in the first round. Piedmont doesn’t have a home field, as some of the athletic facilities including the football stadium, are being renovated. Newark Memorial is a candidate for the D-IV playoffs, but this is one of the deeper NCS brackets. The Cougars came into the weekend ranked 11th in the division by calpreps.com and might need some help. – Mike Lefkow

Vallejo 49, Richmond 0

A week after surviving a wild 56-54 victory over St. Patrick-St. Vincent, Vallejo’s season finale was a much less stressful win against TCAL-Stone opponent Richmond. The Redhawks led 42-0 at halftime, and are locked into the NCS Division IV playoffs as league champions. Jasier Smith completed 7 of 8 passes for 147 yards and three touchdowns. Sedrick Vaughn had 148 yards from scrimmage and scored two receiving touchdowns and one rushing TD. Darrell Hurd and DeShawn Prack also caught touchdown passes. Vallejo finished the regular season 7-3. Richmond fell to 1-8. – Joseph Dycus

South Bay/Peninsula

Branham 21, Pioneer 20

Branham clinched the outright BVAL Santa Teresa-Foothill Division title on Thursday, stopping a late two-point conversion to edge host Pioneer. The Mustangs cut the deficit to one when quarterback Damian Flores connected with Edgar Orozco for the duo’s second touchdown of the fourth quarter. But Branham made the late stop to celebrate a 5-0 league record and an 8-2 mark overall. The Bruins will now get ready for the CCS playoffs. Pioneer finished 4-6, 2-3. Branham twice built a double-digit advantage. The Bruins went ahead 7-0 in the first quarter when Preston Conrad threw an 18-yard pass to Brayden Hodges. The visitors extended the advantage to 14-0 in the third quarter on Elias Antillon’s 25-yard run. Kian Upton’s pick-six pulled Pioneer to within 14-7, but Branham scored again early in the final quarter to make it 21-7. Flores’ TD pass to Orozco with five minutes led cut the margin to seven. – Darren Sabedra

Cupertino 41, Monta Vista 14

Shen Hernandez-Ong threw three touchdown passes and ran for another as the Pioneers sent out retiring coach Chris Oswald on a positive note.”Thirty-one years is enough,” Oswald said.  Malachi Perez and James Xue were on the receiving end of Hernandez-Ong’s TD passes. Arvin Hosseini added a rushing touchdown. Will Hawkins recovered a fumble in the end zone after a strip sack. Dylan Zamouruev also stood out defensively. Cupertino finishes 7-3 overall, 6-1 for second place in the PAL Lake. Monta Vista ends its season 3-7, 2-5. — Glenn Reeves

Del Mar 42, Prospect 31

Andre Latimore ran for 344 yards and four touchdowns to power Del Mar to a BVAL West Valley Division championship and a spot in the CCS playoffs. Prospect quarterback Tommy Lewit threw for a touchdown and ran for two more in a back-and-forth game. Joseph Dycus was in Saratoga and has the full story here.

Evergreen Valley 45, James Lick 21

Jordan Vo had a big game for Evergreen Valley as the Cougars closed out the season and celebrated senior night with a victory at home over James Lick on Thursday. Vo, a junior, ran for 99 yards and three touchdowns in 16 carries and caught four passes for 95 yards and a two-point conversion. Evergreen Valley, which led from start to finish, also got 136 yards passing and 145 yards rushing from Kenneth Brown. On defense, Brandon Chan and Noah Holseberg each had 13 tackles. Holseberg forced three fumbles and nine of Chan’s tackles were solo. Pedro Hernandez was 4 for 4 on PATs and also made a field goal as Evergreen Valley improved to 3-7 overall and 3-4 in the BVAL West Valley Division. Lick’s Malik Martinez ran for 167 yards in 24 carries and Eva Grigalba ran for 42 yards and scored one of Lick’s TDs. Lick finished 1-9, 1-6. – Darren Sabedra

Fremont-Sunnyvale 37 Homestead 0

Don Martinez returned an interception 108 yards for a touchdown and Henry Buenrostro ran for 68 yards and two touchdowns to lead Fremont to a victory over rival Homestead in the annual Battle for the Bell. Martinez finished with two interceptions, two of five turnovers Homestead had on the night. Fremont scored 27 points in the fourth quarter to put the outcome out of reach. Jordan McKinney scored on an 18-yard run on Fremont’s opening drive and finished with 49 yards rushing. Homestead’s Ido Saban and Gustavo Escobedo combined for 60 yards rushing. Fremont finished the regular season 7-3, its best record since it went 9-2 in 2014. Homestead ended the season 1-9. – Darren Sabedra

Leigh 34, Westmont 27

Blake Zanger ran for two late touchdowns as Leigh rallied on the road to beat Westmont and clinch the BVAL Santa Teresa-Valley Division title. The game ended with the Longhorns making a goal-line stop. Nathan Canilao has the complete game story here.

Leland 24, Lincoln 7

Diego Vasquez scored three touchdowns, on runs of 16 and 25 yards and on a pick 6 to lead the Chargers. Luke Whitson kicked a 36-yard field goal. Kyan Phillips scored for Lincoln on an 8-yard run to cut Leland’s 14-0 halftime lead to 14-7. Both teams finish the regular season 3-7 overall. Leland is 2-3 in BVAL Mt. Hamilton Division play, in a three-way tie for third place with Santa Teresa and Oak Grove. Because of tiebreakers, Santa Teresa and Leland will get the division’s final two berths in the CCS playoffs. — Glenn Reeves

Milpitas 35, Woodside 28

Jacob Alvarado ran for 177 yards and two TDs, helping the Trojans (6-4) finish above .500 for the first time since 2019. It’s doubtful that will help the Trojans reach the Central Coast Section playoffs. They were fourth in the PAL Ocean, which gets two automatic bids. Forty teams spread over five eight-team brackets make the CCS playoffs. Capuchino and Aragon, both 4-1 in league, get the automatic bids. San Mateo (3-2) was third, followed by Milpitas and Sequoia, which tied for fourth at 2-3. Woodside (6-4) had already qualified for the postseason before playing Milpitas. The Wildcats finished in a three-way tie for second with Fremont-Sunnyvale and Carlmont, all with 3-2 marks in the PAL El Camino, but they beat both teams in head-to-head play. Los Altos was the division champion. Milpitas led Woodside 17-14 at the half and never relinquished it. The Trojans put the game away on a touchdown run with three minutes to go, making the score 35-21. Sophomore kicker Jerrell Phan had a big game on Senior Night at Milpitas, kicking two field goals and adding three extra points. Emiliano Diaz led the defense with 10 tackles. Woodside got 108 rushing yards and a TD from Evan Frampton, but they were without leading ballcarrier Evan Usher for the second straight game. Usher, who has 1,158 yards and 14 touchdowns this season, is sidelined with an injured shoulder. Coach Justin Andrews texted that he is hopeful Usher will return for the playoffs. – Mike Lefkow

Mountain View 42, Los Altos 13

Arturo Hernandez rushed for three touchdowns and Kevin Conway passed for three as Mountain View bolted out to a 42-0 lead at halftime. Alex Pelkofer made two interceptions in the first quarter to fuel the early onslaught. Lex Silver, Ami Sekona and Brigham Robinson were on the receiving end of Conway’s touchdown passes. Mountain View, from the PAL Bay, finishes 4-6. Los Altos, the PAL El Camino champion, is 5-5. — Glenn Reeves

Oak Grove 19, Santa Teresa 7

Ryan Wright ran for a touchdown and threw a touchdown pass to Junior Alvarado as Oak Grove, playing at home, beat rival Santa Teresa to finish in a three-way tie for third with ST and Leland in the BVAL’s Mt. Hamilton Division. But Oak Grove lost tiebreakers for the division’s final two automatic CCS playoff spots and isn’t likely to advance. Santa Teresa won the three-way tie for third because it has the most section power points among the three, league commissioner Bill Murray said. Leland then won the two-way tie for fourth because it beat Oak Grove. In its win Friday, Oak Grove also got a touchdown run from Aidan Cisneros, an interception from Kufre Etok and a fumble recovery by Shamar Mathews. Oak Grove finished 4-6 overall and 2-3 in the division. Santa Teresa also is 4-6, 2-3. – Darren Sabedra

Overfelt 30, Sobrato 28

With both teams needing a win to qualify for the CCS playoffs, Overfelt overcame a 20-point deficit at home in the fourth quarter to stun the visitors from Morgan Hill. “We overcame mistakes after mistakes,” Overfelt coach Carlo Maningo wrote in a text to the Bay Area News Group. The Royals trailed 28-8 when they staged a wild rally to finish tied for second with Sobrato and Westmont in the BVAL Santa Teresa-Valley Division standings. Because Overfelt (6-4, 3-2) beat both Sobrato and Westmont, it will join league champion Leigh as the division’s two automatic qualifiers into the CCS playoffs. Walter Smith caught nine passes for 108 yards and forced Sobrato’s last fumble. John Stowers rushed for 123 and two touchdowns in 16 carries and contributed 16 tackles, including two sacks and four for loss. Javier Mendoza added 103 yards and a TD in 12 carries, six tackles and an interception. Noah Taylor, one of the nation’s top rushers, had another strong game running the ball and Sobrato intercepted two passes, including one that it returned for a touchdown. The Bulldogs finished 6-4, 3-2. – Darren Sabedra

Palo Alto 34, The King’s Academy 28

Palo Alto used a huge fourth quarter to rally past The King’s Academy in a PAL De Anza Division game on Thursday. The outcome leaves Palo Alto, TKA and Hillsdale tied for second in the division behind champion Menlo School. The division has three automatic qualifying spots into the CCS playoffs. It looks as if it will come down to CCS power points to determine the two teams that will join Menlo as AQs from the division. It is possible that a fourth team from the De Anza could receive an at-large bid. Palo Alto (5-5, 3-2) scored 20 points in the final quarter, including a nine-yard go-ahead touchdown run by Jeremiah Madrigal with two minutes left to put the Vikings in front for good. Palo Alto running back Joseph Kessler rushed for 135 yards and two touchdowns, and Madrigal ran 12 times for 61 yards and two TDS. Vikings QB Declan Packer threw a touchdown to Jason Auzenne, and Landa Mendoza had three sacks. TKA’s Jeaden Underwood rushed for 140 yards and one TD, and Reid Black threw for 255 yards and two touchdowns while rushing for one more. Kyle Welch caught two touchdowns for TKA (5-4, 2-3). The CCS playoff brackets will be announced on Sunday. — Joseph Dycus

Piedmont Hills 20, Gilroy 14

Piedmont Hills’ season ended on a high note after it defeated Gilroy in BVAL Santa Teresa-Foothill play. Gilroy struck first, taking a 6-0 advantage after Talan Warren rushed for a 10-yard first-quarter touchdown. Alex Tran threw a nine-yard TD to Zachary Pallach to give Piedmont Hills a one-point lead. Warren’s 41-yard pass to Juel Martinez, plus a Tyler Russell Hodges two-point conversion, gave Gilroy a 14-7 advantage at halftime. The second half was all Piedmont Hills, with Dylan Blank running for a one-yard TD to tie the score in the third. Alonzo Johnson’s 88-yard fourth-quarter interception return for a touchdown gave Piedmont Hills the victory. Piedmont Hills finished 4-5, 2-3. Gilroy finished its season 0-10, 0-5. — Joseph Dycus 

Sequoia 23, Carlmont 17

Logan Kern‘s 5-yard run for a touchdown in the final minute brought Sequoia from behind to win the Terremere Trophy in the annual rivalry game with Carlmont. It was Kern’s second touchdown of the game. Sequoia also scored on a pass from Brody McKenna to Aaron Harkov and on a McKenna field goal. Also for Sequoia, McKenna completed 21 of 35 passes for 229 yards. Kern carried 21 times for 120 yards. Tony Veimau stood out on the offensive and defensive line, Luke Hitch made an interception in the end zone and LB Giancarlo Toriello was in the backfield rushing the passer throughout the game. Braeden Kumer scored on runs of 50 and 3 yards as Carlmont (3-7, 2-3 PAL El Camino) opened a 14-0 lead. He also kicked a 30-yard field goal to put the Scots back on top 17-16 after Sequoia (4-6, 2-3 PAL Ocean) had taken the lead. — Glenn Reeves

Silver Creek 21, Independence 14

A touchdown scored by Alonzo Hernandez in the final minute broke a 14-14 deadlock and gave Silver Creek the win, second place in the BVAL Santa Teresa-Foothill Division standings and the division’s second automatic berth into the CCS playoffs. Alijah Guzman rushed for 108 yards and a touchdown. Hernandez added another 87 yards on the ground. Mathias McClain had five solo tackles, four assists and 1.5 sacks. Loni Ongolea had six solo, two assists and one tackle for loss. Silver Creek finished the regular season 7-3 overall, 4-1 in the division. Independence ends its season 4-6, 2-3. — Glenn Reeves
