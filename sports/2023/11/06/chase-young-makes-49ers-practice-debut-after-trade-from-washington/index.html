Chase Young makes 49ers practice debut after trade from Washington

SANTA CLARA — Chase Young, in a freshly appointed No. 92 jersey, made his 49ers’ practice debut Monday, six days after being traded from the Washington Commanders.

“We welcomed him to the team,” defensive tackle Arik Armstead of a morning meeting. “We’re happy to have him here and I’m looking forward to playing alongside him.”

The 6-foot-5, 265-pound Young took a seat in that meeting next to former Ohio State teammate Nick Bosa, then, once they were on the practice field, Bosa shared pass-rushing tips during defensive line drills.

The 49ers swung the deal to kickstart their defense and high-priced pass rush, which, a month ago, prompted them to also trade for Randy Gregory, who described the defensive line as “a very special unit” upon Young’s addition.

“I was excited when we added him. I’ve been a big fan of him since he was playing in college,” Gregory said. “Everything he does is how we want to play football on this D-line. He’ll be able to jump right in.”

Young’s Day 1 outfit for the walk-through, non-padded practice: Red jersey, red leggings under black shorts (cut-off jersey pants), red headband, and red-and-white Jordan cleats.

Acquired for a 2024 conditional third-round pick, Young passed a physical Wednesday to formally complete the trade, alleviating some concerns about his right knee injury from two years ago.

Young, drafted No. 2 overall in 2020, had five sacks in seven games this season with the Commanders, putting him on track for a career-high total. He had 7 1/2 sacks as the 2020 NFL Defensive Rookie of The Year, then a November 2021 knee injury sidelined him until the final three games of last season.

“Obviously he’s super talented and has great size,” Armstead said. “He’s been playing extremely well, and he’s unique in his own right.”

Young’s locker is wedged between two rookies — wide receiver Ronnie Bell and defensive end Robert Beal Jr., the latter of whom saw his injured-reserve practice window activated Monday to allow for three weeks of evaluation. Beal and Young are both 24, with Young just four months older.

The 49ers (5-3) look to snap a three-game losing streak Sunday when they visit the Jacksonville Jaguars (6-2). Both teams are coming off their bye week, and both rank No. 3 in their respective conference standings. The Jaguars are on a five-game win streak.

So, it’s back to the East Coast for Young to make his 49ers debut. Gregory, who also played extensively in the NFC East with the Dallas Cowboys, said he’s helped Young’s transition by explaining how the 49ers’ “operations” are different than other teams’.

“It’s kind of a culture shock for me and I’m sure it will be a little bit for him. But a better situation for both,” Gregory said.

Quarterback Brock Purdy returned to Iowa during the bye week, and not only did he harvest a field in a tractor, he also drew a standing ovation while recognized at Iowa State’s game 28-21 home loss Saturday night to Kansas. As for the tractor, his fiancée, Jenna Brandt, posted an Instagram video of him driving it on her family farm.

This Sunday marks Purdy’s first game in Jacksonville, where he has trained in each of the previous two offseasons. His final game as Iowa State’s career passing leader came down the road in Orlando, with a December 2021 loss to Clemson in the Cheez-It Bowl.

Purdy remains second in the NFL with a 105.4 passer rating, behind the Dolphins’ Tua Tagovailoa (106.4).

Left guard Aaron Banks (toe) did not at practice and figures to miss only his second game since becoming a starter last season; he missed the 2022 regular-season finale with a knee injury. Banks’ big-toe injury happened while he successfully anchored in to stop a bull rush, late in the third quarter of the 49ers’ 31-17 loss to Cincinnati on Oct. 29.

Banks is not facing surgery and does not have to wear an orthopedic-support boot while simply letting it heal. He does not foresee it as a long-term issue, but there is no set timeline on recovery. Jon Feliciano practiced in Banks’ place Monday.

Wide receiver Deebo Samuel practiced for the first time since sustaining a hairline fracture in his left shoulder Oct. 15 at Cleveland.

Other 49ers who practiced after recent health issues were: running back Christian McCaffrey (oblique) and linebacker Dre Greenlaw (shoulder). Left tackle Trent Williams (ankle) was on hand but not participating in practice, nor were defensive tackle Javon Kinlaw and wide receiver Ray-Ray McCloud.

The 49ers activated the practice windows for three players on injury lists: rookie cornerback Darrell Luter Jr. (knee), cornerback Sam Womack (knee) and Beal Jr. (hamstring).

“This is the first injury honestly I’ve ever had, so it was just like, a lot of emotion of fighting through the emotion,” said Womack, who went on injured reserve after spraining his knee in Week 2 practice. “A lot of patience waiting for (this) time to come. It was a lot of excitement (to practice), and I’m just hungry to get back out there.”

Luter, a fifth-round pick like Beal, has been out since spring workouts, when he apparently sustained a tibia fracture. “When you’re out and hurt, you get to observe and look at a lot of things you don’t see when you’re out on the field doing things physically with your body,” Luter said. “Mentally, the game has come a lot easier for me. It’s helped me understand it, to break it down a lot smoother before.”

Even after the bye week, Christian McCaffrey has maintained a wire-to-wire lead atop the NFL rushing charts. His 452 yards (4.8-yard average, nine touchdowns) remain ahead of the Colts’ Zack Moss (615 yards), the Eagles’ D’Andre Swift (614) and the Dolphins’ Raheem Mostert (605).

Here are notable stats on where the 49ers and Jaguars rank in the NFL:

Offense:

Jaguars — No. 10 in scoring (24.1 points per game), No. 15 in total yards per game (341.4), No. 26 in red-zone efficiency (47.6 percent).

49ers — No. 4 in scoring (27.3), No. 4 in yards (376.6), No. 5 in rushing (133.5), No. 9 in passing (243.1).

Defense:

Jaguars — No. 9 in scoring (19.5), No. 23 in total yards (342.8), No. 3 in rushing (79.3), No. 30 in passing (263.5)

49ers — No. 4 in scoring (17.5), No. 9 in yards (315), No. 5 in rushing (86.1) and No. 19 in passing (228.9)
