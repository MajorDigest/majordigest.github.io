NCS football playoffs: California stays cool under pressure to beat Clayton Valley: “Everyone took it personally”

SAN RAMON – Trailing by one point with 3:28 left in the game, California quarterback Jayden Macedo was calm and collected before he led the game-winning drive in the Grizzlies’ 31-24 victory over Clayton Valley Charter in the first round of the North Coast Section Open/Division I playoffs.

It didn’t hurt that Cal High, seeded as the No. 5 team, was embarking on the drive in front of its home fans. Higher-seeded Clayton Valley Charter was on the road as a punishment for how the team’s 33-24 victory at California ended on Oct. 27.

The Ugly Eagles celebrated excessively on the field after the game finished, which also led to eight CVC players being suspended for last week’s 48-0 loss vs. De La Salle. All eight players were back for the rematch.

“We were not going to let that happen again,” Macedo said.

“Everyone took it personally,” said Cal tight end Chase McGill, who caught five passes for 126 yards and one touchdown. “We had a hard week of practice, and we came out fighting.”

Macedo captained a surgical eight-play drive that lasted just under two minutes, big-time throws to Chase McGill (22 yards) and Nick Fox (34 yards) getting the ball down to the nine-yard line. 

Once there, Macedo rolled right, cut the ball back into the middle of the field, and stretched across the goal-line for the winning score with 1:29 left in the game.

And just for good measure, California’s do-it-all quarterback caught the two-point conversion against his familiar opponent from the East Bay Athletic League. 

“He played well tonight, and he had to,” Cal High coach Danny Calcagno said. “He did it with his arm and his legs. I’m proud of him.”

Cal’s defense held on the next drive, and the San Ramon school advanced to a semifinal matchup with Pittsburg, the same program that beat Cal 38-35 in the regular season.

“We wanted that rematch,” Macedo said after he threw for 303 yards, rushed for 32 and accounted for three total touchdowns. “We told them we would see them in the playoffs, and now it’s time.”

While Friday’s game was as intense and physical as one would expect, the handshake line came and went without incident after Cal snapped a four-game losing streak to the Ugly Eagles.

“Hats off to Cal High, and they’re a hell of a football team,” CVC coach Nick Tisa said. “We all shook hands after the game, so I think that whole thing is over.”

Sayyidi Abdul-Kareem led all players with 98 rushing yards, and Cal’s wideout duo of Fox and Josh Calcagno combined for 168 receiving yards. Clayton Valley’s rushing attack netted 112 yards, and tight end Tony Keck caught seven passes for 138 yards and one touchdown. 

Abdul-Kareem, starting for the injured Devan Love, ran for a 31-yard touchdown late in the third quarter to tie the game at 17-17. Cal took advantage of its next opportunity after defensive lineman Ethan Cullen and the defensive line forced CVC to go three-and-out on the following drive.

Josh Calcagno found the soft spot in the zone on the right sideline, and he took Macedo’s perfect pass 75 yards for a touchdown and a 23-17 Cal lead with 11:01 left to play.

“Our philosophy is to just keep playing the game,” Cal coach Calcagno said. “Don’t worry about the numbers. Just do what you’re coached to do and make plays.”

After being sluggish for the last quarter, the CVC offense woke up with its season on the line.

Justin Underwood, who also had a key 16-yard run on fourth down during the drive, gave the Ugly Eagles a 24-23 lead with a four-yard rumble into the endzone with six minutes left. 

California’s next drive ended when Clayton Valley tipped a field goal attempt, but provided an opportunity to close things out, the Ugly Eagles went three-and-out to give Macedo enough time to win the game. 

It was a result that seemed unlikely midway through the second quarter.

After a slow start, in which teams exchanged two short field goals, things picked up in the middle of the second quarter. Dion Lew Beverly returned a kickoff for a touchdown, Kameel Colvin intercepted a pass, and Tony Keck broke free for the 57-yard touchdown, all within the span of a minute, to give CVC a 17-3 early in the second quarter.

Those shots weren’t enough to knock out Cal High though, and the Grizzlies responded with a seven-play, 70-yard drive punctuated by Macedo throwing the ball to McGill for a 39-yard touchdown. Back-to-back sacks on CVC’s ensuing drive sent the teams to halftime with the visitors leading 17-10, setting the table for an intense second half that ended with Cal High defeating the team that ended its season last year in the NCS Open/Division I playoffs.

The quarterback had payback on his mind after the game, but his coach was just considered himself fortunate to be able to game plan for another Friday night after surviving the heart-stopping victory.

“I don’t even know who we’re playing next,” Calcagno said.  I’m just happy we’re going to be playing next week.”
