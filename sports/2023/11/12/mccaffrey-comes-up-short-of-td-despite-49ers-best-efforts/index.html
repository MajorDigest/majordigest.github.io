McCaffrey comes up short of TD despite 49ers’ best efforts

The 49ers went to great lengths to get Christian McCaffrey into the endzone Sunday, only to come up short.

His consecutive-games streak of scoring a touchdown ended at 17, leaving him in a tie for the NFL record with Lenny Moore, who scored in 17 consecutive games for the Baltimore Colts in 1963-64.

It was about the only thing that went wrong in a 34-3 win by the 49ers that snapped a three-game losing streak against a Jacksonville team that had won five straight.

“Yeah, I suck. Everyone else on the team scored except for me,” McCaffrey said at the podium with a wry smile. “No, means a lot to me to keep me in at the end of the game there and try and get that record, but I’ll take a huge win.”

The 49ers wanted the record for McCaffrey so badly that coach Kyle Shanahan put him back in the game with a 31-point lead in the fourth quarter. Not only that, he twice called pass plays inside the Jacksonville 10, including once on fourth down.

“I mean, 18 games in a row is a big record,” Shanahan said. “If we had a chance I was going to try and do it for him. It’s nerve-wracking – last thing I want to do is get him hurt . . . . We got close, wish we could have done it for him, but I think that’s a big accomplishment to do it for 17 games straight.”

Shanahan said Jacksonville coach Doug Pederson acknowledged the situation and seemed OK with it.

“He was very well aware, said they were talking about it,” Shanahan said. “Hopefully it didn’t offend him too much.”

McCaffrey’s day appeared to be over when the 49ers got the ball on their own 31 with 11:46 to play, leading 34-3. Elijah Mitchell was in the game for McCaffrey, and the 49ers handed him the ball seven times in eight plays. But on 4th-and-5 at the Jacksonville 29, Shanahan sent McCaffrey back into the game. Running from the shotgun, McCaffrey went 19 yards to the Jacksonville 10.

Another run got him to the 9. A 6-yard pass from Sam Darnold, and they were down to the 3. A third-down run was stuffed, back to the 5. On fourth down. Darnold’s pass to McCaffrey at the right pylon was broken up by Jacksonville cornerback Darious Williams.

The 49ers were in position to get McCaffrey in the end zone on their previous possession as well, but it ended with a 22-yard touchdown pass from Brock Purdy to fullback Kyle Juszczyk.

“I sort of felt bad, like, `Oh, man,’ ” Purdy said. “I was mad, sort of. To get a touchdown is the biggest thing, and so on the next drive, it was like, let’s drive for Christian and get him a touchdown. Every guy on this team wanted it for Christian. At the same time, he’s got a long career and I told him we’ll be able to do it again.”

McCaffrey was pleased to see Juszczyk, who does a lot of the dirty work in terms of blocking, get in the end zone.

“Juice really wanted me to score, unless he could score,” McCaffrey said. “No, I’m so happy. Any time Juice can score, I’m probably the second-most excited guy on the field.”

The NFL’s leading rusher with 652 yards coming in, McCaffrey had 95 yards on 16 carries and caught six passes for 47 yards. Over his previous four games, McCaffrey didn’t have more than 54 yards rushing.
