Cal Bears: Will Jaydn Ott become a Big Game hero?

Cal running back Jaydn Ott practically apologized to his teammates after a 167-yard rushing performance in the Bears’ 42-39 win over Washington State a week ago.

“Honestly, I’m just disappointed in my two fumbles. That could have been the reason we lost . . . it almost was,” he said. “I’ve just got to be better for my teammates.”

Those miscues aside, the Bears can only hope Ott is as good or better in Saturday’s 126th Big Game at Stanford than he’s been most of this season.

Their star sophomore, who scored two touchdowns against WSU, went over 150 rushing yards for the fourth time this season.  No other Pac-12 back has more than one such outing.

Among every player on either team, Ott is perhaps best positioned to emerge as a Big Game hero in the final meeting between the cross-Bay rivals before they transition from the Pac-12 to the ACC.

He cracked the 1,000-yard barrier for the season last week — the 19th Cal player to do it and the first since 2017. His 112.9 rushing yards per game leads the Pac-12 and ranks eighth nationally.

And with the Bears needing two wins to become bowl eligible, Ott is sure to put his two fumbles in the rear-view mirror.

“We’re just focused on this week and the Big Game, focused on going  1-0,” he said.

Cal (4-6, 2-5) is aiming to make it three wins in a row over the Cardinal (3-7, 2-6) for the first time since taking five straight from 2002-06. Ott totaled 148 yards rushing and receiving with a touchdown in last year’s 27-20 Cal victory.

Bears offensive coordinator Jake Spavital is impressed by the progress Ott has made since his freshman season a year ago when he rushed for 897 yards but got 274 of them in one game against Arizona.

“He’s taken on being the guy, understanding he’s going to have to play a lot more, take a lot more hits this year and have a toughness and an attitude,” Spavital said. “He’s progressively gotten better, being able to hit those home run shots like he did last year but also fighting for those tough yards.”

Ott never carried the ball more than 19 times in a game last year, but he has six games of 20 or more rushes this season, including 29 to produce 165 yards against Arizona State and 27 last week vs. WSU.

Injuries to Cal’s other backs mean Ott has had to carry even more of the load. Backup Isaiah Ifanse has missed the past two games and is questionable for Stanford, and freshman Jaivian Thomas will be out for the second game in a row after injuring his neck at Oregon.

“I knew coming in (to WSU) we were low in the running back room but that’s just the way it goes and I’m ready for it,” he said after the game.

Although it didn’t show, Ott said his legs were “more dead” against the Cougars than they have been all year. That means limiting his activity during the week and spending time with the ice tub, hot tub and massages.

“Really staying on top of my recovery so I can come out and be reliable for my offense and my team,” he said.

Ott’s two-year career rushing total of 1,913 yards is the most by any Cal players through his first 21 games.

He is within 161 yards of Shane Vereen for 10th place on Cal’s all-time single-season chart, and he already has the No. 3 performance by a sophomore. Ahead of him on that list are Jahvid Best (1,580 yards in 2008) and Marshawn Lynch (1,246 yards in 2005).

Both Cal and Stanford have struggled defensively this season and the Cardinal is coming off a 62-17 loss at Oregon State in which the Beavers rushed for 277 yards.

Spavital said Ott is still learning to be a voice on the team but has embraced leadership through his actions.

“You are the guy,” Spavital said of being in that role, “and you’ve got a lot of eyes on you and a lot of people are looking at you to make the play.”
