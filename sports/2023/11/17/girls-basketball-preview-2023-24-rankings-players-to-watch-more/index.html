Girls basketball preview 2023-24: Rankings, players to watch, more

CAN ANYONE IN BAY AREA BEAT ARCHBISHOP MITTY?

No.

WHAT MAKES MITTY SO DOMINANT?

There are talented teams in the East Bay, South Bay, and Peninsula, but no program in the region can match Mitty’s superstar duo of five-star sophomore McKenna Woliczko and UConn-bound Morgan Cheli.  Last year’s team lost one game to a Bay Area opponent, Piedmont, but that was early in the season with Cheli out with a foot injury. The 6-foot-2 point guard is fully healthy now and flanked by Woliczko and Los Gatos transfer Belle Bramer, they form a trio capable of torching anyone in coach Sue Phillips’ high-octane press. Elana Weisman, who averaged 19 points and 11 rebounds as a sophomore at Burlingame before transferring and taking a more supporting role at Mitty in 2022-23, is back for her senior year, too. 

AND THE BAY AREA’S SECOND-BEST TEAM IS …

Crowning Mitty is a no-brainer, but figuring out who the second-best team in the Bay Area is much harder given the number of powerful programs. Oakland Tech’s twin tower lineup of sophomores Terri’A Russell and Jhai Johnson will be a handful inside. Kelly Sopak’s Carondelet team will be well-coached and is led by Bramer’s future Lehigh teammate Keshia Vitalicio. San Ramon Valley returns Sofia Bowes and Sierra Chambers, and few teams will boast a backcourt as explosive as Bishop O’Dowd’s trio of Devin Cosgriff, Savannah Jones and Lizzy Quinteros. And don’t count out Steven Pezzola’s perennially competitive Salesian squad. All talented teams with future college athletes. But at the end of the season, seeing anyone but the Monarchs representing NorCal in the state Open Division title game would be shocking. 

SEASON STORYLINES

Oakland Tech has won Division IV, III and I state titles in the past three full seasons, and coach LeRoy Hurt ended last season declaring that he thought his team had shown enough to be placed in the Open Division, the highest playoff bracket. The Bulldogs graduated their veteran backcourt last season, but will the frontcourt of Taliyah Logwood, Russell and Johnson make an inexperienced backcourt irrelevant? … Pinewood struggled with injuries last season (17-12) but rallied to make the CCS Open Division final and the NorCal Division I quarterfinals. Now healthy, a team led by guards Jolyn Ding and Alex Facelo appears poised to make another deep run through NorCal. … Piedmont won its first 27 games before bowing out in the NorCal Open Division semifinals 48-45 to Salesian. Twenty points-per-game scorer Natalia Martinez returns for her senior season, and don’t be surprised to see the 5-7 guard average close to 30 points per game as the Highlanders will challenge for another NCS title … Mitty is the class of the West Catholic Athletic League, but Sacred Heart Cathedral is solidly the second-best team in the league behind tandem Reza Po and Inez Gallegos.

ACALANES LOOKS READY TO MAKE A LEAP

If there’s any team that could be considered a “dark horse” candidate poised to make the jump from good to great in NorCal, it’s Acalanes, which went 21-8 and lost to Pinewood in the first round of NorCal Division I last season. Any championship team needs a great coach, and Margaret Gartner won 10 NCS titles and the 2004 Division II state title during a dominant run at Carondelet. The Dons also boast a trio of juniors who made last season’s all-BANG team. Sophie Chinn, Karyss Lacanlale and Dulcinea Vail, who led Acalanes to a 12-0 record in the Diablo Athletic League, will play powerhouse St. Mary’s-Stockton in non-league competition. The Lafayette school has all the ingredients to be a Top 3 team at the end of the season. 

PLAYERS TO WATCH

Gabrielle Abigor, Berean Christian, 6-3, senior

Diablo Athletic League Valley Division co-MVP led the team to a 22-5 record and a league title. Has a scholarship offer from Cal.  

Ryka Aprameyan, Evergreen Valley, 5-9, senior

BVAL Mt. Hamilton co-MVP averaged 30 points and 5.5 steals per game for the CCS Division I champions. The team captain scored 31 in the section final against Los Altos. 

Sofia Bowes, San Ramon Valley, 5-9, senior

First-team all-EBAL selection averaged 13 points, 11 rebounds, six assists and 2.3 steals per game for the league champions. Played on Poland’s U-16 team in the summer. 

Morgan Cheli, Archbishop Mitty, 6-2, senior

One of the top players in the nation. UConn commit averaged 15.6 points, 5.2 rebounds and 3.5 assists in 14 games. Had 24 points, six rebounds, five assists and four steals in state title game.

Quinn Godfrey, Branham, 6-4, senior

BVAL Mt. Hamilton co-MVP averaged 15.9 points, 10 rebounds and 3.5 blocks per game for the CCS Open Division participants. Had seven blocks in a regular season game against eventual CCS Division I champion Evergreen Valley. 

Jhai Johnson, Oakland Tech, 6-3, sophomore

Burst onto the scene as a freshman, anchored the Bulldogs as a rebounder and shot-blocker in the middle for Division I state champions. 

Savannah Jones, Bishop O’Dowd, 5-4, senior

First-team all-WACC Foothill Division selection averaged 14 points, six assists and three steals for the NCS Division II champion. Helped O’Dowd make it to the NorCal Division I quarterfinals.

Madalyn Kanazawa, Salesian, 5-4, senior

Standout point guard had an impact that went far beyond the 6.1 points, 2.6 assists and 1.8 steals per game she accrued on the stat sheet for the NorCal Open Division finalist. Scored 14 points in a NorCal quarterfinal at Folsom. 

Karyss Lacanlale, Acalanes, 5-4, junior

Diablo Athletic League Foothill Division co-MVP helped lead Acalanes to a 21-8 record and a NorCal Division I finals appearance. 

Natalia Martinez, Piedmont, 5-7, senior

West Alameda County Conference Foothill Division player of the year averaged 20 points, five rebounds, four assists and four steals per game for the NorCal Open Division semifinalists. Was one of the main reasons Piedmont went 24-0 in the regular season. 

Reza Po, Sacred Heart Cathedral, 5-3, senior

The first-team all-WCAL point guard led Sacred Heart Cathedral to a 21-6 record and an 8-2 mark in league play. Had eight points, six rebounds and four assists in a victory over rival St. Ignatius in January. 

Keshia Vitalicio, Carondelet, 5-9, senior

First-team all-EBAL selection averaged 10 points and six assists per game for NorCal Division I quarterfinalists. Helped Carondelet beat host ‘Iolani in the Iolani Classic in December. 

McKenna Woliczko, Archbishop Mitty, 6-2, sophomore

West Catholic Athletic League player of the year averaged 20.2 points and 10.3 rebounds per game for the NorCal Open Division champions. First freshman in Mitty’s storied history to average double digits in points and rebounds.
