49ers report card: Purdy’s A+ passer rating helps key win over Buccaneers

SANTA CLARA – Here is how the 49ers (7-3) graded in Sunday’s 27-14 win over the visiting Tampa Bay Buccaneers (4-6):

Brock posted the first perfect passer rating by a 49ers quarterback since Joe and Steve each did so in 1989 (Disclaimer: last names no longer required for any of that trio, each of whom “threw a perfect game” with three touchdowns and no interceptions). Purdy’s 333 yards came with touchdown passes to Christian McCaffrey (4 yards), Brandon Aiyuk (76 yards) and George Kittle (3 yards). Purdy’s first pass went to Kittle for 24 yards, and he hit him again for a 21-yarder from the Niners’ own 3-yard line to set up Aiyuk’s deep shot on the next snap. Purdy’s trust in Kittle (89 yards, eight receptions on nine targets) was evident and extremely helpful on shots over the middle or perfectly executed bootlegs. Aiyuk finished with a career-high 156 yards, and the 76-yard touchdown marked a career-long for both him and Purdy. Purdy was sacked four times, so that contributed to his “not content” review of his outing.

This was the 49ers’ first game without a rushing touchdown since last Dec. 4 against Miami, ending a streak of 17 straight games (playoffs included). McCaffrey had 21 carries for 78 yards to maintain his NFL rushing lead (825 yards; 134 more than Dolphins starter and ex-49ers star Raheem Mostert). His longest run, 12 yards, opened the 49ers’ final touchdown drive. McCaffrey was admirably steamed he got stopped for no gain on fourth-and-1 with 2:13 to go. Deebo Samuel’s lone carry resulted in a 1-yard loss on the 49ers’ first snap. Elijah Mitchell had 24 yards on four carries, all of which came on a second-quarter series.

Arguably the defense’s biggest play came when rookie Ji’Ayir Brown dove for an interception in the end zone with 3 ½ minutes left to snuff the Bucs’ comeback bid. After Talanoa Hufanga sustained what the 49ers fear is an anterior cruciate ligament tear (on a play in which he was juked in the open field), Brown was summoned for the most significant snaps of his career, and after allowing a 41-yard catch on an eventual touchdown drive, he rallied to break up two would-be touchdown passes before his interception. Another marquee moment came in the second quarter when Nick Bosa chased and hit Baker Mayfield to split a sack with Fred Warner, who jarred free the ball that Clelin Ferrell recovered. Bosa also split a late sack with Dre Greenlaw, and one sack apiece came from Chase Young and Arik Armstead. Mayfield (29-of-45, 246 yards) threw his lone touchdown pass to Mike Evans against Ambry Thomas’ coverage. The 49ers’ 11 pass breakups were their most since 2016.

The Bucs weren’t expected to run much, and they managed 66 yards on 18 carries, one of which was a 17-yard end-around by Deven Thompkins on a play in which Mayfield “blocked” Bosa and flex afterward in celebration. Said Bosa: “That was not him. That was the speed of No. 10. But I’ll let him have that. He was excited about it, I guess.” Two plays later, Bosa and Warner created the fumble-forcing sack. Warner, on his 27th birthday, also had a team-high 12 tackles, and Greenlaw had eight.

The 49ers allowed a season-long 51-yard punt return to the 13-yard line before Dee Winters prevented a touchdown in the closing minutes. Jake Moody made all his kicks (field goals from 37 and 28 yards; three point-after attempts). Nothing special from the returns by Ray-Ray McCloud and Jordan Mason. George Odum drew a fair-catch interference penalty when his legs got tangled with the Bucs’ returner on the game’s opening series, but the 49ers’ defense forced an ensuing 3-and-out.

The 49ers seemed in control from the outset, although a 13-7 halftime lead didn’t reflect that. Still, the 49ers were not caught looking ahead to the coming three-game swing (at Seattle on Thursday; at Philadelphia on Dec. 3; vs. Seattle on Dec. 10). Defensive coordinator Steve Wilks is 2-0 on the sideline, and while he’ll disregard that attention, it’s worth noting the 49ers have held five of their 10 opponents under 15 points.

Source: Cam Inman, The Mercury News
