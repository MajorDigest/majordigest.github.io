Bay Area high school football 2023: CCS championship weekend preview

For the first time since 2010, just one team from the West Catholic Athletic League reached a Central Coast Section final — and that team won’t play this weekend.

Serra captured its championship last week, routing Wilcox for the Open Division title.

Under the section’s new format, Wilcox, as the Open runner-up, moved into the CCS’s Division I final on Friday night against league-rival Los Gatos at San Jose City College.

Five championships will be decided this weekend in the CCS, four involving teams from the Bay Area News Group’s coverage area.

We preview those games here:

CCS Division I: No. 4 Los Gatos vs. No. 7 Wilcox

Records: Los Gatos 10-2; Wilcox 8-4

Kickoff: Friday at San Jose City College, 7 p.m.

Series history: The teams have met 22 times in the MaxPreps era (2004 to present), with Los Gatos leading 16-5-1. Los Gatos has won the past four meetings. 

Storylines: Los Gatos won the regular season matchup between the teams 24-21 after senior running back Boxer Kopcsak-Yeung scored on a last-minute touchdown run to clinch the PAL Bay Division victory. … In the playoffs, Los Gatos defeated Archbishop Riordan 28-14 in the first round and held on for the 21-17 win at St. Ignatius to advance to the program’s first section championship game since 2019 … Los Gatos has won 15 section titles. … Wilcox stunned St. Francis 52-28 in the first round before losing 63-27 to top-seeded Serra in the Open Division title game last week. … As the Open runner-up, Wilcox slid into the Division I title game. … The Chargers won the CCS Division II title in 2021 when they defeated Menlo School 54-20. … Wilcox quarterback Tyson Bonilla ran for a team-high 1,359 yards and 14 touchdowns, but can also make defenses pay through the air. He has passed for 801 yards and six touchdowns. … Wilcox’s Elijah Walker, who scored five touchdowns in the victory over St. Francis, ran for 928 yards and 12 touchdowns in nine games … Linebacker Jeramiah Lewis has led the Wilcox defense with 107 tackles. … Los Gatos coach Mark Krail, a creative play-caller, had his team go for it on fourth down twice in the second half against St. Ignatius last week. … Wilcox averages over 300 rushing yards per game. Los Gatos has rushed for 1,876 yards and passed for 2,030 on the season. … After waiting three years to start for Los Gatos, senior A.J Minyard has thrown for 16 touchdowns, with his favorite target Jaylen Thomas catching 10 TDs. … Kopcsak-Yeung carries a heavy load for Los Gatos, rushing for 1,391 yards and 21 touchdowns this season. 

Computer projection: Calpreps.com predicts Los Gatos to win 28-20

– Joseph Dycus

CCS Division II: No. 8 Christopher vs. No. 2 Soquel

Records: Christopher 11-1; Soquel 10-2

Kickoff: Saturday at San Jose City College, 7 p.m.

Series history: First meeting between the schools.

Storylines: Christopher has had a wild ride the past three weeks, a rollercoaster that began when the Cougars blew a 21-0 lead at home and lost to Live Oak 28-24 in a game that decided the BVAL Mt. Hamilton championship. … Since then, the Cougars have been on the fun side of comebacks as they rallied from nine points behind in the last half of the fourth quarter to beat top-seeded Archbishop Mitty 33-28 and from 17 down in the second half on Saturday to stun fifth-seeded Menlo School 21-17. … QB Jaxen Robinson, a 6-foot-5, 220-pound junior, led the rally against Menlo, throwing a 79-yard touchdown pass to Amari Bluford and a 24-yard TD pass to Evan Vernon before scoring on a 35-yard run with 4:52 left to complete the comeback. … Christopher coach Darren Yafai is stepping down after the season. He has led the varsity team for three years. … This is Christopher’s second appearance in a CCS title game under Yafai. The Cougars lost to Sacred Heart Cathedral 27-3 in the Division III final in 2021. … Christopher won a CCS Division IV title in 2017, beating Leland 24-7 in the final … Soquel is making its third trip to a CCS final, its first since 1987. The program is 0-2 in those games. … The Knights have been on a steady ascent under former San Jose State and NFL defensive back Dwight Lowery, posting back-to-back 10-win seasons … They finished second to Salinas in the PCL’s Gabilan Division and have beaten Carmel 34-14 and Monterey 22-0 in the first two playoff rounds. … Sophomore QB Sam Whelan is completing more than 65% of his passes and Cruz Ferris leads the Soquel rushing attack.

Computer projection: Calpreps.com says Soquel, 21-12

– Darren Sabedra

CCS Division IV: No. 1 Palo Alto vs. No. 2 Mountain View

Records: Palo Alto 7-5; Mountain View 6-6

Kickoff: Saturday at San Jose City College, 1 p.m.

Series history: Palo Alto has won 15 of 16 matchups in the MaxPreps era (2004 to present), with the only Mountain View win being a 38-21 victory in the spring 2021 season.

Storylines: Palo Alto is back in a CCS title game after winning Division V last season … Mountain View is making its first title game appearance since 1995… Palo Alto has scored at least 30 points in each of its past four games, all victories, breaking out of a slump in which the Vikings were shut out in three straight games … Palo Alto defeated Seaside 55-21 and Leigh 45-14 to reach the title game. … Paly quarterback Declan Packer has thrown 21 touchdowns and just two interceptions. … Stopping Palo Alto receiver Jason Auzenne, who averages 19.8 yards per reception and has pulled in 12 touchdowns, will be a top priority for Mountain View. … Mountain View has played this season in honor of Lucinda Daniels, a team mom who died shortly after the season opener. … Mountain View went winless in PAL Bay Division play before getting a second wind in the Division IV playoffs. … The Spartans beat Burlingame 49-21 and Branham 38-34 to reach the title game … Mountain View’s offensive attack is spearheaded by senior running back Arturo Hernandez, who rushed for 271 yards and four touchdowns against Branham. … Spartans QB Kevin Conway has thrown three TD passes to Lex Silver in the playoffs.

Computer projection: Calpreps.com predicts Palo Alto will win 31-28.

– Joseph Dycus

CCS Division V:  No. 4 Santa Teresa vs. No. 3 South San Francisco 

Records: Santa Teresa 6-6; South San Francisco 11-1

Kickoff: Saturday at MacDonald High School in San Jose, 7 p.m.

Series history: The first meeting for these teams in the MaxPreps era that began in 2004.  

Storylines: South San Francisco is the last “C” league team standing, an automatic qualifier after winning the PAL Lake with a 7-0 record while going 9-1 overall. In the first two rounds of the playoffs, the Warriors defeated Silver Creek and Leland, “B” and “A” league teams, respectively. … Santa Teresa, also an “A” league team, is looking for its second straight CCS crown. The Saints captured the D-IV championship last year by beating Branham. Santa Teresa knocked out Los Altos and No. 1 seed Woodside to reach Saturday’s title match. … SSF is a stout defensive team that has yielded only 73 points this season. The Warriors pitched shutouts in four of their last six victories, not counting a forfeit win over Saratoga, which goes as 2-0. … The Warriors are a running team, led by junior Elijah Fields. A first-team all-leaguer last year, he has scored 21 touchdowns thus far… Another standout for SSF is fellow junior WR/DB Darren Miller, who was all-league on offense and defense last season. Also playing a big role on defense is MLB Darius “Ryder” DeGuzman-DeAsis, who averages about nine tackles per game. … Joseph Tapia might be the most indispensable Saint. He plays quarterback, safety and wide receiver. In Santa Teresa’s semifinal win over Woodside, he set up the winning TD with a 65-yard run, caught a 45-yard touchdown pass from QB Brenton Gaches and had a pick-6. “He’s our everything,” Saints coach Steve Papin told the Bay Area News Group’s Glenn Reeves after the win. … South San Francisco will be seeking its third CCS title, and first since 1989, when it beat Carlmont for the D-II North championship. The Warriors were 11-0 that season. They also won in 1980, capturing the Continental Division with a 17-7 win over North Monterey County. Mike Tenerowicz coached both teams. … Santa Teresa’s title last year was its first-ever in football. The Saints did reach the DI-4A finals in 1987, losing to Live Oak 38-15.

Computer projection: Calpreps.com is picking Santa Teresa 17-14.

– Mike Lefkow
