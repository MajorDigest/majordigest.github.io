Clovis North seeking first state win in school history

CONCORD, Calif. - The Clovis North football program is looking to win its first state playoff game in school history tonight, but in order to do that, they will have to go through one of the most storied programs in the state, De La Salle.

From 1992 to 2004, the Spartans, a private Catholic high school in Concord, won a national record 151 straight games in a row, and just recently captured their 31st consecutive North Coast Section title.

At 10-2 this season, they have been good this season, but also appear somewhat beatable this year, having suffered a couple losses early in the season, before stringing together 10 straight wins.

And the 13-0 Broncos, fresh off winning their first Central Section Division I crown since 2012, have been tremendously impressive, actually coming into the game ranked significantly higher than De La Salle by Calprep.com, the computer rankings used by the Central Section.

So suffice it to say, this a "big" chance for Clovis North and the Central Section, to make a "big" statement about the type of football being played in the Central Valley.

“It’d mean everything," said Clovis North senior quarterback Mario Cosma, about what it would mean to win on the road at De La Salle. "I mean, it (would) just show the Central Valley is really underrated, the talent we have here, the players we have here. Any time we play a Southern Section team, everyone always predicts us to lose, so I think it would just be such a big opportunity for the Section, just really put us on the map."    

The Broncos, ranked sixth in the state by Calpreps.com, already showed they are more than capable of playing with the best in the state earlier this season, when they took down Southern Section power, JSerra out of San Juan Capistrano, 23-7 in Clovis back in September. The Lions are currently ranked tenth by Calpreps.

A win by the Broncos would advance them to next week's Division 1-AA state championship game, and move them one step away from joining the list of previous state champions from our viewing area, since the CIF started playing state title games in 2006.

Here is the list of previous high school football state champions from our viewing area.

2019 DIVISION 1-AA: Central
2018 DIVISION 6–AA: Hilmar
2017 DIVISION 6–AA: Strathmore
2015 DIVISION IV–AA: Hanford
2011 Division III: Washington Union

Of course, even though a first-ever state title is the ultimate goal for the Broncos, they will also be handing out some hardware to the Clovis North-De La Salle winner, as the game is also for the Division I-AA Northern California Regional title, and winning a regional title would also be a first for the Broncos program as well.

Division I-AA is the second-highest state division, behind only the two-team Open Division, and if the Broncos find a way to win the state title, they would join Central in 2019, as the only teams from our viewing area and the Central Section to ever win a state title in the second-highest division.

"I think the one thing you’ve heard all our players talk about in all the postgame interviews that they’ve had, there really wasn’t a lot of talk about Section Championship from our guys, it was about the state title, the state title," said Clovis North head coach Mike Jacot. "Darious Storey said it at the end of the (Section championship) game, yeah I’m excited we’re the Section-title champs, that means a lot for our program, but we’re trying to win the state championship. So I know our players are fired up, our coaching staff, and we’re ecstatic that we get an opportunity to try and do this thing against a storied program like De La Salle.” 

This matchup is one of 27 Regional Championship games (state semifinals) taking place around the state over the next two nights, and there are also four other teams from our viewing area who will be in action, with three of them hosting games.

Regional Championship Games (involving teams from our viewing area)

North: Division 1-AA: Clovis North at De La Salle, Friday at 7:30 p.m.
South: Division 2-A: Simi Valley at Central Valley Christian, Saturday at 6:00 p.m.
South: Division 4-AA: Matilda Torres at Jurupa Hills, Friday at 7:30 p.m.
South: Division 4-A: Rio Hondo Prep vs. Mission Oak at Tulare Union HS, Saturday at 6:00 p.m.
North: Division 7-A: Lincoln (San Francisco) at Strathmore, Saturday at 6:00 p.m.
