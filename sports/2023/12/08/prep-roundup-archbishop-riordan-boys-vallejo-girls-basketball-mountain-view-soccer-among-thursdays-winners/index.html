Prep roundup: Archbishop Riordan boys, Vallejo girls basketball, Mountain View soccer among Thursday’s winners

Boys basketball

No. 4 Archbishop Riordan 70, No. 3 San Ramon Valley 59

Andrew Younghill scored 20 of his 25 points in the second half as he helped Riordan — ranked fourth by the Bay Area News Group — rally past an early deficit to defeat third-ranked San Ramon Valley at the Gridley Invitational. Zion Sensley provided 16 points and Jasir Rencher was a threat on the wing and scored 12 for Riordan. 

San Ramon Valley got 25 points from junior guard Luke Isaak, and senior big Seamus Deely scored 11 points to go along with eight rebounds and seven assists. 

No. 2 Salesian 80, Gridley 30

Salesian steamrolled the home team at the Gridley Invitational on Thursday night, with six different players for the Pride scoring at least eight points. 

Aaron Claytor led Salesian with 13 points and seven assists, and sophomore shooting guard Elias Obenyah put in 10 points. 

Alvin Loving IV scored nine, and Zander Jimenez, Deundrae Perteet and Zion Yeargin all scored eight points for 4-0 Salesian. 

Branson 66, No. 6 Clayton Valley Charter 42

Jase Butler scored 21 points and had five assists as Branson used an 18-0 third-quarter run to pull away from Clayton Valley Charter (2-1). Vince Ellis and James Moore each scored nine points for CVC at Gridley.

Los Gatos 50, Menlo School 46

Nolan Koch scored 21 points and junior Osha Moloney had 14 points and six rebounds as the Wildcats held off Menlo School. Anthony Martin also scored six points and dished out a team-high three assists for Los Gatos.  

Girls basketball

Vallejo 71, Hercules 15

Jazmine Cardwell made 13 of 16 shots and scored 29 points in Vallejo’s dominant victory over Hercules. She also grabbed a team-high 15 rebounds. Veekie Head also had a 17-point, 17-rebound double-double, and K’mirah Thornton scored 10 points and had seven rebounds. Sophomore Taliyah Davis did not score a point but made a big impact on the game by grabbing 10 rebounds. 

Menlo School 56, Terra Nova 30

Menlo School outscored Terra Nova 21-0 in the third quarter and rolled to an easy victory. Ruiqi Liu and Arthi Abhyanker each scored 15 points, and Karen Xin also put in 12 points for the Knights. 

Girls soccer

Mountain View 5, Menlo School 1

Mountain View senior Hailey Wescott scored twice and had one assist as the Spartans easily dispatched Menlo School. Karena Shah scored once, and Juno Winegar scored and assisted one time apiece. 

Archbishop Mitty 1, Sequoia 0

Jordan Geis helped Mitty improve to 2-0 on the season when she scored the game’s only goal against Sequoia. 

Homestead 3, Burlingame 1

Jamie Nguyen didn’t need to score to put her stamp on the game, assisting on all three Homestead goals. Jules Hu scored once, and Annika Wickham converted on two Nguyen passes to score a brace. 

Boys soccer

Santa Clara 7, Evergreen Valley 1

Maxwell Nguyen scored two goals and had two assists for Santa Clara. Kyle Campbell, Kai Pham, Angel Hernandez, Santiago Martinez and Alaix Busette all scored one goal each. Lucas Nguyen also had two assists. 

St. Francis 2, Palo Alto 0

Lukas Ramans and David Hernandez Escobar scored one goal apiece as St. Francis put away Palo Alto. Seniors Jake Bloyd and Reece Wyatt also had an assist each. 

Bishop O’Dowd 8, Encinal 0

Jayden Fitzgerald notched a hattrick – three goals – in Bishop O’Dowd’s rout over Encinal. Walker Blake scored twice, and Ian Nasif, Nolan Rittenberg and Theo Weinmann each scored once for the Dragons.
