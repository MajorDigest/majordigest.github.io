Justin Fields reflects on his Bears legacy, fans, fashion, pre-game music and more

CHICAGO — As Justin Fields prepares for what could be his final three games with the Bears, he spoke with Kaitlin Sharkey about a variety of topics in a one-on-one interview.

You can read a transcript of the interview below or watch the full interview in the player above.

Sharkey: Justin, I think back to that first interview we did the day after you were drafted here in Chicago and I think about now you in the final weeks of your third season here with the Bears. When you think about the biggest lesson you've learned about being an NFL starting quarterback, and being a team leader day in and day out, what do you think that lesson has been?

Fields: Really just to expect the highs and lows. Expect outside noise to be there. No matter what, just control and what you can control. Stay focused on what you have going on, what the team has got going on and stuff like that. Block out all the outside noise and just continue to work to have the same mindset no matter what you go through. I think that's the biggest thing I've learned so far in my time being in the league.

Sharkey: You've gone through coaching, changes, personnel changes, all kinds of changes in your short time here. If this was the end of your chapter, or whatever the next chapter is next season, wherever it is - if it's here, if it's somewhere else - what do you want this chapter of your career to say about you, not only as a person, but as a quarterback?

Fields: I think that's the biggest thing is the person. I think just building relationships. All the relationships I've built in the building - teammates in, teammates somewhere else now. I just think the biggest thing I would want to leave, if I wasn't here next year is for everybody to remember the kind of person I was - how I carried myself throughout the building through the good times, through the bad times. Really just that. Because I think that carries farther, much longer than football, you know? It's bigger than football at the end of the day. I just want everybody to remember the kind of person I was, and how I've hopefully had a positive effect on them in their life.

Sharkey: We ask you every week about where you see improvement in your game, and how you grow on the football field. When you look back over the last couple of seasons, is it kind of surreal to see how much you've grown not only as a person, but as a football player?

Fields: It is. As a person, that's crazy. I think I've learned so many lessons. Just grown in so many areas of my life -football too. I've learned so many things. It's been a great journey, so far. Of course, I'm excited for the rest of the season and next year, as well.

Sharkey: We only see you in a certain setting - sometimes it's postgame or during press conferences. It's not always the most joyful experience for us. But when we look at what brings you joy, what brings you passion outside of football - I know how much you speak about your family and your faith and things like that - but what brings Justin Fields joy?

Fields: Little things. Being able to take a walk down Lake Michigan. To be able to just look at nature, like the fall here with all the trees. Really just appreciating the little things in life. Finding joy in those things. The platform that I have, I've got the power to make somebody's day just by saying hello, signing autographs, and stuff like that. Little stuff like that, it's just bigger than football. That's what people are going to remember for their whole life. Nobody's going to remember the score of the second time we played the Green Bay Packers. Nobody's going to remember that. You have to look that up on Google for them that remember that off the top of their head. But, what people are going to remember is the interaction between me and them. I think that's the biggest thing - is the platform that God's given me to have that effect on people's lives.

Sharkey: Do you feel like the city of Chicago has embraced you? Do you feel like you've embraced Chicago given how much you've grown?

Fields: Yeah, I think so. Fans here are great. They're very passionate, kind of like Ohio State. It's that Midwest football thing. I've definitely embraced the city. The fans, like I said before, they're awesome. They're amazing. Of course, they get on you when you're not doing the best, but you wouldn't want it any other way. I just try to twist it around - they care. The city of Chicago is great. Probably the best city in the world or best city in the U.S., for sure. I haven't been around the world to compare that but great city. I've tried to do a lot of things around the city in the community to make a positive change. I've definitely embraced the city and the people here have definitely embraced me.

Sharkey: For most NFL quarterbacks, the outside noise doesn't really go away. Are you a guy that keeps receipts and takes note of the outside noise? Are you internally motivated?

Fields: I don't keep receipts because. at the end of the day, it doesn't affect me. It doesn't change how I'm going to look at things so I don't really keep receipts. People are going to say what they're going to say. At the end of the day, no matter how much you do - Tom Brady is always going to have haters. Patrick Mahomes is always going to have haters. So, no, I don't keep receipts at all.

Sharkey: We've talked about the evolution of your play on the field, but I feel like your fashion has also evolved since your rookie season. Do you work with a stylist?

Fields: I do. Yeah. Her name is Jo. She's from New York. I just started working with her this year, actually. She's been awesome. She cam before the season this year and then mid-season just to kind of get some fits together. What she does is just round up a bunch of clothes, things that she thinks that I might like. She brings them to the house and I just pick and choose what outfits I like. what outfits I'm not feeling and then we just go from there. She's been great.

Sharkey: Why did you want to kind of focus on that because your game day fits are - I can just tell there's a lot of thought that goes into it. Do you have some favorites and why is it important for you to kind of step up the fashion?

Fields: It's important just to feel confident. Like Deion says, 'look good, feel good, play good.' Going out there and just making the day special because I usually - unless I'm going out to eat or something like that - just dress comfortably. You just want to get ready and dress kind of differently to get ready for an event. In college, we're putting on a suit to get ready for game day. But yeah, I stepped up from my rookie year. I don't think I had a stylist whatsoever.

Sharkey: You had some suits rookie year.

Fields: Yeah, I did. Yeah, I did. But, definitely stepped up over the few years, for sure.

Sharkey: Keeping up with the pregame theme - are you a Spotify or Apple Music guy?

Fields: Apple Music.

Sharkey: What are you rocking on Apple Music pregame?

Fields: To be honest, I listen to gospel music before games. I feel like it just calms me. It gives me peace. But every now and then because I just have the same gospel music playing over and over - I mix some Drake in there. But really just gospel music like 75-80% of the time and then mixing some Drake in - some chill R&B. I don't really like to get too pumped up. I know, Jaquan - he loves YoungBoy. That's what he's listening to. I can't get on that way before the game. So, just kind of like chill music, relax.

Sharkey: You talk a lot about your mental health and your breath work and all the things that have helped you stay centered through the ups and downs of being an NFL quarterback. Why has it been so important to you? Do you feel like it's helped you and something that you've added into your game?

Fields: Yeah, I think the biggest thing with playing quarterback is just having a calm mind, a calm body, and really just being calm with so much chaos going on around you. I think that helps you out. It really helps you out with the conditioning part of it too. There are some drives where I might scramble two or three times or might even have a designed run. So, I think it just helps with the conditioning. Kind of gets your lungs and stuff warmed up.

Sharkey: Finally, whatever's next for you - wherever you end up - what do you want Bears fans, football fans to maybe know about Justin Fields, the person and not necessarily the player?

Fields: Wherever I end up - whether it's here or somewhere else next year - just know that I've given it my all. I study till I get tired of studying. Of course, you've got to have those mental breaks. I think I just want everybody to know I've given it my all up to this point. I've done everything I possibly could. At the end of the day, that's really all you can ask for from a teammate, from a coach. If they actually put their best foot forward every day, if they've put in the effort - you really can't ask anything else. I think that's the one thing I'd like to leave with them. I love the fans. I love the city of Chicago. I love everything about it. My time here has been great. It's been amazing.

Source: Kaitlin Sharkey, WGN 9
