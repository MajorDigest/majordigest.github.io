Pass-happy Salesian boys carve up Ygnacio Valley zone as Pride stays perfect

ALBANY – There are two ways to nullify a zone defense in basketball. The first, more popular method, is to shoot the lights out of the gym and force the defenders to creep out to the perimeter. 

The second option, which is far more difficult and requires all five offensive players to be in sync, is to pass the ball into the middle of the court, and then hit another quick pass or two to cutters. 

Salesian went with the latter of the two methods – and made it look easy – in the Pride’s 65-45 victory over Ygnacio Valley at Albany High. Salesian led 32-19 at halftime and although Ygnacio Valley kept things interesting well into the second half, it was never in jeopardy of losing the lead. 

“Tonight, we weren’t shooting the ball great, but we did a really good job of attacking the middle of their zone and going high-low with interior passing,” Salesian coach Bill Mellis said. 

The stat sheet reflected Salesian’s depth, which listed nine players as having scored at least one basket. Usual high-scorers Amani Johnson, Aaron Claytor and Alvin Loving IV took a more supportive role.

They were content to be the floor-spacers on the outside and the passers who stretched Ygnacio Valley to its limit. They were happy to swing the rock to Zander Jimenez, who scored 15, and reserve Zion Yeargin, who was right behind him with 13 points. 

“Our team was way deeper than them, so we knew we had to outrun them,” Jimenez said. “It took a minute for us to do that, but once we found our flow, it was a wrap.”

Ygnacio Valley entered Friday’s game 7-1, with victories against Moreau Catholic and Valley Christian. It was a sign that last year’s 21-7 record and trip to NorCal was no fluke.

To the Wolves’ credit, they kept the deficit to around 10 points until the final few minutes of the third quarter, when an 11-0 Salesian run put the Pride up 51-30 entering the fourth quarter. 

Ygnacio wing Antonio Kellogg scored 27 points on a steady diet of tough fall-aways and step-backs to keep the Wolves in the game. 

Coach Michael Woolridge praised his team’s tenacity, and said this game showed him that his Wolves were not far away from contention. 

“It feels good to be able to play Salesian and be on the same floor with a team like them, who has been a powerhouse for years,” said Woolridge, who expects his team to make noise in NCS Division IV this season. “But you can’t stay in front of that team for too long. They have too many weapons.”

Salesian showed the difference between being a good team in the Diablo Athletic League that can compete in a lower division, and being arguably the best team in NorCal with serious aspirations of winning a state title at the highest level of competition.

Salesian (10-0) walked into the gymnasium with an impeccable resume. Victories over SoCal powers Campbell Hall and Centennial-Corona complemented wins against great NorCal teams Archbishop Mitty and Branson. 

The Pride will be able to add a few more signature victories to its already impressive list when it travels to SoCal and plays in the prestigious Damien tournament. 

“I’m excited for it,” Jimenez said. “I mean, top 50 teams all across the bracket? It’s gonna be crazy.”
