White Sox announce new player development staff under Janish

CHICAGO — The Chicago White Sox have announced their player development staff and assignments for the 2024 season under director of player development Paul Janish Friday.

Justin Jirschele (Class AAA Charlotte), Sergio Santos (Class AA Birmingham), Guillermo Quiroz (Class A Winston-Salem), Patrick Leyland (Class A Kannapolis) and Danny González (Arizona Complex League Sox) will serve as the organization’s managers in 2024.

Jirschele, Quiroz, Leyland and González all return to their managerial posts following the 2023 season.

Santos joins the organization after managing two seasons in the New York Yankees system, which included time with Class A Hudson Valley in 2023. Santos made 194 relief appearances over six major-league seasons with the White Sox (2010-11), Toronto Blue Jays (2012-14), the Los Angeles Dodgers (2015) and the Yankees (2015).

­­“I am extremely excited to work with this talented group of managers, coaches, coordinators and staff that has been assembled for the 2024 season,” Janish said. “Our entire staff has been working all off-season to ensure our players will be ready to hit the ground running for mini camps, spring training and into the regular season. The ultimate goal remains to have our players prepared and ready to contribute at the major-league level, and we believe this staff is well equipped to help all of our players reach his full potential."

Coordinators returning include Doug Sisson (field), Matt Zaleski (pitching), Ryan Newman (infield) and Julio Mosquera (catching). Former White Sox major-league bullpen coach Curt Hasler joins Donnie Veal as assistant pitching coordinators.

Alan Zinter joins Chicago’s system as the new hitting coordinator, and Charlotte has added former major-league infielder and coach Pat Listach as bench coach and former Tampa Bay minor-league coach R.C. Lichtenstein as pitching coach.

Darius Day will make his professional coaching debut as the bench coach with Winston-Salem. Day is a graduate of the White Sox Amateur City Elite (ACE) program and Simeon Career Academy in Chicago, and appeared in 169 minor-league games after being selected by the Texas Rangers in the 23rd round of the 2014 draft.
