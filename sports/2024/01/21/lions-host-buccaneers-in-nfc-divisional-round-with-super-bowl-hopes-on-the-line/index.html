RECAP: Buccaneers fall to Lions 31-23, ending playoff hopes

DETROIT, Mich. (WFLA) -- For the first time in three decades, the Detroit Lions are hosting a postseason matchup at Ford Field after defeating the Rams 24-23 in the Wild Card round.

Now, the Buccaneers are walking into the Lions' den with the hopes of seeking revenge for their defeat back in Week 6, a loss that spoiled the Bucs Creamsicle throwback.

“It’s going to be huge finishing plays up there. It’s going to be loud. We know it’s going to be loud," Bucs' head coach Todd Bowles said about the Lions stadium: Ford Field. "They’re going to be hyped up and they should be. They’re in [the] playoffs. Dan [Campbell] has them playing good football right now, so finishing and not trying to let the crowd get into it too long is going to be important for us.”
