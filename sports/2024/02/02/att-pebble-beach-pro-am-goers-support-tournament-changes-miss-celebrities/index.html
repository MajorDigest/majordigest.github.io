AT&T Pebble Beach Pro-Am goers support tournament changes, miss celebrities

PEBBLE BEACH — Both casual fans and annual tournament goers made their way to Pebble Beach Thursday for the AT&T Pebble Beach Pro-Am adorned in rain gear and umbrellas just in case.

The tournament debuted its new look Thursday, with a higher quality field and no Hollywood celebrities.

This year’s changes made it a bit easier for people to travel around the course and see more of their favorite players and a lot of the feedback from spectators was positive.

“We miss some of the celebrities, especially the ones that showed up every year like Bill Murray and Andy Garcia,” said Chris Molemphy from Portola Valley on the San Francisco Peninsula. “But now we get to see some of the best professionals in the world, so that’s pretty fun.”

For some spectators, they were going to return to the Pro-Am event whether there were going to be celebrities or not, but seeing the changes in full swing gave people a chance to compare the experiences.

Ray Guidi, from Stockton, said he enjoys attending the Pro-Am tournament each year because he’s a fan of the sport, but having celebrities might have kept other friends and family members a bit more entertained.

“I like this format,” said Guidi. “Better golfers are better for the event, but some people are just here for the celebrities. I think having a couple more big names would have kept more people interested.”

The rain came and went (and came back again) but the weather mostly cooperated, as there were no delays in getting the first round in. On Wednesday, spectators were not allowed onto the course during practice rounds due to adverse weather concerns. Thursday was forecast to be another rainy day with the possibility of a thunderstorm, but overall the weather did not affect game play or the spectators’ ability to follow along. Some spectators said even though the weather was chilly, they found other parts of the new tournament formula to be better than last year.

“Last year we had celebrities and really good weather, so it was the best of both worlds,” said Troy Lameson, from Hillsborough on the San Francisco Peninsula . “Some of the good things about this year are that there aren’t long lines, and we get to see a lot more professionals.”

All across the Pebble Beach golf course were volunteers dressed in blue, working through the AT&T Foundation. JR Medina, a 30-year volunteer and chair of the Patriots Outpost Committee, said he enjoyed this year’s format because he had a chance to watch higher quality matches between the professionals.

“We get to watch the best golf in the world,” Medina said. “There is a good international attendance this year too. People are coming from other cities and even outside the country to watch the tournament and event to volunteer.”

Medina’s tent suite was for military veterans, first responders and law enforcement. Other tents provided spaces for people watch up close in a sheltered environment with friends.

“The community keeps me coming back each year,” Medina said. “This year people are overall happy, satisfied and positive.”
