Aggies take over first place after blowout win over Boise State

LOGAN, Utah (ABC4 Sports) - On a night honoring former head coach Stew Morrill, the Utah State basketball team took over first place in the Mountain West Conference with a dominating 80-61 win over Boise State Saturday night.

Mason Falslev scored a season-high 25 points for the Aggies, who bounced back from its first home loss of the season Tuesday against Nevada.

At halftime, the court was named Stew Morrill Court, honoring Utah State's winningest coach in school history with 402 career victories.

“Tremendous win not just for the university and our team, but with coach (Stew) Morrill being honored tonight, all the former players back, and a rivalry game, it means a lot," said USU head coach Danny Sprinkle. "It means a lot to sit in the seat I sit in because I know I'm representing Stew. I know I'm representing every coach that came before him."

Darius Brown II added 18 points, nine rebounds and five assists for the Aggies (20-4, 8-3), who improved to 11-1 at home. Isaac Johnson scored 14, and Great Osobor had 10 points and 10 boards.

The teams began the night tied atop the crowded league standings with No. 25 New Mexico, which lost 80-77 to UNLV at home.

“This game was not about Xs and Os," Sprinkle said. “It’s not about, ‘Hey, we’re doing this against ball screens.’ I just said, ’Play hard. Period.'”

Boise State (16-8, 7-4) was led by Tyson Degenhart with 17 points and eight rebounds. Max Rice scored 12, but the cold-shooting Broncos matched their season low for points.

Falslev finished 10 for 13 from the field and Utah State limited Boise State to 35.6% shooting, including 29% while building a 41-30 halftime lead. Falsev was 6 for 8 in the opening half and scored 15 points, including 11 of his team's first 16.

“(Falslev) was phenomenal,” Sprinkle said. “His mentality was (great) for 40 minutes. I haven’t seen him do it for 40 minutes and he did it for 40 minutes.”

“It feels great," Falslev said. "We watched a lot of film about what happened earlier in the week, and we got better. I knew we were going to get better from the last game, and that’s the goal. The goal is to be going up and not going down toward the end of the year. It was a good game for us.”

Utah State held the Broncos scoreless for five minutes, going on a 13-0 run to open a 23-13 advantage midway through the first half.

Boise State started the second half 6 for 12 from the floor, but nine early points by Brown and two 3-pointers from Johnson kept the Aggies’ lead in double digits.

Utah State shot 60% in the second half.

“For our team, this was a defining game, kind of a defining moment for our team," Sprinkle said. "Tuesday was not who we are, and it wasn't who we've been all year. I don't know if I've challenged a team as hard as we challenged them."

With first place on the line, the Aggies rebounded nicely from successive 14-point losses to San Diego State and Nevada. They reached 20 wins for the 33rd time in school history and 19th in the last 25 seasons.

USU captured its 20th victory of the campaign, sweeping the season series with the Broncos for the first time since the 2010-11 season. 

Utah State next visits Wyoming on Wednesday.
