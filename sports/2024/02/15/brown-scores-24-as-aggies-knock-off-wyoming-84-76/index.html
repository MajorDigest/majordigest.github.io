Brown scores 24 as Aggies knock off Wyoming, 84-76

LOGAN, Utah (ABC4 Sports) - After falling out of the top-25 rankings for the first time in six weeks, the Utah State basketball team is trying to make a case to get back in.

The Aggies had four players score in double-figures, including season-high performances from Darius Brown II and Josh Uduje, as the Aggies rallied for an 84-76 Mountain West win here Wednesday night.

Utah State now has 10 wins away from home this season as it improves to 6-3 in road games and is 4-0 on neutral courts. Those 10 wins away from home are tied with James Madison, Indiana State and Purdue for the most in the nation.

Utah State (21-4, 9-3 MW) trailed by as many as 10 points in the first half, but outscored Wyoming (12-12, 5-6 MW) by 12 points in the second half (45-33) as it posted the eight-point win. USU has now won three-straight and nine of the last 11 games in the series with the Cowboys.

With the victory, Utah State remains in first place in the Mountain West as it holds a one-game lead over both New Mexico and San Diego State.

Brown led Utah State with a season-high 24 points as he was 9-of-12 shooting, including 4-of-6 from 3-point range, and was 2-for-2 at the free throw line. He also had eight rebounds and five assists, with no turnovers in 39 minutes. Uduje added a season-high 20 points for USU off the bench as he was 8-of-14 shooting, including 2-of-5 from 3-point range, and 2-for-2 at the free throw line.

Ian Martinez added 13 points, including a dagger three-point play with just over four minutes to play. Great Osobor finished with 10 points to give him 1,000 in his career.

Brandon Wenzel led Wyoming with 24 points on 8-of-18 shooting, while Sam Griffin scored 18 points and Mason Walters finished with 16 points.

Utah State only led once in the first half at 7-6 following a jumper from Brown. Wyoming controlled most of the half and used a 7-0 run to take a 30-20 lead with 6:04 to play. The Cowboys later led 35-25 at the 3:54 mark, before USU used a 14-8 run to cut the deficit to four points at 43-39 at intermission.

Utah State opened the second half by scoring nine of the first 11 points to take a 48-45 lead. The two teams stayed within one possession of each other for the next 9:18 before Brown hit a layup to give the visitors a 65-60 advantage with 6:31 to play. USU took its largest lead of the game at 76-66 with 58 seconds remaining following a 3-pointer from Martinez and the Aggies went 6-for-6 at the free throw line down the stretch to post the eight-point win.

Utah State finished the game shooting 56.6 percent (30-of-53), including 42.9 percent (9-of-21) from 3-point range, and 71.4 percent (15-of-21) at the free throw line. Wyoming shot 41.8 percent (28-of-67), including 36.0 percent (9-of-25) from behind the arc, and 68.8 percent (11-of-16 at the charity stripe.

It is the 14th time this year that Utah State has shot over 50 percent and the fifth time it has made at least nine 3-pointers.

Wyoming had the advantage on the glass as it outrebounded Utah State 38-32 and UW committed two fewer turnovers in the game (10-8). USU, however, had a 26-10 advantage in bench points and a 12-0 edge in fast break points. The Aggies also had a season-high eight blocks in the contest, while the Cowboys had zero.

The Aggies stay on the road as it plays at Colorado State (19-6, 7-5 MW) on Saturday, Feb. 17, at 3:30 p.m.
