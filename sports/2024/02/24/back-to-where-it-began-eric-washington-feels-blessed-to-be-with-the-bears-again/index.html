Back to where it began: Eric Washington feels blessed to be with the Bears again

LAKE FOREST, Ill. — When new Chicago Bears defensive coordinator Eric Washington stepped up to the mic during his introductory press conference Thursday, he opened with saying it was a blessing to be back with the Bears.

For Washington, it's a full circle moment coming back to Halas Hall and in the near future, Soldier Field. He started his NFL coaching career with Chicago in 2008 and shortly before leaving in 2011, his son, Ellis, was born in Evanston.

And even though Bears head coach Matt Eberflus will maintain control of play-calling duties on defense next season, that wasn't a concern for Washington Thursday, or during the hiring process. He referred to the defense as "our system," a shared entity between him, Eberflus and the rest of the defensive coaching staff, and his position as being one where he will help elevate the group to the next level.

"From my role, you do everything you possibly can to make sure that we perfect our system," Washington said. "And you bring the experiences you've had—that I've had over 16 years in the NFL and even before that in college football, to add something, to inform, to maybe provide a different perspective or some tools and resources.

"That's how I intend to approach it — just to come in and really be a part of what we've established. Bring my perspective and my level of expertise to things I've been fortunate to be a part of in terms of winning situations, explosive defenses."

When asked if there was any reluctance on his part to come to Chicago knowing he wouldn't be calling plays on defense, Washington said he is focused on what the job is, and not on what it isn't.

"The opportunity to come here to this organization with a defense that is clearly on an upward trajectory, that has some really dynamic performers in place with plans of, obviously, augmenting that," Washington said. "That’s what my focus was and what it continues to be."

Washington didn't dodge the question though. He also said he's "pretty confident" he will be a play-caller again at some point, but right now, he's "really confident" in what Eberflus is trying to bring to the table defensively, and will support him to the best of his abilities.

Washington's past experiences with "explosive defenses" tied into why he ultimately chose the Bears job too. Not only did he say he believes in Eberflus's direction coaching the team, but he also said the talent level of the players in the locker room was "explosive" and contributed toward "winning football."

"It’s an explosive group of players. This defense was elite in several important categories that lead directly to winning football," Washington said. "Making teams one dimensional, stopping the run. We’ve got some really good players on the defensive line. This team is in the top two or three in taking the football away.

"When I looked at that and the things of value, those were the things that got me excited, along with the scheme and structure of the defense.”

After his first stint with the Bears, Washington spent time with the Carolina Panthers (2011-19) and Buffalo Bills (2020-23).

Before his first stint with Chicago, Washington served as Northwestern's defensive line coach (2004-07) and as an assistant coach with Ohio University (2001-03).

Washington originally joined the Bills in 2020 as their defensive line coach before he was promoted to be their senior defensive assistant and defensive line coach for the 2022 season.

Buffalo's 2023 defense was top 10 in the NFL across numerous categories, including fourth in sacks (54.0), fourth in points allowed per game (18.3), sixth in opponent gross passing yards per game (216.2), tied-sixth in touchdowns allowed (34), eighth in scrimmage yards allowed per game (326.8) and tied-tenth in tackles for loss (86).

Under Washington in 2022, the Bills finished the season tops in the AFC with 17.8 points allowed per game, and ranked second in the NFL with 286 points allowed overall.

In 2021, Buffalo's defense ranked first in the NFL in points allowed per game (17.0) for the first time in franchise history, and also finished first in yards allowed per game (272.8) and passing yards per game (163.0), which included 19 sacks across a four game stretch that helped fuel a four-game winning streak to finish the regular season.

Prior to arriving in Buffalo, Washington spent nine seasons with the Panthers in various roles. He served as their defensive coordinator from 2018-19 and helped linebacker Luke Kuechly earn two All-Pro selections — a first-team All-Pro season in 2018 and second-team All-Pro season in 2019.  Under Washington in 2019, Carolina's defense ranked second in the NFL in sacks (53).

With Washington leading the defense in 2018, Carolina's secondary improved from 28th to 12th in interceptions, 32nd to 13th in passes defended, and 18th to ninth in tackles for loss.

As a defensive line coach from 2011-17, he led one of the most productive defensive lines in the NFL. Since 2012, no defensive line group produced more sacks than Carolina's 219, part of Carolina's NFL-leading 280 total sacks. Carolina ranked in the top 10 in sacks in five of his seven seasons as defensive line coach, including a No. 1 ranking in 2013, No. 2 in 2016 and a No. 3 ranking in 2017.

Moving forward, Eric Washington and the Chicago Bears will partake in the NFL Scouting Combine, which takes place from March 26 – Feb. 4 in Indianapolis, Indiana.
