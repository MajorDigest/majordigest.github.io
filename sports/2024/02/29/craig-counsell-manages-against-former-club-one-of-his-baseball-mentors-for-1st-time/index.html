Craig Counsell manages against former club, one of his baseball mentors, for 1st time

MESA, Ariz. — For the first time Wednesday, Craig Counsell managed against the Milwaukee Brewers and one of his baseball mentors.

It was just a spring training game with no ramifications in the standings, of course. But it was still the first time the new Cubs manager filled out a lineup card against the franchise he grew up around and the one he managed to much success over the past eight-plus seasons.

It was also the first time Counsell managed against new Brewers skipper Pat Murphy, Counsell's head coach in college at Notre Dame from 1989 to 1992 and his bench coach for each of Counsell's eight full seasons as Milwaukee's manager.

Nobody would be much concerned about Wednesday's final score at the Brewers' American Family Fields of Phoenix — a 6-1 Cubs victory, by the way — but the storylines made for a nice prelude to an NL Central rivalry that's grown intense in recent years and now has a whole lot more juice to it.

Now, Counsell and Murphy will be matching minds from opposing dugouts.

"As a player, I took a ton from Pat," Counsell told the Sun-Times before Wednesday's game. "I learned a lot. He was very hard on players, in a good way, that you expect a college coach to be.

"... He certainly demanded that you were mentally tough. And what that does is, frankly, it gives people confidence after a while. And I think he's really good at doing that for the players."

Counsell was drafted in 1992 after playing for Murphy at Notre Dame and went on to an MLB career that spanned 16 years and included two World Series titles.

Murphy, who took the head coaching job at Arizona State a few years after Counsell left Notre Dame, told the Sun-Times he maintained a strong friendship with his former player during Counsell's professional playing days.

That included long dinners in which baseball was about the only topic.

"We'd sit there five hours, whatever, (and) we'd talk about baseball," Murphy told the Sun-Times. "... We'd do it year in and year out, week in and week out."

Murphy eventually moved into the professional coaching ranks, and Counsell was hired to manage the Brewers after they fired Ron Roenicke in May 2015. Counsell subsequently hired Murphy to be his bench coach the next season, and the duo went on to lead the Brewers to five playoff appearances, including three NL Central titles, over the past six seasons.

After Counsell departed the Brewers to move down I-94 on a record-setting contract, the Brewers named Murphy their manager.

Counsell's move shocked — and angered — many Brewers fans. According to the Milwaukee Journal Sentinel, there were some boos Wednesday from the crowd of 3,996 when Counsell and Murphy exchanged lineup cards before the game.

But players and managers from both teams seem more concerned about getting ready for Opening Day.

If they have grievances, they don't seem to be airing them publicly. Not yet, at least.

"It was weird seeing him in those colors," Murphy admitted to the Journal Sentinel. "But that's what the reality is right now.

"Move on. Every time we see each other, we'll exchange pleasantries. But we're both worried about getting our clubs ready to play the regular season."

Brewers shortstop Willy Adames, who flourished for Milwaukee under Counsell after the Tampa Bay Rays traded him there in 2021, told the Journal Sentinel he'll say hi to his former manager whenever he sees him.

"I'm always grateful," Adames said. "I mean, you can't forget what he's done for this organization."

The Cubs and Brewers are scheduled to play twice more in spring training, on March 12 and March 23, both at the Cubs' Sloan Park in Mesa.

Their first regular-season series is a four-gamer scheduled for May 27-30 at American Family Field in Milwaukee.

Wicks tosses three scoreless

As far as how Wednesday's game went on the field, left-hander Jordan Wicks worked three scoreless innings for the Cubs, allowing four hits with no walks and two strikeouts, to improve to 2-0 in spring training.

Shortstop prospect Luis Vazquez had two hits and drove in two for Chicago, outfield prospect Owen Caissie had two hits, drove in one and score one, and Seiya Suzuki doubled and scored a run in three at-bats.

The Sun-Times reported that Cubs right-hander Keegan Thompson experienced neck soreness, which Counsell said will push him back four or five days.

The Cubs improved to 3-2 in Cactus League play and were scheduled to host the Rockies at Sloan Park at 2:05 p.m. Thursday.
