NorCal thriller: San Ramon Valley comes up big in clutch, beats Mitty to advance to Division I final

DANVILLE — Six weeks ago, San Ramon Valley forward Jack Moxley thought his high school career might be over.

A dislocated patella in his right knee had many people within the team thinking about how the Wolves were going to go on without the senior in the playoffs.

Fast forward to Saturday: Moxley was the hero, extending his team’s season for at least one more game.

The 6-foot-3 forward made the two biggest plays, including a go-ahead basket late in the fourth quarter, to help lift the No. 2 seed Wolves over No. 3 Archbishop Mitty 52-49 in a CIF NorCal Division I semifinal.

SRV will play league rival Granada, the fourth seed, for the NorCal title on Tuesday night at Dublin High School. Granada, the defending Division I regional champion, advanced with a win at top-seeded Clovis West on Saturday.

Luke Isaak led SRV with 17 points, Seamus Deely had 10 and Moxley finished with eight.

Caeden Hutcherson and Tyler Jones each had 16 points for Mitty in the loss. Gavin Ripp had 11.

“It was win or go home,” Moxley said. “We’re battling for our lives out here and I’m glad we got it done.”

Mitty came out and blitzed the Wolves from the jump as the Monarchs needed three minutes to score the first 13 points.

SRV coach Brian Botteen readjusted his team’s defense during a timeout and the Wolves didn’t allow a Mitty basket until early in the second quarter.

The Danville school took a one-point advantage didn’t the second period but didn’t see a lead again until the fourth. Mitty led 29-25 at halftime.

Mitty center Gavin Ripp, the West Catholic Athletic League’s player of the year, picked up his third foul with 5:47 left in the third quarter, forcing coach Tim Kennedy to go with a smaller lineup.

It seemed to work. Even without Ripp, the Monarchs took advantage of the increased spacing on the floor.

Hutcherson scored eight points in the third quarter and helped stretch Mitty’s advantage to eight. Hutcherson continued to get to the basket at will in the fourth quarter as the Monarchs tried to pull away.

“I think it was something I had to do,” Hutcherson said about taking over with Ripp in foul trouble. “There was a momentum shift as soon as Gavin had to come out. So, I felt like I had to take over a little bit to just keep us in the game.”

For a moment, it looked as if Mitty might survive. Moxley botched a wide open layup that would have cut the visitor’s lead to three with just under five minutes left.

But Moxley soon redeemed himself.

With the Wolves down three with 1:17 left, Isaak fired a deep 3-pointer that didn’t draw iron. Moxley rushed in from the other side of the lane to grab the rebound and found Isaak cutting to the basket.

Isaak, the Wolves’ leading scorer, made a layup to bring SRV to within 49-48.

On the Wolves’ ensuing possession, they took the ball out of bounds and ran a play initially designed for Deely to get a layup. But as Moxley set the screen, he noticed both Mitty defenders went with Deely to the left side of the basket.

Moxley immediately went to the right side of the hoop, where Matthew Diekmann found him wide open. Moxley converted the go-ahead layup to put the Wolves up 50-49.

“His high school career could have been over six weeks ago,” Botteen said about Moxley. “Here he is six weeks later and he had one of the biggest rebounds and one of the easiest layups I’ve ever seen. I’m so glad for him. That was a good moment.”

Mitty had one last chance to tie on its last possession, but Ripp’s deep 3-pointer fell short.

For Mitty, the Central Coast Section Open Division runner-up, it was a tough end to its season.

“It was heartbreaking for our seniors who were huge parts of our program,” Kennedy said. “They threw themselves into it for so long just to help this program to see it keep building and getting better.”

SRV will now aim for its first state championship game appearance since 2015 when it plays Granada in the NorCal regional final. In 2015, SRV won the title against a Chino Hills team led by Chicago Bulls point guard and former No. 2 overall NBA draft pick Lonzo Ball.

Botteen, who was in his first season as head coach that year, said he sees similarities between the current SRV team and the squad that won it all nine years ago.

“Both teams play for each other,” Botteen said. “The 2014-2015 team was my first year and they had a knack for learning how to play with each other. There was also a lot of veteran leadership there. The similarities between this year’s team and 2014-2015 are parallel to one another.”

With Granada’s 70-53 win over No. 1 seed Clovis West on Saturday, it sets up the fourth meeting between the the Matadors and the Wolves. Granada got the best of SRV in the first matchup, but the Wolves won the next two in dominant fashion.

SRV and Granada have had a growing rivalry the past few years, which makes Tuesday’s NorCal final have even more stakes.

“It’s always a superheated game,” Isaak said. “(Granada) was in the state finals last year. I’m excited to go play them and hopefully end their season.”
