Clovis West star Tomlinson leading Eagles into huge state showdown

(Attached Video) As the Golden Eagles get ready for a huge state showdown Tuesday night, hear what inspired a young Athena Tomlinson to begin developing the skills that have made her one of the best high school basketball seniors in the country.

(KSEE/KG) - There are three teams from our viewing area still alive in the state basketball playoffs, and that group again includes the Clovis West girls basketball team, one of four teams still standing in the girls Open Division, the premiere division in the state playoffs.

A big reason why they are two wins away from the program's second-ever Open Division state title, is senior point guard Athena Tomlinson, a four-star recruit who is ranked 65th nationally in the ESPN.com HoopGurlz recruiting rankings.

Tomlinson scored 30 points in the Golden Eagles Open Division, NorCal Regional semifinal win on Saturday over St. Mary's out of Stockton, 69-57.

The 5-foot-5 phenom is putting in 19.3 points per game this season, but Athena does more than just score. She also averages 2.4 assists, 3.4 rebounds, and 3.0 steals per game, and puts on an entertaining show with her incredible ball handling, a skill she began developing when she was really young, while tagging along with her older brother to his games.

"I mean, from like four or five year's old, just sitting at my brother's basketball practices," said Tomlinson after the win over St. Mary's. "You know, working on my handles every single day, cause I wasn't even tall enough to shoot into the baskets. So what am I doing? I'm working on my dribbling, like, like, every single day!"

"Athena is such a dynamic player, because she can impact the game at both ends of the floor," said Clovis West head coach Craig Campbell after the St. Mary's win. "Defensively and offensively, she's terrific, and then having a co-pilot in Ariyah Smith, their two of the best backcourt (players) in the entire state, so having the two of them that we can trust, and just let them do their thing is really a blessing."

The Golden Eagles are 30-2, with their only two losses coming to out-of-section competition, but they will be a significant underdog in the next round, when they travel to San Jose to face private school power Archbishop Mitty in the Open Division, NorCal Regional Championship game.

Clovis West is the No. 2 seed in the NorCal bracket, while Mitty is the No. 1 seed, and at 29-0, the Monarchs are ranked No. 1 in the state, and the country, by Maxpreps.com.

These two teams have history in the state playoffs. Memorably, Clovis West beat Mitty 44-40 in the 2017 Open Division championship game at Golden 1 Center in Sacramento.

The last two seasons though, the Monarchs have eliminated the Golden Eagles in the Open Division, NorCal Regional semifinal round, beating them 53-45 in 2022, and 63-43 in 2023.

Both those games were played at Mitty's home gym in San Jose.

Despite those losses, Tomlinson is confident the Eagles can spring the upset Tuesday night.

"We played them the last two years in a row," said Tomlinson. "We're used to them, we're used to the prep, and honestly, it's a lot similar to this game (the win against St. Mary's), so I'm super confident about our chances against Mitty."

Mitty has five players on its roster over six-feet tall, and some serious star power, headlined by 6-foot-2 senior guard Morgan Cheli, a UCONN signee, who ESPN has ranked as the 18th-best player in the country in the class of 2024.

Clovis West only has one player over six-feet, so what will Campbell's message be:

"It's simply, you got nothing to lose," said Campbell. "You can't worry about making mistakes and playing tight, because we're gonna have to shoot the ball really well, and if you're second-guessing shooting and other things, you have no chance. You gotta come in, believing in what's on your chest in 'Clovis West,' and play the best Clovis West game we can, and you got a shot."

Yes, the Golden Eagles may be overmatched on paper, but as history has shown us, this Clovis West girls program always seems to have a puncher's chance.

"We do it from a culture standpoint," says Tomlinson. "So whoever comes in here (to Clovis West), they're gonna get coached hard, and they're gonna play super, super hard every game. That's the only way we can beat these teams is to be more physical than them, and play harder than them every possession of the game. So that's what we do, we come together as a team, and we're able to get big wins like that."

Also playing Tuesday in the state playoffs is the Caruthers girls basketball team, and the Bullard boys basketball squad.

The Blue Raiders are the No. 14 seed in NorCal Division III, and will travel to San Francisco to play top-seeded University High School, in the girls Division III, NorCal Regional Championship game.

The Knights are the No. 6 seed in NorCal Division III, and will play at No. 1 seed Santa Cruz High School in the boys, Division III, NorCal Regional Championship game.

Winners of these regional championship games will advance to the state championship games in their respective divisions, this upcoming Friday and Saturday at the Golden 1 Center in Sacramento.
