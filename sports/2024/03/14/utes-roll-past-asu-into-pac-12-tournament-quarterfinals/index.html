Utes roll past ASU into Pac-12 Tournament quarterfinals

SALT LAKE CITY (ABC4 Sports) - If the Utes are going to end their 8-year NCAA Tournament drought, they need to make a deep run in the Pac-12 Tournament.

Wednesday night was a good start.

Cole Bajema scored a career-high 22 points, Deivon Smith was one assist shy of his fourth triple-double of the season and Utah rolled over Arizona State 90-57 on Wednesday night in the Pac-12 Tournament.

No. 6-seeded Utah will play No. 3 Colorado in a quarterfinal game on Thursday at 9:30 p.m. MT.

"A very good win for us," said Utah head coach Craig Smith. "I thought our guys really competed well. Arizona State beat us the first two times, obviously at their place and then at our place. And so they had our number here a little bit. But I was proud how we played."

Bajema, who scored a career-high 21 points four games ago before scoring 3, 5, and 0 points in the next three games, hit 6 of 9 3-pointers and 7 of 11 shots overall.

"This is my fourth year in the Pac-12, said Bajema, a transfer from Washington. "it being the last year, we want to make our mark, end it well and keep advancing."

Smith had 15 points, 10 rebounds and nine assists, narrowly missing his fourth triple-double of the season. Hunter Erickson had 11 points and Gabe Madsen 10 for the Utes (19-13).

"I think we could be special in these last few games," Deivon Smith said. "We have the talent enough to win the championship here. So I think we're ready for whatever."

Utes leading scorer Branden Carlson, who was a game-time decision with an injured elbow, gutted it out and had seven points and two rebounds in 18 minutes of action.

"It was great to see Branden come out there," Smith said. "It was a tough injury, and obviously he's not over it. But I think that kind of helped us as well as a team and he was able to stick two 3s and just be solid."

Frankie Collins scored 20 points and Kamari Lands 11 for Arizona State (14-18).

Utah shot 70% in the first half and Bajema led the way with 14 points and 4 of 6 3-pointers. The Utes already led 31-18 before Bajema hit three 3-pointers on three consecutive possessions for a 40-18 lead with 4 1/2 minutes to go. The Utes' lead peaked at 28 points when Bajema made a layup with 90 seconds left and they led 47-22 at halftime.

Bajema hit a 3-pointer to put the Utes up by 33 points midway through the second half and the lead peaked at 35 when Jayden Peat hit a 3 with 43 seconds left.

The Utes finished the game at 59% from the field and made 12 of 26 3-pointers. Arizona State shot 31% overall and made 3 of 19 3-pointers.

Utah had lost both meetings against Arizona State in the regular season, and had dropped five straight to the Sun Devils overall.

Utah split their season series with Colorado with each team winning on their home court.

Source: Dana Greene, ABC 4
