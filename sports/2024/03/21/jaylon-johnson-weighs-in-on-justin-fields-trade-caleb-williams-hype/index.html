Jaylon Johnson weighs in on Justin Fields trade, Caleb Williams hype

CHICAGO — Jaylon Johnson joined the Up and Adams Show Wednesday where the Chicago Bears All-Pro corner revealed his reaction to the Justin Fields trade, and his initial thoughts of standout quarterback prospect Caleb Williams.

Up and Adams Show host Kay Adams asked Johnson Wednesday what his initial reaction was to the Fields trade that was announced Saturday.

“If you truly believe in a guy, I feel like the speculation would’ve been shot down, and I think for me, that’s when I kind of started preparing for it — When the speculation and the questions weren’t kind of like shot down," Johnson told Kay Adams Wednesday. "Still, that last little bit of hope I think still is something that I held on to … But then when you actually see it come across your page … It’s like, ‘D*mn, my dog really isn’t going to be with us no more.’”

Johnson and Fields spent the last three years together in Chicago, where one became a top-five-level defensive back in the National Football League, and the other flashed examples of athletic brilliance under center, but was ultimately weighed down by struggles that included throwing with anticipation, holding onto the ball too long, and a lack of ability to elevate his play during key moments.

Fields was traded to the Pittsburgh Steelers in exchange for a conditional sixth round pick that's upgraded to a fourth round pick if the former-Bears quarterback plays in at least 51% of the Steelers offensive snaps in 2024.

The move preceded what most say is almost a forgone conclusion that the Bears will now select Williams with the No. 1 overall pick when the NFL draft commences at the end of next month.

When asked what he sees in the former-USC Trojan quarterback, Johnson was frank about what life will be like for a superstar prospect from California walking into the NFL.

"I feel like you just humble yourself coming into the building ... You can't bring that Hollywood stuff into the building," Johnson told Adams. "Especially not with guys who have played this game, I feel like, at a high level for consecutive years in the league.

"It's just something that guys like myself, guys like Tremaine [Edmunds], T.J. [Edwards], the vets, Keenan Allen … We're going to see through it. What you did in college, the Hollywood, it's not that. You got to prove yourself, stuff like that don't matter."

Johnson also said it's important to find a balance between getting to know Williams — The person — and finding out the nuances of what gets him going, in both positive and negative directions.

"You've got to get to know him too. I think it's a fine line between trying to prove a point to him, but also getting to know him because at the end of the day, you want him to be the absolute best he can be," Johnson said.

In Johnson's eyes, that's where he and other veterans on the team will come in to help Williams grow, if he ends up being selected by the Bears, and help him become a better professional football player.

"That's what we're bringing him in for — To win games," Johnson said. "I think truly, just learning who he is as a person, learning him deeper than just all the Hollywood stuff you see, but actually trying to learn and get to know him and then from there, knowing what pushes him.

"If it is trash talk — If trash talk makes him crumble, then I feel like we got to push that button because people in the game, they're going to push that button. Just depending on whatever that is that he needs to be pushed at, that's our job and that's our duty as his teammates to make him better and strengthen his weaknesses."

In two years at USC, Williams established himself as one of the premier quarterback prospects in college football. Over those two seasons, he completed 599 of 688 passes (67.5%) for 8,170 yards, 72 touchdowns and 10 interceptions, to go with 210 carries for 524 yards (2.5 YPC) and 21 touchdowns on the ground.

This included his Heisman trophy campaign, where he threw for 4,537 yards, 42 touchdowns, 5 interceptions, and ran the ball 113 times for 382 yards and 10 touchdowns in 2022.

After sending a contingent of coaches and front offices members to Williams' pro day at USC Wednesday, the Bears will now go about holding their Top 30 prospect visits to team facilities, leading up the NFL draft taking place in Detroit from April 25-27.

The Bears currently hold four draft picks — No. 1, 9, 75 and 122.
