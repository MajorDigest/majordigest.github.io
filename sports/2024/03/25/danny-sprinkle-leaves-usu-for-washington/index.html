Danny Sprinkle leaves USU for Washington

LOGAN, Utah (ABC4 Sports) - After a historic run to its first outright Mountain West Conference title, and its first win in the NCAA Tournament in 23 years, Utah State head coach Danny Sprinkle is moving on.

One day after losing to Purdue in the second round of the NCAA Tournament, Sprinkle is leaving Utah State to become the head coach at Washington, where he has reportedly agreed to a six-year deal worth $22.1 million.

In his only season in Logan, the 47-year-old Sprinkle, who is from Pullman, Washington, led the Aggies to a 28-7 record and the Mountain West regular season championship.

"It was going to take something special for me to leave Logan, and the University of Washington was it," Sprinkle said in a statement. "The university means a great deal to our family and I am excited to return to my home state."

Washington reportedly paid a buyout to Utah State of almost $3 million to hire Sprinkle.

The Aggies beat TCU in the first round of the Big Dance on Friday, 88-72, its first win in the tournament since 2001. Utah State then got blown out by top-seeded Purdue in the second round on Sunday, 106-67.

After being picked ninth in the preseason Mountain West poll, Utah State started the season 16-1 and won the Mountain West at 14-4 in league play. Sprinkle was named Mountain West Coach of the Year.

Utah State athletic director Diana Sabau released a statement Monday saying, "Utah State men’s basketball had a great season under coach Sprinkle’s leadership. We did everything possible to retain him with a package that would have been highly competitive in the Mountain West in terms of compensation and support. Our tradition of success over the years has never been about one coach or one player. With 24 NCAA Tournament appearances, including 12 this century under several head coaches, winning is a part of our DNA. We have an incredible and knowledgeable fan base with generous donors, and we compete in one of the nation’s most competitive conferences. We are aligned with the University on a pursuit of excellence with elite facilities and a proven commitment to the student-athlete experience. Our search is already underway and our candidate pool is strong. I cannot wait to introduce our new head coach and begin to write the next chapter in our storied basketball history.”

When Sprinkle left Montana State for Utah State, he brought Darius Brown II and Great Osobor with him. Brown is out of eligibility, but Osobor, the Mountain West Conference Player of the Year, has one season left. It is unclear if Osobor will follow Sprinkle to Washington, but it is entirely possible.

Josh Uduje, who also has one year of eligibility left, transferred to Utah State with Sprinkle from Coastal Carolina this past season.

"With a strong commitment to excellence and incredible fan support, we will recruit exceptional young men who will take pride in wearing the purple and gold, who will win championships and who will leave UW with an impressive degree," Sprinkle said. "I am excited to get started and to be a part of such a special place.”

Sprinkle has made the NCAA Tournament in three straight seasons, two with Montana State and one with Utah State.

Utah State has now gone through three coaches, Craig Smith, Ryan Odom and Sprinkle since 2018. The school's next coaching hire will be its sixth in the last ten years.

Sprinkle will lead Washington into the Big Ten Conference next season, along with Oregon, UCLA and USC from the Pac-12.

Among the possible candidates to replace Sprinkle is Toledo head coach Tod Kowalczyk, who has won the regular season in the MAC the past four seasons. Utah State associate head coach Andy Hill is also expected to be considered.

A national search for Utah State's next head coach is underway.

"Utah State University is a remarkable institution and our athletics programs play an important role on our campus and in our community and is a significant aspect of our plan to further elevate the University," said Utah State University president Elizabeth Cantwell. "Our men’s basketball program has been a point of pride for a long time and its success has lifted our amazing students, faculty, staff, alumni, fans and community. We are well-positioned to continue our track record of winning and I am confident that Director of Athletics Diana Sabau will lead a comprehensive search and identify the right coach to take us to even greater heights. We thank coach Sprinkle for an incredible season leading Utah State basketball and wish him the very best.”

Sprinkle, whose father played football at Washington, is replacing Mike Hopkins, who was fired this month after seven seasons in Seattle.
