Archbishop John Carroll Women's Lacrosse Team Fights for 4th State Championship

Last week, we brought you a story about the “Archbishop John Carroll” Patriots lacrosse team’s success over the last 25 years, under the leadership of Coach Lorraine Beers. She told PHL17's Jimmy Marlow that she never expected to make the PCL championship for 25 straight years.

This week, we hear from a group of seniors who have the chance to win their 4th straight state championship, and they explain how they continue to enjoy each game, as they look to make more history.

Senior, Sienna Golden said that she has an amazing relationship with her teammates adding, “I don’t have teammates; I’ve got family here for life.” Sam Hewitt, who is also a senior, reflected on the last 3 years saying, “Being on a team that is so successful brings a lot of responsibility, but also a lot of confidence.”

The entire team is excited and focused, but the Seniors are especially brimming with confidence as they try to finish their high school careers with 4-straight state championships, while continuing the program’s dominance in the PCL by winning a 25th championship, under Coach Beers. When they were asked to reflect on having so much success throughout their time at Carroll, while playing the sport they love with some of their best friends Golden replied, “It’s been an amazing experience playing on such big stages and playing as a team. It’s something that a lot of people don’t get to experience, so I’ve learned to not take that for granted.”

Brooke Hipper, another Senior, responded similarly, saying “It’s really rewarding because we put in so much time and work all year round; it really shows how much we work and how hard we’ve worked to get where we are.”

Jayanna Ramsey-Williams who is also a state champion says the struggle has helped her mature as a person and player, “It’s been challenging and really fun; challenging because everyone is really good, and we always have to push each other and keep each other motivated. I really like our team because I feel like we have a great connection.”

The culture and focus of the Lady Patriots is guided by a living legend, coach Lorraine Beers, who’s players say that she is the guide, and catalyst for their cohesiveness, continuity, and intensity as a team. Hewitt says her attention to detail is a major reason that the team continues to dominate, “It’s comforting because you know any criticism that she gives you, you know she’s right and she’s looking out for you. All of the potential you have, she can see it in you before you see it in yourself.”

Ramsey-Williams, says agreed explaining how Coach Beers is a consistent and careful leader, “She’s made a really big impact, she’s definitely helped open my eyes to know what I want to do for college, and if I really wanted to play or not. She really is a great coach; she keeps us motivated and she keeps our eye on the prize.

Chloe Bleckley, yet another senior leader on the Lady Patriot’s squad, confirmed her teammate's sentiments saying Of Coach Beers, “She is the best, she is one of the main reasons I picked Carroll; she’s the greatest of all-time.

Now that the season has begun, the Lady Patriots have a message for their competition, Ramsey-Williams says, “We look sweet, but we are coming for you”.

Hewitt says, “My message to our competition is that we have been preparing all year, and even though we lost a lot of seniors, we have a lot of underclassmen that are ready to step up to prove themselves”.

Chloe Bleckley says they’re not letting up this year, “We are coming back stronger than we did last year, so we’re trying to win the 4-peat, keep the streak going in the PCLs, so watch out.”

Brooke Hipper added, “I would tell them to prepare more because we have been preparing all year, so we are coming, and just to be ready.”

Many of the players on this team will be go on to play college lacrosse, but they are hopeful that they can pull off the unthinkable, which is a “4-peat.”

Source: Jimmy Marlow, PHL 17
