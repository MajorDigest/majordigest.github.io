White Sox off to worst 81-game start in franchise history, on pace for worst record ever over 162-game schedule

CHICAGO (AP) — The Chicago White Sox have now had the worst half-season in the franchise's 124-year history.

And they're on pace to challenge for the ignominious title of worst team of all-time.

Shohei Ohtani hit a leadoff homer and a tiebreaking RBI single Tuesday night, leading the Los Angeles Dodgers to a 4-3 victory over the White Sox at Guaranteed Rate Field.

At 21-60, the White Sox are off to the worst 81-game start in franchise history. Their previous mark for futility was 27-54 in 1934 and again in 1948.

For reference, the 1962 New York Mets went 40-120, which remains the worst record ever since MLB went to a 162-game schedule prior to that season (the Mets had two postponed games that season that were not made up).

Through their first 81 games, the 1962 Mets were 23-58.

So the 2024 White Sox, who've lost six of their last seven and host the first-place Dodgers again Wednesday night, are two games behind the pace of the infamous 1962 Mets through the first half of the season.

The 2023 Detroit Tigers, by comparison, also went 20-61 through their first 81 games but managed to finish the season with 43 wins.

Close losses

Including Monday's defeat, the White Sox dropped to 6-15 in one-run games this season.

"It’s been tough. It might look a lot uglier than it actually is," said outfielder Andrew Benintendi, whose two-run homer in the bottom of the first Monday gave Chicago an early lead that didn't last.

"We’ve played a lot of one-run ballgames. We’ve put together one, two good weeks. But we’ve got 81 games left, just keep trucking, keep working on things, keep getting better."

After Ohtani led off Monday's game by driving a hanging curveball from White Sox starter Chris Flexen into the visitor’s bullpen in right-center — just beyond the glove of leaping right fielder Tommy Pham — for his NL-best 24th homer, the White Sox responded well off Dodgers starter Bobby Miller in the bottom of the first.

Miller is a native of Elk Grove Village in Chicago's northwest suburbs.

Pham drew a leadoff walk, then Benintendi homered to right to give Chicago a quick 2-1 lead. Luis Robert Jr. then singled and Eloy Jiménez plated him with a double to left, giving the White Sox a 3-1 lead before they made an out.

But they would go on to make 27 outs after that without scoring again.

Meanwhile, Flexen (2-7) pitched pretty well, allowing four runs and six hits in 5 2/3 innings. The right-hander is winless in his last nine starts, however.

"I thought I battled pretty decent, but obviously it didn’t put us in position to win," he said. "Handful of walks, a couple home runs. Early lead, so pretty disappointed about it. Tough battle."

Led by Ohtani and Freddie Freeman, the Dodgers won for the seventh time in nine games and are on the opposite end of the spectrum from the White Sox. The NL West leaders are 50-31 at the midpoint of their schedule.

Ohtani also walked and scored on Freeman’s two-run homer in the third inning. He batted again in the fourth and gave Los Angeles a 4-3 lead with a two-out RBI single.

"It wasn't an easy pitch to hit, but my mindset was getting a base hit," Ohtani said through an interpreter.

Ohtani has driven in at least one run in his last nine games, matching a franchise record. The two-time AL MVP is batting .306 (26-for-85) with 10 homers and 22 RBI in 22 games this month after switching leagues — and Los Angeles franchises — from the Angels to the Dodgers in free agency this offseason.

“I don't know what more we can really say about him,” Freeman said. “I think we've said everything we can since he entered this league, about what an amazing player he is. ... Sometimes you just got to step back and just appreciate a player like this.”

Miller, meanwhile, lasted just two innings in his second start since returning from shoulder inflammation. Michael Petersen (2-0) got six outs for the win, and Evan Phillips handled the ninth for his 13th save in 13 chances.

“The shoulder feels great. My body feels great,” said Miller, who played his prep ball at McHenry High School and was a first-round pick of the Dodgers in 2020 after attending the University of Louisville.

“I think it's just sometimes trying a little bit too hard out there, doing a little bit too much.”

Dodgers outfielder Teoscar Hernández rejoined the team after he missed Monday's 3-0 victory in the series opener to fly home to the Dominican Republic for a personal reason. But manager Dave Roberts gave Hernández another day off.

Trainer's room

White Sox: Manager Pedro Grifol said the team is monitoring Benintendi in his comeback from left Achilles tendinitis. “We’ll manage that with maybe some matchups that are good for us as a team and at the same time gives him an opportunity to rest that Achilles and kind of regroup,” Grifol said. ... RHP Mike Clevinger (elbow inflammation) pitched 4 2/3 innings in his second rehab start for Triple-A Charlotte on Sunday, but the team hasn't confirmed a date for his next outing just yet.

Up next

Right-handers Gavin Stone (8-2, 3.04 ERA) and Erick Fedde (5-2, 3.05 ERA) pitch on Wednesday night in the series finale. Stone is 7-1 with a 2.18 ERA in his last 10 starts for Los Angeles. Fedde is 1-1 with a 2.88 ERA in four June starts for Chicago.

First pitch is scheduled for 7:10 p.m. at Guaranteed Rate Field.
