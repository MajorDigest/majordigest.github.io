A look back at Klay Thompson's 5 best games in a Warriors uniform

(KRON) -- It's the end of an era in Golden State. Franchise legend Klay Thompson is no longer a member of the Warriors as he reportedly agreed to a three-year, $50 million free agency deal with the Dallas Mavericks on Monday.

Thompson, 34, will be suiting up for another NBA team for the first time after spending his entire 13-year career with the Warriors. Since being drafted by the Dubs in 2011, Thompson was a catalyst for four NBA championships in the Warriors' dynastic run.

Although it may be a sad day for Dub Nation, here's a look back at five of Thompson's greatest games in a Warriors uniform. Which one was your favorite?

Jan. 23, 2015: NBA-record 37-point quarter

Nearly a decade later, the feat still seems unfathomable. Thompson scored an NBA-record 37 points in a quarter.

Thompson could not be stopped in the third quarter against the Sacramento Kings. He went a perfect 13-of-13 from the field, nine of them 3-pointers. The sharpshooter scored 37 of the Warriors' 41 points that quarter.

May 26, 2016: The birth of 'Game 6 Klay'

An NBA playoff legend was born on that Saturday night in Oklahoma City.

With his team facing elimination, Thompson kept the Warriors alive by scoring 41 points, including a playoff-record 11 3-pointers. It was the first of many iconic Game 6 performances to come.

Other "Game 6 Klay" performances include the 2018 Western Conference finals against the Rockets and 2022 Western Conference semifinals against the Grizzlies.

Dec. 5, 2016: 60 points in 29 minutes

What was more impressive? The fact Thompson scored 60 points in essentially three quarters or that fact he did it on 11 dribbles.

Along with his 37-point quarter, Thompson's performance against the Pacers shows how hot the sharpshooter can get. He set a career-high on 21-of-33 shooting, including 8-of-14 from deep.

Oct. 29, 2018: Breaking single-game 3-point record

Thompson scored 52 points against the Bulls in Chicago, breaking the NBA single-game record with 14 3-pointers made. He went 18-of-29 from the field and 14-of-24 from distance.

The shooter made the most 3-pointers in a single game -- a league record that was previously set by his Splash Brother Stephen Curry. Thompson's record still stands to this day.

June 13, 2019: NBA Finals Game 6 vs. Raptors

Thompson was arguably playing the best basketball of his career at this point. The game became a bittersweet memory as Thomspon's night was cut short when he suffered a torn ACL -- a factor in Golden State's loss.

Nonetheless, it was another "Game 6 Klay" performance as he scored 30 points on 8-of-12 shooting, including 4-of-6 from distance. He also hit all 10 of his free throws.

It was unfortunate timing that he tore his ACL in the third quarter of a game in which the Warriors were facing elimination. This was the first of two season-ending injuries Thompson suffered -- the second was a torn Achilles in 2020.

Game 6 of the 2019 Finals was the last time Warriors fans saw Thompson play until 941 days later when he made his return on Jan. 9, 2022 against the Cavaliers.

Honorable Mention

This wasn't an NBA game, but Thompson's performance in the 2016 3-point contest was a memorable one. One year earlier, he got beat by Curry. In 2016, Thompson got his revenge by edging out his teammate with 27 points in the final round.
