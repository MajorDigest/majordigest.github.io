Warriors fans on social media react to Klay Thompson reportedly leaving for Mavericks

In the grand scope of the broader NBA landscape, Klay Thompson’s decision to leave the Warriors​ and sign a reported three-year, $50 million deal likely isn’t the biggest basketball move of the offseason. Thompson, 34, can still be a great complimentary piece to Luka Doncic and Kyrie Irving in Dallas, but his days of being an elite 3-and-D player are long over.

But as far as sentimentality? Few moves this summer, if any, will touch the magnitude of Thompson’s departure — even if it was expected.

Of course, there’s the basketball element. Thompson didn’t just orchestrate some of the best moments in franchise history, but in league history. Game 6 in Oklahoma City. 14 three-pointers in Chicago. Two free throws on a torn ACL. 37 points in a single quarter. 60 points on 11 dribbles. He’s a four-time champion, a five-time All-Star and, depending on who you ask, the second-best shooter of all-time.

But there was also Thompson, the person. Remember the time he signed a toaster? Or when he was interviewed about scaffolding in New York? The time he missed a 360 dunk in China? How many covers of SLAM Magazine are funnier than his? What about the many exploits of “Captain Klay” and his dog, Rocco? Even his impersonator felt par for the course.

The internet, to the surprise of no one, has had much to say since Thompson’s signing hit the internet. Here are some of those reactions:
