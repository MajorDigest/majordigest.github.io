Bulls offseason: DeRozan likely to leave, LaVine in limbo as team enters youth movement

CHICAGO — The Bulls have made it clear they're headed for a full-on youth movement with their offseason moves so far.

So it would appear that one of Chicago's veteran players and top offensive options the last three seasons will not be back with the team, while his high-scoring Bulls running mate's status remains in limbo.

According to an X post Monday night, a source told the Sun-Times that Chicago's offseason trade of Alex Caruso for Josh Giddey was the "final straw" in veteran small forward DeMar DeRozan's mind for a possible return to the Bulls.

Meanwhile, Bleacher Report's Chris Haynes reported Sunday that the Bulls proposed trading guard Zach LaVine to the Golden State Warriors for a package centered around Chris Paul and Andrew Wiggins, but the Warriors did not feel the proposal was "feasible."

The Warriors waived Paul, the future Hall-of-Fame point guard, on Sunday.

Meanwhile, the Bulls have made their roster significantly younger this offseason.

First, they traded Caruso, a 30-year-old guard known best as a strong defender, for Giddey, a 21-year-old guard once drafted No. 6 overall by the Oklahoma City Thunder who has standout play-making ability but hasn't been a good shooter.

Then, the Bulls reportedly agreed this week to free-agent contracts with 22-year-old forward Patrick Williams, keeping the team's one-time No. 4 overall draft pick in Chicago, and 24-year-old center Jalen Smith, who comes over from the Indiana Pacers and will presumably back up Nikola Vucevic.

And in last week’s draft, the Bulls selected 19-year-old Matas Buzelis — a Chicagoland native who played one season of high school basketball at Hinsdale Central — at No. 11 overall.

Plus, guard Coby White, drafted No. 7 overall by the Bulls a year before Williams, is still just 24 and is coming off a breakout 2023-24 season.

So where does that leave DeRozan and LaVine?

It seems certain one or both won't be with the Bulls next season, but their situations are also different.

DeRozan a free agent

It's probably a lock to assume DeRozan will be on the move, because he's an unrestricted free agent.

Now 34, DeRozan spent the last three seasons of his 15-year career with the Bulls. He currently ranks 31st on the NBA's all-time scoring list with 23,582 points and could soon crack the illustrious list of 25 players in league history to score at least 25,000 career points.

Known most for his stellar mid-range game, DeRozan signed a three-year, $81.9 million contract with the Bulls prior to the 2021-22 season to come over from the San Antonio Spurs.

Given his status as one of the NBA's top all-time scorers and the hefty contracts that have reportedly been dished out already this offseason to free agents who don't have nearly the career accomplishments as DeRozan, it's clear he'll command a sizeable salary that the Bulls would want no part of at this point.

If DeRozan's time in Chicago is indeed over, it was a three-year run filled with some great moments, including several buzzer-beaters. He averaged 25.5 points per game with the Bulls, more than with his previous two teams, on 49.6% shooting.

But as a team, the Bulls couldn't break through into the Eastern Conference's elite with DeRozan. They were knocked out in the first round of the playoffs in 2021-22 and failed to advance out of the play-in round each of the last two seasons.

LaVine under contract

LaVine's situation isn't so cut-and-dried.

He still has two more years, plus a player option for another season after that, on the five-year deal worth just over $215 million he signed with the Bulls prior to the 2022-23 season.

That's a hefty contract to move, so it's very possible LaVine will remain with the Bulls, even though it seems they're actively shopping him. However, with contracts soaring this offseason, perhaps the price tag for a player with LaVine's scoring capability isn't as high as it might look.

Whatever happens, LaVine's legacy with the Bulls is a complicated one since he came to Chicago in a multi-player trade with the Minnesota Timberwolves in June 2017 that also brought in Lauri Markkanen but sent out Jimmy Butler.

LaVine is still just 29, but injuries have hampered his tenure in Chicago. He played just 24 games in 2017-18 and only 25 last season. That did come, however, after playing in 67 and 77 games, all starts, in 2021-22 and 2022-23, respectively.

LaVine has never been a good defender, and his offensive decision-making can often be questioned. But there aren't many scorers in the NBA as gifted as he is.

LaVine has averaged 24.2 points per game with the Bulls, a scoring output that can't easily be replaced. He made back-to-back All-Star teams from 2020-22, averaging a career-high 27.4 points per game in 2020-21.

If LaVine does stay with the Bulls, it's possible they could either showcase him to other teams for an in-season trade, or keep him off the floor to avoid an injury that would hurt his trade value.

Either way, it's obvious the Bulls are moving on from the LaVine-DeRozan era, a star-crossed period that includes a what-might've-been if point guard Lonzo Ball — who hasn't played since 2022 — had healthy knees.

That star-crossed, what-might've-been feeling for Bulls fans now goes all the way back to that fateful April 28, 2012, night, when Derrick Rose tore his ACL in the playoffs and changed the course of a franchise that's still trying to seek the right one since the 1990s glory days of Michael Jordan and company.

For the Bulls, it's time to start over again.
