NASCAR in Chicago: Saturday's updates from downtown

CHICAGO — The second version of NASCAR's street race in the second city gets underway Saturday, kicking off a weekend of racing and entertainment that aims to thrill racing fans, introduce the sport to future fans, and repeat last year's boost to the city's economy.

Gates around the course and to the free-to-the-public NASCAR Village experience at Butler Field open at 8:45 a.m.

Race practice and qualifying rounds begin at 9 a.m. with a 50-lap race called The Loop 110 scheduled for 2:30 p.m.

The Black Keyes and The Chainsmokers will take the stage for the evening's concerts.
