She joined the shooting club at U of U. Now she's headed to Paris for her 2nd Olympics

SALT LAKE CITY (ABC4) -- For Lexi Lagan being called an Olympian was something she had never imagined for herself, but her life has been full of twists and turns.

The 31-year-old Colorado native originally planned to come to Utah to attend BYU but made the last-minute change to attend the physics program at the University of Utah. That decision set off a butterfly effect of changes that is now making her a two-time Olympian.

This summer, Lagan will be competing for Team USA's shooting team in the Air Pistol events at the Paris 2024 Summer Olympic Games. But her Olympic journey started as a Ute.

"When I went to the University of Utah, I was looking to make some new friends because I had just moved up to the Salt Lake area," Lagan told ABC4.com. "I went to the freshman orientation and along with some of the rows and the clubs I was interested in, the marksmanship club was there. I asked them what it was like and what they did, and they kind of downplayed how cool the sport was."

Through the marksmanship club, Lagan met her air pistol coaches Elwood Powell and Matt DeLong. After just two weeks, Lagan was sold. She was going to fully commit to shooting and by 2015, she was determined to become an Olympian.

Lagan started competing in national tournaments. She quickly tried out for the 2016 Olympic Games in Rio and was named alternate for Team USA. While she didn't end up competing, she said that pushed her on the right path.

Her life took another unexpected turn, taking her out of Utah and to Colorado Springs, where she said, one thing led to another. She began training for the World Championships in 2018. After that, she became a member of the United States Shooting National Team. Then, she finally solidified herself on the Olympic team in March 2020 for the Tokyo Summer Olympic Games.

But then a funny thing happened. Life took yet another unexpected turn.

"Shortly after that day after I got nominated for the team, we had the whole shut down for COVID," Lagan said. "So, we had a year-long delay where I got to say I had been nominated for the Olympic team. I'm going to be an Olympian, but I'm not there yet."

The Tokyo 2020 Summer Olympic Games were finally held in July 2021. Lagan said after the year's delay, finally making it to the land of the rising sun was unreal. She competed in the 10m Women's Air Pistol where she finished just 22 points below the podium.

She told ABC4.com that being a part of the Olympics as an athlete was so much bigger than she ever expected, and it brought a lot of emotions. But her teammates were telling her she hadn't seen anything yet.

"My teammates that had been to previous Olympics had said this isn't anything like a non-COVID, non-pandemic Olympics," Lagan said. "[They told me], 'You really need to go again to really experience it.' So that's what I did."

After years of hard work, Lagan solidified her spot on Team USA for the Paris 2024 games. She said she is excited to compete in the Olympics without a global pandemic looming over the city. She told ABC4.com she is looking forward to seeing so many people celebrating the games and celebrating Team USA.

This year, the Air Pistol competition is scheduled to be one of the first events in the games. Lagan won't be apart of the Opening Ceremonies -- something she attended when she was in Tokyo -- because she will be busy getting a good night's rest before her competition the next morning. But she is looking forward to being part of the closing celebrations -- something she missed in Tokyo due to the pandemic.

After that, she is looking forward to experiencing Paris.

"My parents and I are planning on doing some excursions and some tours in addition to going to watch some of the other competitors and competitions such as beach volleyball under the Eiffel Tower," Lagan said. "So there will be a lot of really cool things happening there."

But first, it's all about the competition. Lagan said the last week has been stressful, filled with lots of paperwork, forms, and training in preparation for Paris. But she -- along with the rest of Team USA -- is taking it day by day and living every single moment. She said she feels overwhelming support from her family, her friends, her coaches, and several people she has never met in person.

As the Paris Olympic games draw closer, she said she feels more excited than anything.

Despite all of life's unexpected twists and turns, Lagan told ABC4.com it's been quite a blessing to experience her unique journey. For anyone else aiming to become an Olympian, Lagan said they should "keep charging on."

"Never compare your challenges to somebody else and remember to always go for gold," she said.

As for what the future holds for Lagan? Right now she is focusing on the Paris Olympic Games but when she gets home, she and her husband of five years will be looking to move from Colorado Springs. She said they are unsure if that move will be back to Boulder or Salt Lake City in the Beehive State.

But wherever life takes her, she will have an eye toward Los Angeles and the Summer Olympic Games in 2028.

"Having an Olympics on home turf is a huge deal," Lagan explained. "The only person that I've been able to talk to who experienced something similar is Kim Rhode, who is a fabulous shotgun shooter from Team USA. She won her first medal at the Atlanta Games. She said, 'You have to stick around for LA. Get everybody that you know excited because the entire country gets overwhelmingly supportive of their competitors, their athletes, as we get ready for the home turf Olympics.'"
