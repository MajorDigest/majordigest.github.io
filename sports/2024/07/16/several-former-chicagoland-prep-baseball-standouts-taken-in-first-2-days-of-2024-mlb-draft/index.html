Several former Chicagoland prep baseball standouts taken in first 2 days of 2024 MLB Draft

Several one-time prep baseball standouts from Chicagoland could soon be entering the professional ranks after being selected within the first 10 rounds of the 2024 MLB Draft.

This year's draft began Sunday and concludes Tuesday in Fort Worth, Texas, held in conjunction with All-Star Week. The All-Star Game is Tuesday night in Arlington.

The first 10 rounds of the 20-round draft have been completed, with the final 10 rounds on Tuesday before the All-Star Game.

The highest Chicagoland prep player taken was Ryan Sloan, out of York Community High School in the western suburb of Elmhurst. He went in the second round, on Day 1 of the draft, at No. 55 overall to the Seattle Mariners.

A right-handed pitcher, Sloan was the 19th-ranked draft prospect by MLB Pipeline and this past season was named the Gatorade Illinois High School Player of the Year.

Players drafted right out of high school can choose to forego signing a pro contract and play college baseball instead. They'll then be draft eligible again down the road.

Sloan, for example, is a Wake Forest recruit. But given his status as a second-round pick, it's unlikely Sloan would pass up signing a pro contract now. The approximate value for the slot he was drafted in, per MLB.com, is $1.64 million, so it's likely he'll sign a pro contract and enter the Mariners system right away.

Here's a glance at the players with Chicagoland prep ties who were drafted in the first 10 rounds this week:

Ryan Sloan, RHP

High School: York Community (Elmhurst)

Round: 2nd (No. 55 overall)

Team: Seattle Mariners

MLB Pipeline says: "Sloan has a strong lower half and is built to eat innings. He uses his size to create extension and a flat approach angle to add deception. He repeats his delivery and throws his entire arsenal for quality strikes."

Tyler Bell, SS

High School: Lincoln Way East (Frankfort)

Round: Competitive Balance B (No. 66 overall)

Team: Tampa Bay Rays

MLB Pipeline says: "One of the top shortstops in the 2024 high school class, Bell plays quicker than his average speed and covers ground to both sides. He's a smooth defender who can execute throws from any angle with solid arm strength. He also has a good internal clock and the athleticism to play all over the diamond if he becomes more of a utilityman than an everyday player."

Brandon Tunink, OF
