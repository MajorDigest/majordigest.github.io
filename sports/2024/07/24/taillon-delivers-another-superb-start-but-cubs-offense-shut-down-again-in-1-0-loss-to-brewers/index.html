Taillon delivers another superb start, but Cubs offense shut down again in 1-0 loss to Brewers

CHICAGO (AP) — It was a storyline all too familiar for the 2024 Cubs on Tuesday night, as their postseason hopes continue to slip away.

A fantastic starting pitching performance was wasted by an offense that just couldn't get the big hit.

Brice Turang drove in the game's only run with a two-out single in the top of the eighth inning, while the Cubs stranded 10 runners on base against five Milwaukee pitchers, spoiling a superb start by Cubs righty Jameson Taillon as the first-place Brewers emerged with a 1-0 victory at Wrigley Field.

With one game left in the three-game series, and in the teams' 13-game season series, the Cubs dropped 10 games back of the Brewers in the National League Central standings. Milwaukee also took a 7-5 lead to clinch the season series, regardless of the outcome of Wednesday afternoon's finale at Wrigley Field.

The Cubs will enter Wednesday just a percentage point ahead of the Cincinnati Reds for last place in the NL Central and 4.5 games back of the third and final NL Wild Card spot, with three other teams now ahead of them on the outside looking in.

It all came a day after Cubs president of baseball operations Jed Hoyer on Monday said the team likely would not be buyers by next week's MLB trade deadline, which is Tuesday at 5 p.m.

Taillon superb

Tuesday's game began almost 90 minutes late after a quick but lightning-heavy thunderstorm.

Once it started, Taillon was locked in.

Seeking to win his fourth straight start, Taillon worked 7 1/3 shutout innings, allowing four hits and three walks with three strikeouts.

“The results were pretty good,” Taillon said. “Honestly, I didn’t feel my best, but that’s kind of been a common theme this year. Just find a way to get it done and find a way to get creative with the pitch mix and get contact on my terms and (get) my pitches in my good areas.”

Taillon departed in the eighth after allowing back-to-back one-out singles and would be saddled with the tough-luck loss to drop to 7-5. But he lowered his season ERA to 2.96, and the veteran right-hander could be a top trade target for other teams should be Cubs decide to be sellers by next week.

Julian Merryweather, fresh off the injured list, came on to relieve Taillon in the eighth Tuesday night with a runner on third and one out. He struck out Garrett Mitchell looking, but with two outs Turang grounded a 1-2 pitch up the middle to plate Jake Bauers for the game's only run.

“Our pitchers were shutting them down,” Turang said. “I was just happy to get a run in.”

Trevor Megill worked the ninth for his 20th save and made a winner of Elvis Peguero (6-3), the second of four Milwaukee relievers.

Colin Rea went the first five innings for the Brewers, allowing four hits and two walks while striking out eight.

“It’s a good win,” Milwaukee manager Pat Murphy said. “We kept our poise.”

Cubs can't capitalize

Meanwhile, the Cubs had a number of chances to get on the scoreboard at least once Tuesday but couldn't come through.

They loaded the bases against Rea, all with two outs, in the bottom of the second, but Nico Hoerner grounded out to end the inning. The Cubs then put runners on first and second against Rea in the fifth, but Seiya Suzuki was called out on strikes to end that threat.

After the Brewers took a 1-0 lead in the top of the eighth, the Cubs had a golden opportunity in the bottom of the inning against Brewers reliever Jared Koenig.

They loaded the bases with two outs, but pinch-hitter David Bote's drive to center came up short of the wall and hung up in the air just long enough for Mitchell to catch it for the final out.

Then, in the ninth, after Swanson's leadoff single, Miles Mastrobuoni flied out to center, Pete Crow-Armstrong struck out swinging after Swanson stole second and Hoerner grounded out to second to end the game.

Hoerner had a pair of hits for the Cubs.

Trainer's room

Brewers: All-Star OF Christian Yelich left in the sixth with back tightness, and Murphy said he feared that Yelich would require a stint on the injured list. ... Koenig took a grounder from Hoerner off the back of his right thigh in the eighth but stayed on the mound after a visit from the training staff.

Up next

The Cubs send LHP Justin Steele (2-4, 3.07 ERA) to the mound for Wednesday’s series finale. Milwaukee is planning a bullpen game.

First pitch at Wrigley Field is scheduled for 1:20 p.m.
