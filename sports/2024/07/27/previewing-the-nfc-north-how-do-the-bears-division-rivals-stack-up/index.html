Previewing the NFC North: How do the Bears' division rivals stack up?

CHICAGO — While the Chicago Bears training camp is fully underway, the rest of the NFC North prepares for the 2024 regular season as well.

Are the Bears primed to win the division this year, or will they trail the pack? We surveyed reporters from around the Midwest who cover the Green Bay Packers, Detroit Lions and Minnesota Vikings to see how they stack up compared to Chicago.

Green Bay Packers

After Jordan Love gained his footing under center and the Green Bay Packers came up just short against the San Francisco 49ers in the NFC divisional round, Super Bowl aspirations are once again a realistic goal for Packer faithful, according to FOX 6 Milwaukee's Lily Zhao.

"I believe this is a team that's confident," Zhao said. "This is a team that coming into last season, then getting into the playoffs would have met my expectations, but they far exceeded that with how they played against Dallas and San Francisco."

The Packers finished 9-8 a year ago and snuck into the playoffs as the NFC's final wild card team, thanks in large part to a decisive win over Chicago at the end of the regular season.

In the first round of the playoffs, Green Bay hung 48 points on a stunned Dallas Cowboys squad and advanced to face the 49ers, who forced a late fourth quarter interception of Love to preserve a 24-21 win in the NFC Divisional Round.

"This is a team under Matt LeFleur that were talking about [the] Super Bowl before they got taken out by the 49ers last year," Zhao said. "That potential was there so, the thought is now, lets now get to that level and higher this season."

When it comes to the strength of the Packers roster, new defensive coordinator Jeff Hafley has yet to prove the value of his unit so, the offense will look to be the bread and butter of their 2024 season.

"Right now, the strength of this team is offense. Lets see them give this defense a couple weeks to meld and figure out how they want to attack the quarterback, and lets put some points on the board," Zhao said.

Zhao pointed toward a young, but talented receiver room — Featuring the likes of Christian Watson, Romeo Dobbs, Jayden Reed and Dontayvion Wicks — that could find success through the air when paired with a tight end room that features Luke Musgrave and Tucker Kraft.

The only caveat being Musgrave and Kraft avoid the injuries that kept them off the field in 2023.

"With what they were able to do with their wide receivers and tight ends, the health kind of wasn't there for both Tucker Kraft and Luke Musgrave, their tight ends, to be on the field at the same time," Zhao said. "You have those guys coming back healthy and you have a really deep receiving core.

"What they were able to do against Dallas, dropping 48 points, was kind of what we expected this Packers offense could eventually get to, and they got there in the playoffs so, right now the strength of this team is offense."

With the departures of fan favorites like Aaron Jones and David Bakhtiari, the relative lack of experience across the roster could be perceived as a weakness, but after Love and his receivers got on the same page around mid-season, Zhao believes it's only a question of how much better they can become, despite how different the locker room is, compared to what fans are used to.

"You had a learning curve the first half of [last] season, but these guys figured things out together, and they're doing so with such a young team," Zhao said. "If we saw just what they were able to do — The youngest team in the playoffs [last year], how far they got and how much farther they could've got — You see that infusion of youth.

"[It's] just so different with so many young guys. I think the median age was 24 years old so, i's an infusion of youth, but these guys aren't playing like your typical rookies or second year guys."

Zhao's NFC North Prediction

The FOX 6 Milwaukee sports reporter said she could see a world where any combination of the Lions, Packers and Bears could finish first through third in the NFC North.

"It's such a tough question because I feel like you can go one-two-three with the Lions, the Bears and the Packers," Zhao said. "That's nothing against the Vikings. You're looking at the different teams, and the Lions and Packers finished one and two, and I don't think that's a far cry from where things could potentially end up again, but the Bears are the 'X' factor.

"I theoretically could see Packers-Bears-Lions or Lions-Packers-Bears, or any of that amalgamation, which makes it fun, but I think in Packer Land they want Green Bay-Detroit-Minnesota-Chicago, but I don't think it's going to end up that way."

Detroit Lions

If you talk to WOOD TV8's Alec Giannakopoulos, there's nothing but sunshine and positivity when looking toward the skyline of the Detroit Lions' aspirations for the 2024 NFL season.

"There's nothing but great vibes in Detroit and for Lions fans," Giannakopoulos said.

The Lions finished atop the division last year with a 12-5 record before beating the Los Angeles Rams and Tampa Bay Buccaneers in the first two rounds of the playoffs.

Detroit ended up falling short in the NFC Championship Game against San Francisco, 31-34.

Going into the offseason, the one glaring need the Lions had to address was their secondary, and address it, they did.

Detroit traded for cornerback Carlton Davis III and months later in the 2024 NFL Draft, the team spent their top two draft picks on Alabama's Terrion Arnold and Missouri's Ennis Rakestraw Jr.

Elsewhere, the Lions locked down the cornerstones of their offense, kept their top coordinators in house, and bolstered their defensive line.

On offense, Detroit signed quarterback Jared Goff, right tackle Penei Sewell and wide receiver Amon Ra St. Brown to handsome contract extensions that made them some of the highest paid players at their positions across the NFL.

The Lions also kept both offensive coordinator Ben Johnson and defensive coordinator Aaron Glenn on their coaching staff, despite the two fielding multiple head coaching offers over the offseason.

"I'm surprised he came back for a second year. Personally, I thought Ben Johnson was gone after his standout year [during his first year], which was two years ago," Giannakopoulos said. "So, for the second straight offseason cycle, Ben Johnson stayed in Detroit … He said he wanted to see this through, he wanted to see Detroit go all the way to a championship.

"I think from the outside looking in, he also knows he can wait for a pretty good spot. he doesn't need to overly analyze or jump at the first head coaching job available. He knows he's the hottest candidate."

On the defensive line, Detroit added nose tackle D.J. Reader and edge rusher Marcus Davenport to bolster an already strong run defense, and if the secondary improves from last season, Giannakopoulos believes there could be improved numbers when it comes to their pass rush as well.

"If there is a weakness on this defense, outside of maybe the inexperienced cornerback room, it is that there's no 'B' guy to what [Aidan] Hutchinson can do on the edge," Giannakopoulos said. "I think that might be the one lone spot where fans aren't crazy about.

"If you have cornerbacks you can trust to stick to receivers, that allows that new, revamped defensive line to maybe get more pressure."

The only other question mark Giannakopoulos brought up was the No. 2 wide receiver role in the Lions offense, which he said many expect Jameson Williams to claim as he embarks on his third season in the NFL — A season that should go a long way toward determining his future in the league.

"This is the last year that Lions fans will be patient with him so, this is do or die for him," Giannakopoulos said. "If he can make that step and become the Robin to the Batman of Amon Ra St. Brown, I don't really see anywhere you can attack this offense."

Giannakopoulos' NFC North Prediction

The WOOD TV8 sports reporter said he would take the Lions, Packers, Bears and Vikings, in that order.

“It’s going to be hard to bet against Detroit. Locking in three impactful players in Goff, Sewell, and St. Brown will help, but returning highly-coveted offensive coordinator Ben Johnson ensures every piece that made 2023 special is still in place," Giannakopoulos said. "It’s tempting to put a Jordan Love-led packers squad in first. They were a team that found their stride at the end of the year.

"A smaller sample size of success mixed with opposing defenses now having a year of tape on him has me balking a touch."

"The Bears had one of the most exciting off-seasons in football, but I imagine it’ll take time for those pieces on offense gel," Giannakopoulos added. "Their best route to the division is a late run by a talented rookie quarterback who’s always kept in games by a sticky secondary. 

"The Vikings have too many questions to rank any higher in the division. We’re not sure when National Champion J.J. McCarthy will lead this team. Paired with issues on the defensive end, and the Vikings seem to use 2024 to find their direction in a post-Kirk Cousins world.”

Minnesota Vikings

According to former Minnesota Vikings linebacker and radio analyst Pete Bercich, the center of the Vikings offense is quarterback heavy, leaving a lot of uncertainty when it comes to the success of the franchise for the first time in years, after they finished tied for last in the division with the Bears in 2023 at 7-10.

"Kevin O'Connell, he was a NFL quarterback, he's a quarterback coach, an offensive coordinator and now a head coach," Bercich said. "He depends a ton — And that offense depends a ton — On the quarterback and the adjustments that they can make."

Bercich said Sam Darnold, who joined Minnesota over the offseason after signing a one-year, $10 million deal with the team, should have every opportunity to be the Vikings' starting quarterback come September once regular season games get underway, but that will come with a short leash from the fanbase.

"Ideally for the Vikings, they have Darnold get through this season depending on how well it goes," Bercich said. "Of course, first game we lose, fans are going to want [J.J.] McCarthy, but that's just how it is. He's the most popular guy in town."

After a rough go-around to begin his career with the New York Jets and the Carolina Panthers, Bercich sees a better foundation and supporting cast for Darnold in Minnesota, which he hopes unlocks improved play for the journeyman quarterback.

When it comes to the rest of the offense, If there's anything the Vikings can look to rely on, it's their group of wide receivers.

Between Justin Jefferson and Jordan Addison, in addition to the receiving threat posed by tight end T.J. Hockenson, Bercich believes Minnesota's receivers offer the most certainty when it comes to pulling their weight on the Vikings roster.

"[Jefferson] works hard. That's what makes him who he is," Bercich said. "Everybody has talent, but Justin Jefferson wants to be great. That's his motivation, and now that he's gotten paid with a new contract, what he's worried about is his legacy and winning a Super Bowl."

If Minnesota wants to win games in 2024, Bercich said they will need to see improvement from their running game, where they signed former Packers running back Aaron Jones over the offseason, and rely on their defensive backs to help carry the defense in a division that has become quarterback-heavy across the board.

"Last year our running game was pretty abysmal," Bercich said. "Especially on short yardage, third-and-one, fourth-and-one, and deep in the red zone. So, Aaron Jones, that's kind of his forte from what we've seen over the years ... When you need two, he'll get you three, and when you need five, he'll get you [at least] three. He can bust out those tough yards.

"Defensively, it's going to come down to the defensive backs," Bercich said. "You have Akayleb Evans and CJ Booth. Byron Murphy will be back. He's really good ... That DB group needs to get better, we got to get better situationally running the football, and we have to hold on to the football when we're on offense."

Bercich's NFC North Prediction

Bercich said he hates the Packers, which means by default, he believes the Lions will likely win the division again, with the Bears and Vikings duking it out for second and third place.

"I would pick the Green Bay Packers to finish last because I hate the Packers, I can't stand 'em," Bercich said. "Nobody in Minnesota hates the Packers — Or maybe in the country — Hates the Packers more than I do so, I'd pick them to finish last, which means Detroit might have to win this whole thing again."

"When you go to Ford Field, they're actually loud. It gets loud there when they pack that place in, and then maybe it'll be between [Chicago and Minnesota] for third place."
