Utes open training camp loaded with talent

SALT LAKE CITY (ABC4 Sports) - After missing all of last season, Utes quarterback Cam Rising was all smiles in his first day of fall camp.

"It was a great day," said Rising, who is finally healthy after a knee injury he suffered at the Rose Bowl in January, 2023. "It's always good to be back with the team. Everybody looks good flying around. We've still got some stuff to clean up, but it's just the first day."

Now in his seventh year of college football, Rising is not quite back at the form that made him the Pac-12 Offensive Player of the year in 2022, but he'll get there.

"Yeah, I'm still kind of getting it back," Rising said. "I definitely feel 100 percent, and I'm just trying to make sure I get some more cuts and stuff like that at high speed. I just want to start jumping a little bit more and try to just move around, but it's nice to be back."

In order to keep Rising healthy this year, offensive coordinator said don't expect Rising to run the ball as much as he has in the past.

"Absolutely," Ludwig said. "Designed quarterback runs will be limited limited, if any, but he's a smart guy and knows that he wants to play for a long time and play in every game this season."

Tight end Brant Kuithe is also back after missing the last year and a half with a knee injury of his own.

"I kind of just went out there and just ran with my eyes closed and catch the ball," Kuithe said. "But it's great just being able to be out there with [Rising] again. It's our last season, it's exciting and we just have to take advantage of it."

Kuithe is one of several weapons Rising will have, that includes transfer wide receivers Dorian Singer, Damian Alford and Taeshaun Lyons.

"I definitely think we have a top ten receiver room in the country," said Singer, who previously played at USC and Arizona. It may not look like that on paper, but if you come on and watch the guys work, you can definitely see."

Singer and Rising are already building a strong bond on and off the field.

"The chemistry is great," Singer said. "We hang out outside of football, do fun activities, stuff like that. I feel like I can do anything with him because he's been in a system like this, a pro-style offense, and he knows the ins and outs."

The transfer portal has brought the Utes a lot of new players, so it may take some time to gel.

"We do have almost a third of the team that's brand new," said head coach Kyle Whittingham, who is beginning his 20th season at the helm. "We had a team meeting last night, and we had all the guys that are new to the program stand up, and there was about 35 to 40 guys there. So it's a big group of new guys that need to learn how we do things here."

Running back is the biggest question on the team, but Whittingham feels it will all get sorted out between Micah Bernard, Jaylon Glover, Mike Mitchell and Charlie Vincent.

"We have enough live work to to determine the pecking order," Whittingham said. "Right now Micah Bernard is at the top of that order, but we've got a bunch of other guys that are going to be fighting for playing time."

"Sharing this caring," added running backs coach Quinton Ganther. "What I will say, is if there's a guy who just takes over and he wants to become that alpha male, I'm not going to stop him from doing so. But until then, if somebody steps forward and take their spot, then we'll share it."

On defense led by Connor O'Toole and Junior to few now the defensive line may be the deepest it's ever been, and that's saying something.

"I'm very excited about my room," said defensive line coach Luther Elliss. "I'm very excited about the competition that's going to take place starting today, and it's been going on all summer. But, with practices and things of that nature, what's happening today will set the tone for the whole year."

Defensive coordinator Morgan Scalley is excited about the depth of the defense, and he doesn't really want to talk about that "head coach in waiting" status right now.

"The biggest thing is them understanding culture and how important it's been in this to this program and what Kyle has been able to do," Scalley said. "Just understanding that this is a special place, I'm obviously grateful that they have faith in me to continue coaching here. But the bottom line is, I'm a defensive coordinator right now."

The Utes kick off the season August 29th at home against Southern Utah.
