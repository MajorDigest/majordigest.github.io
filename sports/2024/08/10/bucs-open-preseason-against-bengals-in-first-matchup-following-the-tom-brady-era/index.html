As it happened: Buccaneers cage Cincinnati Bengals 17-14 in preseason opener

TAMPA, Fla. (WFLA) -- The Tampa Bay Buccaneers caged the Cincinnati Bengals in their preseason opener. It was the first time the teams went head-to-head following the Tom Brady era.

The tides have shifted since the Bengals defeated then-quarterback Brady and the Buccaneers 34-23 at Raymond James Stadium in 2022. The beginning of the Brady vs. Burrow showdown looked promising for the Bucs, but the team crumbled after halftime, allowing Cincinnati to take control.

Saturday's preseason opener showcased Bucs' quarterback Kyle Trask at the helm with much of the team's rookies getting a taste of what's to come this season.

Meanwhile, some of the Bengals starters, such as quarterback Joe Burrow and wide receiver Tee Higgins started the game.

While the Bengals scored first with a Higgins touchdown, the Buccaneers answered as rookie running back Bucky Irving secured the team's first preseason touchdown. Kicker Chase McLaughlin nailed a 35-yard field goal to extend the Bucs' lead by 3 points before Cincinnati came back with a late touchdown in the fourth quarter.

Bengals' Burton scored on a 37-yard touchdown, but it didn't take long for the Bucs to reclaim the lead and finish out the matchup on top with a 3-yard touchdown by Jefferson.
