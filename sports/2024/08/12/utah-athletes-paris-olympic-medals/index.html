How did Utah fare at the 2024 Paris Olympic Games?

SALT LAKE CITY (ABC4) -- After two and half weeks of spirited competition, filled with heartbreak and triumph, the Paris 2024 Summer Olympic Games came to a close.

Utah was represented by more than three dozen athletes with ties to the Beehive State. Twenty-five of those athletes wore the red, white, and blue on Team USA and eight were born in Utah. So how did Utah's athletes do during the games?

Overall, Utah-tied athletes earned three silver medals and four bronze for a total tally of seven Olympic medals. Utah natives earned two of those medals with one silver and one bronze.

Utah isn't finished competing on an international stage either. More Utahns, such as Ali Ibanez, will be competing in the Paris 2024 Summer Paralympic Games starting on Wednesday, Aug. 28.

A full breakdown of each Utah athlete can be found below. Those marked with a "*" earned a medal in their sport.

Utah Natives

Haley Batten* - Park City, UT

Haley Batten claimed one of Team USA's 44 silver medals after a spectacular race during the Women's Cross Country Mountain Bike Cycling event.

Batten was in a heated battle for the podium with Sweden's Jenny Rissveds, beating her after seven laps with a five-second split. Batten raced the full 19 miles of the cross-county event and finished with a time of 1 hour, 28 minutes, 59 seconds to claim the silver medal. She was 2 minutes and 57 seconds behind gold medalist Paulin Ferrand Prevot of France.

This was Batten's second appearance in the Olympics having competed in the Tokyo Games. Batten didn't medal in Japan, finishing with a time of 1 hour, 20 minutes, and 13 seconds.

Taylor Booth - Eden, UT

Taylor Booth joined the U.S. Men's Soccer Team in the first Olympic soccer appearance for the Stars and Stripes since the Beijing Games in 2008. The U.S. Men's team played four games overall before being eliminated in the quarter-finals courtesy of a 4-0 rout from eventual bronze-medalist Morocco.

Booth appeared in all four matches, coming into each game as a second-half substitution. Overall, Booth played 83 minutes in his Olympic debut.

Conner Matnz - Logan, UT

While Conner Mantz didn't leave Paris with a medal hanging from his neck, his performance during the Men's Marathon in his first-ever Olympic appearance is nothing to be forgotten.

Mantz put in a season-best time of 2 hours, 8 minutes, and 12 seconds to finish 8th overall in the race. His time was just 1 minute and 46 seconds behind the first-place finisher Tamirat Tola of Ethiopia and just 1 minute and 12 seconds behind the podium.

Whittni Orton Morgan - Panguitch, Utah

Whittni Morgan did nothing but improve in both of her races during the 2024 Olympic Games. Morgan first competed in the second heat of the Women's 5000m qualifiers, finishing 6th in the race with a season-best time of 15 minutes and 2 seconds. By finishing in the Top 8, Morgan was automatically qualified to race in the final for the gold medal.

While Morgan didn't end the final with a medal, she set a new personal best in the race with a time of 14 minutes and 57 seconds. Morgan finished in the final 14th overall, 25 seconds behind gold medalist Beatrice Chebet of Kenya.

Alex "Spiff" Sedrick* - Herriman, UT

"Spiff" Sedrick made Team USA Rugby history with a bronze medal finish at the Paris 2024 Summer Olympic Games.

In the dying seconds of the bronze-medal match against Australia, Sedrick placed the game-winning try by plowing through the Australian line and running the full length of the field. Her heroic play put the United States in the lead over Australia, 14-12, to claim Team USA's first-ever medal in Women's Rugby Sevens.

Overall, Sedrick scored 17 points for Team USA with three tries throughout the Rugby Sevens tournament.

Maka Unufe - Provo, UT

Maka Unufe helped lift Team USA's Men's Rugby Sevens team to an 8th-place finish overall in the Paris 2024 Olympic Games tournament. The team made it through to the quarter-finals round where they dropped an 18-0 result to Australia. The team then fell in both of its placement rounds against Ireland and Argentina to finish the tournament.

Unufe played in five of Team USA's six Men's Rugby Sevens matches, earning a record of one win, one draw, and three losses. This was Unufe's second Olympic appearance, having played with the Men's Rugby team during the 2016 Olympic Games in Brazil. During those Games, Team USA ranked 9th in the tournament.

Courtney Wayment - Layton, UT

Courtney Wayment competed in the Women's 3000m Steeplechase where she qualified for the final in a tight finish during the qualifiers.

Wayment first raced in the first heat of the steeplechase qualifiers, finishing 4th with a time of 9 minutes and 10 seconds. Her time guaranteed her spot as one of five qualifiers from the qualifying heat to compete for the gold medal in the final.

In the final, Wayment would place 12th overall with a time of 9 minutes and 13 seconds. She was 21 seconds behind Winfred Yavi of Bahrain, who finished first for the gold medal. Wayment was only 18 seconds behind bronze medalist Faith Cherotich of Kenya.

Clayton Young - American Fork, UT

Provo-resident and American Fork High School alumni Clayton Young had an impressive run in the Men's Marathon. Alongside teammate and friend Conner Mantz, Young finished 9th in the race overall with a season-best time of 2 hours, 8 minutes, and 44 seconds. Young was 2 minutes and 18 seconds behind the gold medalist from Ethiopia, Tamirat Tola, and 1 minute and 44 seconds behind the podium.

Team USA Athletes with Utah ties

James Corrigan - BYU Cross Country and Track & Field

Provo-resident James Corrigan made his Olympic debut racing in the Men's 3000m Steeplechase for Team USA. Corrigan raced in the first heat of the qualifying round but unfortunately failed to qualify for the final race for the gold medal.

Corrigan finished 10th overall with a time of 8 minutes and 36 seconds, 18 seconds behind the final qualifying position.

Colin Duffy - USA Climbing based in Salt Lake City

Colorado native Colin Duffy competed in his second Olympic Games, having previously competed in Tokyo. Duffy qualified for the final having earned 33.8 in the boulder semifinal and a score of 87.9 in the lead semifinal.

In the final, Duffy scored 68.3 points in boulder and 68.1 points in lead for a combined total of 136.4, however, it was not enough to secure a medal at the event. Duffy finished 4th overall, only 3.2 points behind the podium and 18.8 points behind gold-medalist Toby Roberts of Great Britain.

Grant Fisher* - Utah's Run Elite Program

Park City resident Grant Fisher walked away from the 2024 Paris Olympic Games with not one, but two, bronze medals. The 27-year-old track and field athlete competed in the Men's 5000m race and the Men's 10,000m race, finishing 3rd overall in both.

In the 5000m qualifying heat, Grant finished with a time of 13 minutes and 52 seconds to finish among the Top 8 of the automatic qualifiers. He went on to the final race, keeping in the middle of the pack throughout the first 4000 meters. By the final lap, Fisher had moved up to third to finish with a time of 13 minutes and 15 seconds. He was only .09 seconds behind Kenya's Ronald Kwemoi who finished second and 1.47 seconds behind Norway's Jakob Ingebrigsten who claimed gold.

In the 10,000m race, Grant finished with a season-best time of 26 minutes and 43 seconds. Throughout the race, Grant kept pace among the leaders, at one point crossing the 6000m mark just a second behind the race leader. Grant earned his spot on the podium finish .02 seconds behind second place and .32 seconds behind the gold medal.

Natalia Grossman - USA Climbing based in Salt Lake City

Natalia Grossman, a Salt Lake City resident, just missed out on qualifying for the Boulder and Lead final during her Olympic debut at the Paris games.

Grossman scored 69.2 points in boulder which was paired with a 39.1 in lead for a combined total of 108.3 points. Her total put her 11th overall, eight points shy of ranking among the Top 8 to qualify for the final.

Jesse Grupper - USA Climbing based in Salt Lake City

Jesse Grupper made his Olympic debut competing in the Men's Climbing Boulder and Lead event. Grupper failed to move out of the semifinal after ranking 18th overall in both boulder and lead. In boulder, Grupper scored 18.9 while in lead, he scored 30.9.

Zach Hammer - USA Climbing based in Salt Lake City

Salt Lake City resident Zach Hammer made his Olympic debut during the 2024 Paris Games. He competed in the Men's Speed Climbing event where he finished 14th overall with arguably one of the toughest seedings possible.

Hammer finished the seeding round in 12th place with a time of 6.05 seconds. This put him up against fellow USA teammate and world-record holder Sam Watson in the elimination heats. Hammer would be undone in the next round by Watson after falling during his climb.

Chari Hawkins - Utah State University Track & Field

Utah State alumni Chari Hawkins competed in her first Olympic Women's Heptathlon - a track and field competition made up of seven different events - during the 2024 Paris Olympic Games. Overall, she ranked 21st in the competition with a combined score of 5255.

Hawkins made most of her points during the 100m Hurdles event, scoring 1100 points after a fifth-place finish. She made another fifth place during the long jump scoring an additional 819 points. Hawkins' Olympic hopes for a medal, however, were undone in the high jump event where she failed to post a score resulting in a "No Mark" earning zero points toward her combined score.

Emma Hunt - USA Climbing based in Salt Lake City

Georgia native Emma Hunt competed in her first Olympic Games, finishing fifth overall in the Women's Speed Climbing event during the 2024 Paris Olympic Games.

Hunt started the event off strong, posting a personal best time of 6.36 seconds in the seeding round. Hunt went on to beat France's Manon Lebon in the elimination heats with a time of 6.38 seconds versus Lebon's 7.07. Hunt would then be eliminated from the competition in the quarterfinal after posting a time of 7.98 against Indonesia's Rajiah Sallsabillah, who posted a personal best time of 6.54.

Piper Kelly - USA Climbing based in Salt Lake City

Salt Lake City resident Piper Kelly made her Olympic debut competing in the Women's Speed Climbing event. Her Olympics were unfortunately cut short, however, after she lost in the elimination round to Indonesia's Desak Made Made Rita Kusuma Dewi who placed a time of 6.38 seconds. Kelly had posted a time of 8.43.

Lexi Lagan - University of Utah alumni

Lexi Lagan, a Colorado native, made her second Olympic appearance competing in the Women's 10m Air Pistol. Through six rounds, Lagan earned a score of 570-16x, finishing 24th overall in an event with 45 competitors. Unfortunately, the score was not enough to qualify for the final round, ending Lagan's time in the Paris 2024 Olympic Games.

Brooke Raboutou* - USA Climbing based in Salt Lake City

Colorado native Brooke Raboutou claimed the silver medal in her Olympic debut during the Paris Games in the Women's Boulder and Lead event.

Raboutou qualified for the final with ease, scoring a combined 155.8 points with 83.7 points in Boulder and 72.1 points in Lead. Raboutou only improved in the final event against the seven other finalists.

In the final, Raboutou finished second in boulder with a score of 84 points and 5th in lead with a score of 72 points. The combined total of an even 156 points put Rabotou in second place overall to claim the silver medal. She was 8.5 points behind gold medalist Janja Garnbret of Slovenia and 8.6 points ahead of bronze medalist Jessica Pilz of Austria.

Kenneth Rooks* - BYU Cross Country and Track & Field

BYU alumni Kenneth Rooks shocked the world when he rallied for a second-place finish in the Men's 3000m Steeplechase. The Provo resident made his Olympic debut in Paris qualifying for the final race with a time of 8 minutes and 24 seconds during the third heat.

Rooks took a slow and steady approach in the final, sitting near the back of the pack by the time he surpassed the first 1000 meters. Rooks worked his way to the front of the pack in the next 1000 meters before making his explosive finish in the final lap.

Rooks recorded a personal best time of 8 minutes and 6 seconds to claim the silver medal. He was only .06 seconds in front of bronze-medalist Abraham Kibiwot of Kenya and .36 seconds behind gold-medalist Soufiane El Bakkali of Morocco.

Stephanie Rovetti* - BYU Basketball

From Nevada, Rovetti had ties with Utah when she came to BYU and played basketball. However, Rovetti was on the team that made Team USA history when she claimed the bronze medal in the Women's Rugby Sevens.

Rovetti played in all six games for the Team USA Rugby squad helping lift the team to the bronze medal finish. The 2024 Paris Olympic Games marked Rovetti's Olympic debut.

Sam Watson* -- USA Climbing based in Salt Lake City

At just 18 years old, Sam Watson had an unforgettable Olympic debut experience in Paris. The Texas native who trains for the Olympics with the USA Climbing team in Salt Lake City took home the bronze medal in Men's Speed Climbing and he did it while setting new records.

Watson first broke his own world record he set in April of 4.79 seconds by beating fellow American teammate Zach Hammer in the elimination heats with a time of 4.75 seconds. Watson then won the quarter-final with a time of 5.03 seconds before losing the semi-final match with a time of 4.93 seconds.

In the bronze-medal final, Watson broke his world record again with a time of 4.74 seconds to beat Iran's Reza Alipour Shenazandifard for the medal.

Athletes with Utah ties representing other nations

Paige Crozon - Canada - University of Utah basketball

Paige Crozon, who played on the University of Utah basketball team from 2012 to 2016, made an impressive Olympic debut with Team Canada in Women's 3x3 Basketball. Crozon played through all nine games Team Canada played in, scoring 39 points in the tournament.

Team Canada advanced through the pool round to reach the Play-in games. The team beat Australia before falling to Germany in the semi-final, 15-16. Crozon and Team Canada then played in the bronze medal match against the United States but lost 13-16.

Josefine Eriksen - Norway - University of Utah Track & Field

Josefine Eriksen entered her first Olympic Games racing in the 4x400m relay for Team Norway. The University of Utah track & field athlete would not advance out of the qualifying heats, posting a time of 3 minutes and 28 seconds. The time was nearly four seconds behind France, who took the last automatic qualifying spot.

Macey Fraser - New Zealand - Utah Royals

Macey Fraser, who joined the Utah Royals in April, competed in her first Olympic games as part of New Zealand's team in Women's Soccer. She only competed in one game, New Zealand's opening 2-1 loss against Canada, where she recorded 31 minutes as a second-half substitution

New Zealand would go on to lose its remaining two group stage matches to be eliminated from the competition.

Emilia Nilsson Garip - Sweden - University of Utah Swimming & Diving

In her first Olympic event, Emilia Nilsson Garip made an impressive run for gold in the Women's 3m Springboard. Garip had a Top 10 finish in the preliminary round with a point total of 295.2 to qualify for the semifinal. Garip advanced again into the final after posting a score of 279.6 to take one of the last two automatically qualifying positions.

In the final, Garip performed a series of somersaults and twists to score a total of 279.4. The score would rank Garip 9th overall, 39.35 points behind the podium and 96.6 points behind the gold medal.

Amandine Henry - France - Utah Royals

Amandine Henry, who joined the Utah Royals as a mid-season signing, starred in three games for the host nation, France. She came onto the field as a second-half substitution against Colombia and Canada and started in the win against New Zealand. She did not appear in the quarterfinal loss against Brazil that ended France's run for the Women's Soccer gold medal.

Rory Linkletter - Canada - Herriman High School, BYU Cross Country and Track & Field

Provo-resident Rory Linkletter competed in his first Olympic Games, racing in the Men's Marathon. Linkletter's run would not earn him any Olympic medals, finishing 47th overall in the race with a posted time of 2 hours, 13 minutes, and 9 seconds.

Ify Onumonu - Nigeria - Utah Royals

Ify Onumonu, who joined the Utah Royals in December 2023 ahead of the club's inaugural season, made her first Olympic appearance by playing with Team Nigeria in Women's Soccer.

Onumonu played 52 minutes in two games with Nigeria, coming on as a second-half substitution both times. Nigeria would suffer heartbreak in the group stages, losing all three games to be eliminated from the competition.

Michelle Plouffe - Canada - University of Utah basketball

Michelle Plouffe made her Olympic debut as part of Team Canada, competing in Women's 3x3 basketball. The former University of Utah basketball player played in all nine games for Team Canada, scoring 52 points throughout the tournament.

Team Canada advanced through the pool round to reach the Play-in games. The team beat Australia before falling to Germany in the semi-final, 15-16. Crozon and Team Canada then played in the bronze medal match against the United States but lost 13-16.

Simone Plourde - Canada - BYU, U of U Cross Country and Track & Field

Simone Plourde competed in her first Olympic Games, taking part in the Women's 1500m event. Plourde suffered her first setback, however, after she failed to qualify for the final in Heat 2. Plourde posted a time of 4 minutes and 6 seconds, 4 seconds off from automatically qualifying for the gold medal final.

Plourde had a chance for a comeback in the Repechage event but she narrowly missed the Top 3 finish needed to qualify. In the Repechage, Plourde posted a time of 4 minutes and 8 seconds, just a second behind the third-place finisher.

Mina Tanaka - Japan - Utah Royals

Mina Tanaka, one of the Utah Royals' newest members, was a bright spot for Team Japan in Women's Soccer during the 2024 Paris Olympic Games.

Tanaka started in all four games for Japan, playing a total of 285 minutes. In that time, she took eight shots on goal, five of which were on target. Tanaka netted one goal during the 3-1 win over Nigeria and helped Japan advance out of the group stages and into the quarterfinal.

Tanaka and Team Japan would not go further than the quarterfinal, playing a narrow game against the United States. The U.S. would win the match, 1-0, and eventually claim the gold medal.
