Angel Reese and the Chicago Sky set sights on second half, with visions of Los Angeles Games down the road

CHICAGO — With the WNBA All-Star Break in the rearview mirror and Team USA back home triumphant after a gold medal performance, Angel Reese has her eyes set on some lofty goals for herself and her teammates heading into the second half of the season.

To recap, the Chicago Sky went into the all-star break 10-14, with Reese being selected as the team's lone representative for the 2024 WNBA All-Star Game.

In typical Reese fashion, she walked away from the contest with a double-double (12 points and 11 rebounds on 5-10 shooting), and added another accomplishment to what has already been an impressive freshman campaign for her — Becoming the first rookie to record a double-double in a WNBA all-star game.

This came after she averaged 15.3 points and 12.2 rebounds per game in a streak of 15 consecutive double-doubles in the first half of the season — Also a WNBA record.

Once WNBA all-star festivities wrapped up, Reese flew to Paris and watched Team USA compete for gold against France.

"I want to first congratulate France, they put up a fight. The crowd in there was amazing too. That atmosphere was crazy," Reese said of the gold medal game. "I saw the officiating and I was like, 'Wow, they play through a lot.' So, I'm really happy for them that they could actually bring home gold, which is really cool. I always admired a lot of those women so, it's exciting to see."

The experience of taking in Team USA's 67-66 victory in the gold medal match cemented a goal for Reese to pursue four years from now, when the Summer Games return to the United States in 2028.

"I want to be on that 2028 team in LA, and just being able to be a part of that, watching those little things and what it takes to be an Olympian — What it takes to get that gold medal — It wasn't easy for them," Reese said.

"I love everything to be hard, and I know a lot of those women do that, just being able to watch and have a good relationship with a few of them [and] just being able to talk to them about it. I'm excited for them to get back and hear a lot of things about it."

With the first half of the WNBA season and the Paris Games in the books, Reese said her personal goals for the second half are centered around consistency, and performing at a high level when her team needs her to shine.

"Just continue to be consistent with what my team needs me to do," Reese told WGN's Kaitlin Sharkey in an exclusive one-on-one interview Monday. "Rebounding is something that I love to do and what I'm really good at, but just being able to finish around the rim. Being able to guard my matchup and be able to switch one-through-five. Just continue to do that.

"I watched a lot of film over the break so, just being able to tweak a couple things. I think I did do a lot of great things in the first half, but just being able to continue that."

While Reese is focused on taking the next step in her game on the court, she wasn't the only headliner for the Sky through their first 24 games.

As the LSU alum will proudly tell you, guard Chennedy Carter played at an all-star level in the first half, forming a dynamic 1-2 punch on the offensive end with Reese, and when the two were at the top of their game, the Sky could compete with any team in the league.

"I've been watching Chennedy since she was at Texas A&M. I tweeted out a long time ago when she was at A&M that she's a dog like she's been a dog," Reese said. "I've always admired that about her. I love players who go out there and play with something on their shoulders. You can tell Chennedy plays with a chip on her shoulders, and I love that, being her teammate."

Carter is amid a career renaissance of sorts, averaging 17.2 points, the most she's averaged since her rookie season in 2020 (17.4), and a career high 1.3 steals per game.

"To go out there every single night, sometimes you just get out the way," Reese said. "You get out of Chennedy's way, and we just have that great relationship because we understand each other. I can get on her and she can get on me.

"I'm super excited for Chennedy. She deserves this more than anybody else, and I know her second half is going to be even better than the first half."

Other headlines included Sky general manager Jeff Pagliocca shaking up the roster by trading Marina Mabrey to the Connecticut Sun, netting Chicago a pair of veterans Reese is excited to see join the roster.

"We had two great vets that came into this program from another great team and the chemistry has already continued to build, as you've probably seen on social media," Reese said. "It's been great being able to have those two vets, but I know we're going to just continue to grow and get better every day."

On July 17, The Sky sent Mabrey, the team's second-leading scorer at the time, and a second round pick to Connecticut in a blockbuster trade that landed Chicago Rachel Banham, Moriah Jefferson, a 2025 first round pick, and the right to swap first round picks in the 2026 WNBA Draft.

Jefferson, a guard drafted second overall in the 2016 WNBA Draft by the San Antonio Stars, has played nine games this year amid knee injury complications. Across those nine games, Jefferson has averaged 2.1 points per game on 37.5% shooting, and 33.3% shooting from the 3-point line.

In 188 career games in the WNBA, Jefferson has averaged 9.1 points, 2 rebounds and 3.5 assists per game, while shooting 44.1% from the field, and 39.6% from three.

Two picks later in the same draft, Banham — A guard out of Minnesota — Was selected by the Sun. In 21 games this season, Banham has averaged 4.8 points and 1.2 rebounds 35.8% shooting from the field, and 34.8% from three, while also averaging 90% from the free throw line.

Across her career, Banham has averaged 5.2 points per game on 39.1% shooting and 36.9% shooting from 3-point land, which also recently included her coming off the bench to hit eight 3-pointers in a 27-point win over the Phoenix Mercury on July 14 — A WNBA single-game record for a player coming off the bench.

"Moriah [Jefferson] is a veteran point guard. I watched her when she was at UConn so, I already know what she's bringing in. Big Latto, aka Rachel [Banham], [is] our sharpshooter," Reese said. So, I'm excited for both of them. They give me a lot of insight of things they've had — Not even just on the court, but [also] off the court.

"They always ask me, how are you being treated? And they said, I got lucky because, luckily, I have great vets here. They didn't have that their rookie years. They give me a lot of insight off the court too, which is really cool [and] really funny too so, I'm just happy to have them on the team."

With a slightly revamped roster after the trade, Chicago sits in the No. 8 spot in the WNBA playoff standings, and while Reese said the Sky are happy with the progress they've made so far, she also said they know what they need to do to take the next step toward qualifying for the playoffs at season's end.

"I think we did a great job leaving off in [Las] Vegas. We're not satisfied with where we are, but [we'll] just continue to grow and get better," Reese said. "I think we know where we like the ball. I know where we want the ball [with] our strengths and weaknesses, and I think that's important."

Tip-off is set for 7 p.m. CT tonight between the Chicago Sky (10-14) and Phoenix Mercury (13-12) at Wintrust Arena.
