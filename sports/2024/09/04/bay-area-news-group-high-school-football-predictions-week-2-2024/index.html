Bay Area News Group high school football predictions: Week 2, 2024

Serra or De La Salle? St. Francis or McClymonds? Liberty or Los Gatos?

The Bay Area News Group’s high school crew is back for another week of football predictions.

Christian Babcock, Nathan Canilao and Joseph Dycus join long-timers Mike Lefkow and Darren Sabedra to see who can pick the most games correctly each week.

All in good fun, of course.

In our video roundtable, the crew discussed Serra’s chances of beating De La Salle. 

Coming off a stunning 22-21 victory at powerhouse Folsom, Serra has an opportunity to reclaim its spot atop the Bay Area pecking order if it takes down the Spartans in Concord. 

If Serra is looking for doubters to provide motivation, all they’ll need to do is watch the pick ‘em show. For the second week in a row, all five panelists picked against the Padres. 

“You know, Patrick Walsh said ‘Never doubt those Serra Padres,’ but that’s exactly what I’m gonna do,” said Canilao, who covered the upset at Folsom.  

“If Serra wins this game, they need to get everyone on the team a subscription to the Mercury News (and East Bay Times), because we’re giving them an endless supply of bulletin board material at this point,” Babcock added. 

De La Salle’s experience could be a determining factor. The Concord school has lost each of the past two meetings to the Padres, but Serra had to replace 18 of the 22 starters from last year’s 28-0 decision. 

“I think that if De La Salle is going to get back to being the top dog in NorCal – they’ve got a veteran team with a veteran quarterback, quick running backs and are good on the line — if they don’t get Serra this time, I’m not sure when they’re going to get them,” Sabedra said. 

The show wasn’t all predictions and bluster, though. 

Bay Area high school football historian Mike Lefkow remembered the late Steve Alameda, the longtime Kennedy-Richmond and Pinole Valley coach who died this past weekend. 

“He was one of the great coaches in West Contra Costa County,” Lefkow said. “He had some really good players and some really good teams, and you know, he was just a nice person.”
