New US CIO appointments, March 2024

Movers & Shakers is where you can keep up with new CIO appointments and gain valuable insight into the job market and CIO hiring trends. As every company becomes a technology company, CEOs and corporate boards are seeking multi-dimensional CIOs with superior skills in technology, communications, business strategy, and digital innovation. The role is more challenging than ever before — but even more exciting and rewarding! If you have CIO job news to share, please email me!

Ruchir Swarup appointed CIO at KKR

KKR

KKR is a global investment firm that offers alternative asset management as well as capital markets and insurance solutions. Prior to joining KKR, Swarup was at Addepar, and held a lengthy career at BlackRock. He earned a BE from Pune Institute of Computer Technology.

Crown Castle welcomes Ed Chan as CIO

Crown Castle

Crown Castle is a provider of wireless communication infrastructure services. The company owns, manages, and leases towers, fiber solutions, and small cell networks. Chan arrives at Crown Castle after a long career with Verizon. He holds an MSEE from Rutgers University and an MSE from the University of Pennsylvania.

Maximus names Derrick Pledger CDIO

Maximus

Maximus partners with state, federal, and local governments to provide communities with critical health and human service programs. Pledger joins Maxims from a lengthy career at Leidos. He is a US Army veteran who served multiple tours of duty overseas in Iraq, Afghanistan, and the Horn of Africa. Pledger holds a BA from Virginia Tech.

Jim Bossio appointed CIO of Columbia University Irving Medical Center

Columbia University Irving Medical Center

Columbia University Irving Medical Center (CUIMC) is a clinical, research, and educational enterprise located on a campus in northern Manhattan, NY. Bossio arrived at CUIMC in July 2023 as interim CIO. He was previously at Columbia University Information Technology since 2014. Bossio earned a BBA, MIS from Iona University.

Brinker International announces Chris Caldwell as CIO

Brinker International

Founded in 1975 in Dallas, TX, Brinker International is a casual dining restaurant company and home of Chili’s Grill & Bar, Maggiano’s Little Italy, and virtual brand: It’s Just Wings. They own, operate or franchise more than 1,600 restaurants in 30 countries and two US territories. Caldwell had a lengthy career at Yum! Brands, most recently at KFC. He earned a BS from the University of Louisville.

Talal Butt appointed CIO at Generac

Generac

Founded in 1959, Generac Power Systems is an energy technology company that provides backup and prime power products and energy storage systems for home and business applications, as well as energy monitoring and management devices and services, along with other power products. Butt had prior roles with Rockwell Automation, Robert W. Baird, Covance, Inc. and General Electric HealthCare. He holds a BSc from University of Wisconsin Oshkosh.

The Wendy’s Company Names Matt Spessard CIO

The Wendy’s Company

Founded in 1969, The Wendy’s Company is a restaurant chain with thousands of restaurants across the globe. Spessard joined Wendy’s in May 2020 and most recently served as SVP, global CTO. He had prior roles at Sonic, YUM! Brands, Church’s Chicken, and Braum’s Ice Cream & Dairy Stores.

Mike Flanagan rejoins Compucom as CIO

Compucom

Compucom sources, integrates and supports workplace and advanced technology solutions for enterprise organizations. Prior to rejoining Compucom, Flanagan had roles at Computacenter and Pivot Tehcnology Solutions. He previously spent more than two decades at Compucom.

University of Tennessee Medical Center names Lynnette Clinton CIO

University of Tennessee Medical Center

Located in Knoxville, TN, The University of Tennessee Medical Center provides patient-centered care and remains at the forefront of research, technology, and treatments. Clinton arrives at UT Medical Center from BayCare Health System and held a prior role at Lehigh Valley Health Network. She holds a BS from Albright College and an MS from The University of Tampa. She’s also earned a Certified Healthcare CIO (CHCIO) designation, as well as Certified Digital Health Executive (CDH-E).

Doug Saunders appointed CIO of Arctic Glacier

Arctic Glacier

Arctic Glacier was founded 140 years ago, and currently produces and delivers more than 2.5 billion pounds of ice annually to supermarkets, mass merchants, c-stores, dollar stores, gas stations, and liquor stores, as well other commercial and industrial businesses. Saunders comes to Arctic Glacier from Sweeping Corporation of America. He holds a BA and MS from The University of Alabama.

Sumit Johar joins BlackLine as CIO

BlackLine

Founded in 2001, BlackLine is a provider of cloud software that automates and controls financial close and accounting processes. Johar most recently held a position at Automation Anywhere. He held prior roles at MobileIron, Tibco Software, Wipro, and Infosys. Johar earned a B Tech from the National Institute of Technology Kurukshetra.

Montrose Environmental Group appoints John Jackson CIO

Montrose Environmental Group

Montrose is an environmental solutions company focused on supporting commercial and government organizations with over 2,800 employees across more than 80 locations around the world. Jackson had prior positions as Aerojet Rocketdyne, Pratt & Whitney, and Tronox. He earned a BS from John Jay College (CUNY) and completed the executive leadership program at the University of Virginia, Darden School of Business.

Barrett Schiwitz named CIO at Basware

Basware

Basware is a global company specializing in AP automation, developing and integrating AI solutions to deliver benefits across the invoice processing cycle, including invoice capture, data extraction and PO matching. Schiwitz held prior roles at Snap One, Travelport, and NCR Corporation. He earned a BS from Florida Southern College.
