New US CIO appointments, July 2024

Movers & Shakers is where you can keep up with new CIO appointments and gain valuable insight into the job market and CIO hiring trends. As every company becomes a technology company, CEOs and corporate boards are seeking multi-dimensional CIOs and IT leaders with superior skills in technology, communications, business strategy, and digital innovation. The role is more challenging than ever before — but even more exciting and rewarding! If you have CIO job news to share, please email me!

BNY names Leigh-Ann Russell as CIO

BNY

Founded in 1784, BNY is a global financial services company. Their clients include more than 90% of the companies in the Fortune 100. Russell arrives at BNY after a lengthy career at bp. She earned a BS from the University of Aberdeen. 

Charles (Rusty) Patel joins Baxter as CIO

Baxter

Baxter is a healthcare solutions company, and their products and therapies can be found throughout hospitals and clinics — from the ER to the OR, from the pharmacy to the ICU — as well as advancing patients’ care in their homes. Patel joined Baxter from Tenneco and held prior roles at Johnson Controls, Tyco, VWR International, Motorola, A.T. Kearney, and EDS Delphi Automotive Systems. He earned a BS from Marquette University and an MBA from the University of Notre Dame.

Clayco names Jeff Miller as CIO

Clayco

Clayco is a full-service, turnkey real estate, architecture, engineering, design-build and construction firm. Miller arrives at Clayco from Quantinuum and held a prior role at Honeywell. He served in numerous roles in the US armed forces. Miller earned a BS from the University of Arizona.

Kevin Ruggiero named CIO of Parsons Corporation

Parsons

Parsons Corporation, founded in 1944, is a digitally enabled solutions provider with a focus on national security, defense, and global infrastructure. Ruggiero’s most recent role was with Moog and prior to that he had a lengthy career at Northrop Grumman. He earned a BA and MS from the University of Buffalo and an MBA from Indiana University Bloomington.

Urmila Menon joins Tri Pointe Homes as CIO

Tri Pointe Homes

Tri Pointe Homes, a publicly traded company operating in 12 states and the District of Columbia, builds premium homes and communities. Menon held previous roles at ATI Restoration, Smart Energy Water, and Allied Universal. She earned a BE from MKSS Cummins College of Engineering for Women.

Signature Aviation appoints Al Lettera as CDIO

Signature Aviation

Signature Aviation is an aviation hospitality company and operates a network of private aviation terminals, with over 200 locations covering key destinations in 27 countries across five continents. Lettera arrives at Signature Aviation from Tractor Supply Company. He had previous roles at Office Depot and PriceWaterhouseCoopers. Lettera earned a BS from Florida State University.

Boart Longyear names Niel Nickolaisen as CIO

Boart Longyear

Established in 1890, Boart Longyear is a provider of drilling services, drilling equipment, and performance tooling for mining and drilling companies. Nickolaisen joined Boart Longyear from Utah State University. Prior to Utah State, he held roles with Sorenson Communications, OC Tanner, Western Governors University, and Headwaters. Nickolaisen earned a BS and MBA from Utah State and an MS from MIT.

Jordan Ruch named CIO at AtlantiCare

AtlantiCare

AtlantiCare is a healthcare organization with more than 6,500 staff and providers serving the community in more than 100 locations in five southern New Jersey counties. Prior to his role at AtlantiCare, Ruch had a lengthy career with RWJBarnabas Health. He earned a BBA from Loyola University Maryland and an MBA from Amberton University.

Holley Performance Brands names Charan Mann CIO

Holley Performance Brands

Holley Performance Brands designs, manufactures, and markets high-performance products for automotive enthusiasts. Mann was most recently with OneAmerica. She had prior roles at Zeta Interactive, Selligent, and Cummins. Mann earned a BS from Middle Tennessee State University, and an MS and MBA from Lipscomb University.

Heath Tuttle named CIO at the University of Buffalo

University of Buffalo

Founded in 1846, the University of Buffalo is a public research university. Prior to joining, Tuttle had a lengthy career at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. He earned a BS from Chadron State College, an MS from the University of Nebraska at Omaha, and a PhD from the University of Nebraska-Lincoln.

MEC appoints Daniel Bourquin as CIO

MEC

Founded in 1945, MEC is a US-based value-added manufacturing partner that provides a broad range of prototyping and tooling, production fabrication, coating, assembly and aftermarket components. Bourquin held prior roles at Fathom Digital Manufacturing, Generac Power Systems, and PSSI. He holds a BS from the University of Wisconsin-Stevens Point.

George Haddad joins North American Partners in Anesthesia as CIO

NAPA

North American Partners in Anesthesia (NAPA) is the largest anesthesia provider in the US and headquartered in NY. Haddad previously held positions at VUMC, Healthways, and KPMG. He earned a BS from the University of Alabama, Huntsville, and an MS from the Owen School of Business at Vanderbilt University.
