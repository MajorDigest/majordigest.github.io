Intel plans massive layoffs that will slash 15,000 workers worldwide

SANTA CLARA — Intel has disclosed plans for massive layoffs that will lead to a loss of 15,000 jobs worldwide, a restructuring ushered in by “disappointing” results for the legendary tech titan.

The layoff plans emerged in the wake of Intel’s disclosure of the company’s financial results for the April-through-June second quarter.

“Our second quarter financial performance was disappointing, even as we hit key product and process technology milestones,” said Intel chief executive officer Pat Gelsinger. “Second-half trends are more challenging than we previously expected.”

Santa Clara-based Intel said it has decided to slash 15% of its workforce worldwide, which will result in the jaw-dropping loss of 15,000 jobs.

Intel has already conducted multiple rounds of layoffs over the last year that have resulted in significant employment impacts in the Bay Area.

Since the tech industry began significant cutbacks starting in 2022, Intel has disclosed plans to eliminate 1,064 jobs in the Bay Area, affecting employees in Santa Clara and San Jose.

The tech titan has so far lagged in attempting to fully exploit the artificial intelligence wave that is sweeping through virtually the entire tech industry.

“Our revenues have not grown as expected,” Geisinger stated. “We’ve yet to fully benefit from powerful trends like AI.”

During the second quarter that ended in June, Intel lost $1.61 billion on revenue of $12.83 billion.

The loss in the April-through-June quarter contrasted sharply with a profit in the same three-month period a year ago and the tech company’s revenue was down 0.9%.

“Our costs are too high, our margins are too low,” Geisinger said. “We need bolder actions to address both,  particularly given our financial results and outlook for the second half of 2024, which is tougher than previously expected.”

Intel offered a forbidding outlook for the company’s financial prospects during the final six months of 2024 alarmed investors.

During regular trading, Intel’s shares plunged 5.5% and closed at $29.05 a share. But in after-hours trades following the close of the financial markets on Thursday, Intel’s shares nosedived by another 19%, according to the Yahoo Finance site.

“This is an incredibly hard day for Intel as we are making some of the most consequential changes in our company’s history,” Geisinger said. “We must align our cost structure with our new operating model and fundamentally change the way we operate.”

Intel also conceded that it is not nimble enough to keep up with its rivals and must take steps to be a more agile company.

“There’s too much complexity, so we need to both automate and simplify processes,” Geisinger said. “It takes too long for decisions to be made, so we need to eliminate bureaucracy. And there’s too much inefficiency in the system, so we need to expedite workflows.”

Intel vowed to deliver $10 billion in cost savings from now to the end of 2025. The company also said it would launch a voluntary retirement program to further whittle down the size of the workforce.

“I have no illusions that the path in front of us will be easy. You shouldn’t either,” Geisinger said. “This is a tough day for all of us and there will be more tough days ahead. But as difficult as all of this is, we are making the changes necessary to build on our progress and usher in a new era of growth.”
