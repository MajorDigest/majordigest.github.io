On the eve of his one year anniversary as Denmark head coach, Brian Riemer is set to lead his side against Greece as they look to qualify comfortably for the 2026 FIFA World Cup.

The Danes are unbeaten in their last 5 outings, most recently demolishing Belarus 6-0 thanks to goals from Rasmus Hojlund, Andres Dreyer, Patrick Dorgi and Victor Froholdt. Denmark currently sit at the top of Group C, with two wins and one draw and are yet to concede a goal in the qualifying stage.

Riemer has developed a team built on defensive solidity whilst also being able to showcase the nation's up-and-coming attacking talent. Denmark have a strong blend of youthful energy and experience that will make them a dangerous team in North America next year if they do eventually progress through the qualifying stages.

Ivan Jovanovic's Greek side have struggled as of late. Having previously guided his team to a four-game win streak earlier in the year, Jovanovic's side have come unstuck in their last two outings. Those two losses are a huge blow to Greece's participation chances in next year's World Cup.

In their last qualifying game, Greece suffered a vital 3-1 loss to qualification rivals, Scotland. Despite scoring the first goal, a handful of defensive and goalkeeping errors saw Greece capitulate on the pitch. Despite having seasoned pros at their disposal such as Dimitrios Giannoulis, Konstantinos Tsimikas and Christos Tzolis, Greece have now got a mountain to climb if they are to make it to North America next year.

The two sides met last month in a game which resulted in a comfortable 3-0 victory for Denmark and they meet again this Sunday with even higher stakes on the line for both teams in a must watch fixture.
