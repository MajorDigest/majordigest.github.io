Watch United States Grand Prix live streams as Oscar Piastri and Lando Norris are now joined by in-form title outsider Max Verstappen in battling it out for the 2025 Formula 1 drivers' championship with just six races to go.

McLaren teammates Piastri and Norris remain the title favorites, as the latter cut the former's lead at the top of the standing to 22 points at the Singapore Grand Prix two weeks ago. Norris finished third, ahead of Piastri in fourth, but not without controversy after an overtaking incident on the opening lap of the race as the Briton brushed past the Australian. The friendly foes completed McLaren's victory in the constructors' championship last time out.

Defending champion Verstappen finished second to continue his recent improved for in the Red Bull and put the Dutchman back on the big two's radars. George Russell picked up his second win of the 2025 season in a dominant display that he hopes will build confidence into next term in the Mercedes. Lewis Hamilton posted the fastest lap of the race in Singapore and will hope to convert that speed into more points in Texas.

Remember, there's a Sprint race this weekend, meaning more points are up for grabs than usual. That can only be a good thing for Norris, Verstappen and the chasing pack.

Race weekend begins on Friday, with the practice session at the Circuit of the Americas in Austin, Texas. Here’s how to watch the United States Grand Prix online from anywhere and potentially for FREE.

What is the Sprint race format?

The United States Grand Prix in Austin, Texas, is one of six race weekends in 2025 to feature a Sprint. Over a 100km distance, the drivers compete in a fast-moving race designed to encourage lots of overtaking and less strategy.

First to take the chequered flag gets eight points, down to one point for finishing eighth. Sprint qualifying takes place on Friday, with the Sprint race the first track action of the day on Saturday.

Lewis Hamilton recorded his maiden Ferrari win in the China Sprint in March, McLaren's Lando Norris took the flag in Miami in May and Red Bull's Max Verstappen was first in Belgium in July.

After the US, there are Sprints scheduled for Sao Paulo and Qatar.
