Coloradans switching sex designation to 'X' on IDs increasing

DENVER (KDVR) — For over five years, Coloradans have been allowed to switch their sex designation on their driver's license or identification card. Residents can do this without specific surgeries or going to court.

But this wasn't always the case for Colorado.

Coloradans had to go to court for sex designation on legal documents

The option to be listed as "X" on a Colorado license started in late 2018.

This rule came after two Colorado court cases discussed sex designation on federal and state legal documents, according to a 2018 press release from the Colorado Department of Revenue.

One of those cases took place in September 2018. After Zzyym v. Pompeo, the U.S. District Court for the District of Colorado ordered the U.S. Department of State to issue a passport with an alternate sex designation to a Colorado resident.

In another case, B.D. v. CDPHE, a minor's parents sought to have the state’s birth certificate policy declared unconstitutional, according to the press release. This was because the Colorado Department of Public Health and Environment didn't allow a person, at the time, to update the gender marker on their birth certificate unless their sex had been surgically changed.

Current rules for sex designation on legal documents

Now, the Department of Revenue has a webpage with all the information on changing your sex identifier.

According to the website, in Colorado, people can identify as M (male), F (female) or X on their birth certificate, driver's license and identification card.

People above 18 years old or guardians for those under 18 years old can file for a change of gender on legal documents.

Colorado law doesn't require residents to undergo any specific surgery, treatment, clinical care or behavioral health care to update their sex designation, said the website.

Thousands of Coloradans switched to an X-identifier

Since 2018, many Coloradans opted to switch their identification and license.

The Colorado Department of Revenue provided FOX31 with up-to-date data on residents who switched to an X-identifier.

For over five years, more than 4,000 residents switched their identifiers to X.

According to the Department of Revenue, there are no additional costs to adding a non-binary sex identifier to a license or ID, but there are fees to renew or establish a credential.

More than half the states allow sex designation on legal documents

The Movement Advancement Project keeps track of the nation's stances on identity document laws and policies including name change, driver's license and birth certificate.

Almost half the states in the U.S. allow residents to mark M, F or X on their driver's license and have an easy-to-understand form and don't require provider certification, according to the website.

Eight states and two territories require proof of surgery, court order or amended birth certificates, and only two states don't allow for updating gender markings on driver's licenses. These two states are Florida and Kansas.
