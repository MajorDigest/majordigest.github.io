Indy mom charged after 3-year-old's foot turns black from frostbite

INDIANAPOLIS — Court documents reveal that a 3-year-old boy might lose most of his lower leg after his mother kept him in a home without heat until his foot turned black with frostbite.

Amber Griffith, 28, has been arrested and charged with one count of neglect of a dependent resulting in serious bodily injury, a Level 3 felony.

According to court documents, police began investigating the case after Griffith’s son was brought to a hospital on Jan. 19 due to his foot turning black from frostbite.

Police said the child underwent surgery that same day to remove the damaged tissue on his foot with the worst-case scenario being the child having his leg amputated below his knee.

Police ended up speaking to Griffith and found out she lived at a home E. Gimber Street with two relatives. The home had no heat and no running water due to the pipes being frozen, police said.

Court documents reveal that despite Griffith claiming her son’s foot was “slightly discolored” for weeks before the hospital visit, the child was only taken to the hospital once Griffith noticed her own foot was beginning to hurt.

Temperatures were in the single digits in the days leading up to the hospital visit.

"Certainly it's a disturbing case our child abuse detectives are working," said IMPD Officer William Young.

The victim’s aunt and grandmother also lived in the home, but insisted they didn’t notice any signs of frostbite before going to the hospital.

The grandmother said she didn't notice the boy’s foot had turned black with frostbite until attempting to put shoes on him on Jan. 19 to take his mother to the hospital for her foot pain. She did admit to noticing the boy’s legs looking “dry and red” for several days before noticing his foot was black, according to the documents.

"This investigation is still ongoing, but it’s so important to speak up and say something because a lot of times kids can’t speak for themselves," said Young.

"Really it is all of our jobs to protect children and families," said Sarah Cline.

Sarah Cline with the Villages, which oversees Prevent Child Abuse Indiana, reminds everyone to contact the state’s hotline if they see warning signs of abuse or neglect. The state hotline is 1-800-800-5556.

"Child abuse is preventable. We all have a role in preventing it," said Cline.

The case then took an unusual twist after police interviewed Griffith and she made a series of outlandish claims.

According to court records, while talking to police about the alleged neglect, Griffith told police she has given birth to multiple children with no names or social security numbers.

While speaking to Griffith, police reported the woman talking wildly of all sorts of crazed things including that she gave birth to twins in November before she beat one of the babies to death to get it to stop crying.

She even claimed she buried that baby herself.

Griffith reportedly told police she was a user of heroin and meth in addition to smoking marijuana. The grandmother who lived in the Gimber Street home admitted to police to being the child’s primary caregiver due to having concerns over Griffith’s hallucinations and paranoia.

In addition to Griffith’s drug use, the grandmother told police the woman was diagnosed with bipolar and severe depression but has refused to seek out help or treatment.

Despite the bizarre nature of her claims, documents reveal that Griffith had two other children removed from her custody in 2015 after the children were found to be living in a home in filthy conditions without power, heat or running water.

That made her claims credible enough that police searched the home using a cadaver K-9. Police did not find any signs of human remains.

"Our agency takes all allegations extremely seriously and we have to go out and investigate those claims," said Young.

The suspect made her initial court hearing this week and is being held on a $40,000 bond pending trial.

If convicted, Griffith could face between three and 16 years in prison on a Level 3 felony.

At this time, police are unaware of the results of the child's surgery.

Jesse Wells contributed to this report.
