Middle school group wins $1M in Powerball lottery

FLORENCE, Ky. (FOX 56) — A group of current and former employees of a Boone County middle school won $1 million playing Powerball together.

Kentucky Lottery said in a news release on Wednesday that the group of 30 carpooled to the lottery headquarters in Louisville on Tuesday and were greeted with cheers after a ticket tucked into a math textbook turned out to be a big winner.

The Jones 30, named after Rector A. Jones Middle School in Florence, realized on Sunday that they had won, according to the lottery.

A retired math teacher, the group's organizer, was the first to notice, per lottery officials. She kept the ticket safe by hiding it in a math textbook.

“No one looks in a math book,” she joked. “I knew it would be safe there … page 200. I have checked this a thousand times.”

The group said they have been playing the lottery for years.

“We all taught at the same school at one point or another,” one winner said. “We’ve got counselors, admins, special ed teachers, the school nurse … Some have moved on or retired, but we still continue to do it. We’ve remained buddies for all of these years.”

The Jones 30 have been pooling for the lottery for at least eight years. They told the lottery office they picked a set of numbers in 2019 and have been playing them ever since.

“Our math teacher and assistant principal pulled them out of a hat,” one winner explained. “At first, we didn’t have the right amount of numbers to choose from, so we drew again. Thank goodness we did.”

After taxes, each person walked away with a check for $24,000, according to the lottery.

“The Kentucky Lottery’s mission is funding education and creating fun for our players; in short, fueling their imaginations with 'what if we won,'” said Kentucky Lottery President and CEO Mary Harville.

Members of the Jones 30 each have their own designs for the money, some telling Kentucky Lottery they plan to invest it, while others plan to use the money for travel or home repair.

“A lot of us have gone on trips together; we’ve had babies and grandbabies over the years,” one winner shared. “We always have so much fun. It’s just cool to win as a group and share this experience.”

This win won't be the end of the group, though. They said they plan to continue their tradition of playing the Kentucky Lottery as a way of staying in touch.

The Hebron Kroger will receive $10,000 for selling the winning ticket, according to the news release.
