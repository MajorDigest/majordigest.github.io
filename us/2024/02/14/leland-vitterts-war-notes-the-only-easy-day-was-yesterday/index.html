Leland Vittert’s War Notes: The Only Easy Day Was Yesterday

NewsNation Chief Washington Anchor and On Balance host Leland Vittert was a foreign correspondent for four years in Jerusalem. He gives you an early look at tonight’s 7 p.m. ET show. Subscribe to War Notes here.  

The Only Easy Day Was Yesterday

Navy SEALs say about their notorious selection training, “The only easy day was yesterday.” That applies to House Speaker Mike Johnson, who woke up today with an even smaller majority than he had yesterday. 

  • And yesterday didn't go well. Johnson won the vote for the impeachment of Secretary of Homeland Security Alejandro Mayorkas by one vote.  

What's worse for Republicans? The special election in New York proved that Democrats can also win and run on immigration. Tom Suozzi won the congressional seat by nearly eight points in a district that Biden won in 2020 and Republican George Santos turned red in 2022. This district in suburban Queens and Long Island has the kind of voters that Trump won in 2016 and lost in 2020.  

A must-read New York Times write-up from Nicholas Fandos and Katie Glueck says, “The strategy went something like this: Challenge Republicans on issues that they usually monopolize, like crime, taxes and, above all, immigration. Flash an independent streak. And fire up the Democratic base with attacks — in this case, nearly $10 million in ads — on the abortion issue and former President Donald J. Trump, the likely Republican nominee for the White House.”

  • BUT no special election, especially one with eight inches of snow on the ground, predicts the future — just ask Republicans who promised massive victories in the 2022 midterms after Virginia Governor Glenn Youngkin’s win in 2021.  

Zoom out:

  • Fandos and Glueck point out in the article that both Republicans running for president said the special election proves their electoral thesis.
    • They quote Nikki Haley spokeswoman Olivia Perez-Cubas saying, “We just lost another winnable Republican House seat because voters overwhelmingly reject Donald Trump. Until Republicans wake up, we will continue to lose.”
    • Versus what Trump said on Truth Social: “This very foolish woman, Mazi Melesa Pilip, running in a race where she didn’t endorse me and tried to ‘straddle the fence,’ when she would have easily WON if she understood anything about MODERN DAY politics in America.”

Bottom line: Swing voters — who will decide the 2024 presidential election — like those on Long Island want things fixed and their lives to improve. They find the performative battles popular with each party base huge turnoffs. Republicans have utterly failed to fix anything while chasing things like an impeachment of Mayorkas.  

As Prem Thakker (@prem_thakker) at The Intercept headlines, “One Year After East Palestine, Some Senate Republicans ‘Haven’t Looked’ at Rail Safety Bill.” 

  • Republicans made this a signature issue and shamed President Joe Biden into going this Friday, yet they have done nothing to actually help the people they claim to care about. 

Electric Car Obituary

Nowhere is the elite (Ivy League) and regular American divide greater than when it comes to climate change. There’s now evidence of the non-existent demand for electric vehicles (EVs). Wealthy, elite urbanites loved buying them, especially with the tax credits, but now that EV tax credits come with income caps, normal folks don’t want them, even with massive subsidies. 

The Wall Street Journal wrote an obituary to the electric car fad, something our resident car dealer Tom Maoli told you was coming more than a year ago.  

  • They write that “(Ford CEO Jim) Farley and other industry CEOs are still confident that EVs will eventually take off, albeit at a slower pace than initially envisioned. But for now, the massive miscalculation has left the industry in a bind, facing a potential glut of EVs and half-empty factories while still having to meet stricter environmental regulations globally.”

The issue is a mindset. Non-elite Americans are scared of EVs. They don't have garages for them. They don't want to install chargers. They have farther to drive. They are more likely to drive rather than fly on vacation. They spend more time in rural America.  

The Biden administration’s messaging to them actually backfires. Just look at the Rasmussen polling that says only 24% of voters would ban gas-powered cars compared to 81% of Ivy League graduates. 

Watch tonight: Batya Ungar-Sargon will discuss how climate change became the cause du jour of elite America. 

  • Thought bubble: Fighting climate change is expensive and complicated, but this just pushed the issue too far. Now, fighting climate change is a luxury.
