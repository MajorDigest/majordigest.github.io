Indiana man charged for recording dogsitter while nude

FISHERS, Ind. — A Fishers man has been charged with voyeurism after police say he used hidden cameras to record his dogsitter, and possibly more than 15 other unsuspecting women, while they were naked and then tried to intimidate them to not tell anyone.

Kristan P. Reibel, 55, was charged in Hamilton County court Tuesday with two counts of felony voyeurism, and marijuana possession, after victims came forward to the Fishers Police Department. He was booked Wednesday night after there'd been an active warrant for his arrest.

Dogsitter accusations

The investigation into Reibel began in the summer of 2022 after a woman he hired to dogsit contacted police about suspicious activity at his house on Athalene Lane regarding two alarm clocks that had red dots in the middle.

The victim told officers that while staying in Reibel's home she noticed several hidden cameras in both the living room and bedroom areas that she was not made aware of. She told officers that she then gained access to Reibel's iPad and discovered nude photos of her had been saved from the cameras.

When the woman asked Reibel - who was on a trip to Los Angeles - about the cameras, he claimed that his deceased son was the only person who had access to the accounts and that the cameras were there for security.

The victim later returned to the house to get paid for dogsitting and saw that the iPad had been wiped of all digital data.

Interview with Reibel

FPD officers then got a warrant to search Reibel's house on July 5, 2022.

During an interview with the suspect, officers said he again claimed he had no access to the cameras and that they had been set up for security reasons due to "hundreds of kids" having access to his garage code. However, when asked why he doesn't just change the garage code police said Reibel was unable to answer.

Officers further pressed Reibel by confronting him with claims that he had camera access to the cameras. Eventually, FPD said he admitted that he saw the dogsitter in a towel for "approximately 3 seconds" but that he ended up deleting the videos and throwing away the cameras.

At the end of the interview police searched Reibel's home and found three black boxes containing cameras, a Nest camera and several cell phones and iPads.

Further investigation

A review of the devices soon revealed that the dogsitter was not the only victim of Reibel's alleged voyeurism. On the electronics, officers found folders named after females known to Reibel that had been accessed recently and contained videos of the various women naked.

Then, in July of 2022, a woman who identified herself as Reibel's life coach of the past 6 years came to police and said her client had admitted to secretly recording 58 videos of him "mostly having sex" with other women.

Reibel also allegedly told the life coach he'd recorded other women visiting the house while they were naked.

For reasons not made clear in court documents, the case then stalled until new officers were assigned to follow up in June of 2023.

During this renewed investigation, a second woman came forward as a victim.

Another victim

The woman told officers that she'd met Reibel in 2018 and that in April of 2021, he took her out to dinner for her birthday. After dinner, Reibel allegedly invited the woman back to his house to "give her a present."

Once at the house, the woman said Reibel made her a drink and that she "didn't remember much after drinking the beverage." After that, the woman could only recall coming to and finding herself naked in a bedroom while Reibel performed oral sex on her.

The woman said she abruptly stopped Reibel, got clothed and left. She told officers that they did not speak much again until he contacted her in August of 2022 and insisted they meet up.

During a meeting at a cafe, the woman said Reibel admitted he was under investigation for voyeurism.

"According to [victim], Reibel told her that he had recorded 16 women who he had sexual relations with, along with 5 individuals who had watched his dogs," court docs read.

The victim said Reibel implied that she had been recorded while naked both the night of her birthday dinner and other times when she had dogsat. Police later confirmed that photos of the woman had been saved.

The woman told police she thought Reibel then tried to "intimidate her" into not speaking up about the crime.

Aftermath

In court documents filed on Tuesday, Fishers police concluded that at least two people were victims of Reibel's actions.

He is now being charged with voyeurism, which is enhanced to a Level 6 Felony when committed with a camera.

According to Hamilton County prosecutors, it is believed that there are more victims of Reibel's voyeurism that have yet to come forward.

Anyone with information on the investigation can contact FPD at (317) 595-3300 or call (317) 262-TIPS to remain anonymous.
