Bear, 'the dog with a box on its head,' has finally been caught

MOBILE, Ala. (WKRG) — The "dog with a box on his head" for almost a year has finally been captured, and he's now at the City of Mobile Animal Shelter.

Here's the full story, in case you missed it, along with what this Cane Corso is up to now.

A dog with a box on his head

In late January, News 5 exclusively reported about the "dog with a box on its head."

People passing by the James T. Strickland Youth Center would see this dog and worry for its safety.

We learned that the dog had been roaming that area for months with something on his head; for simplicity's sake, we just called it a box.

The dog must have been able to eat and drink; otherwise, it wouldn't have survived.

Still, the situation raised concerns.

The dog was too large to fit inside a standard-size trap, and Mobile's Animal Services Department couldn't get close enough to remove the box, they said.

Food-based traps wouldn't work, either, because the dog appeared to be well-fed.

No more box, but on the loose

On Wednesday, Feb. 21, the City of Mobile Animal Shelter and the Mobile County Animal Shelter had a breakthrough: they finally got close enough to the dog to remove the box from its head.

Martin Miller, a “Scottish Dog rescuer living in the Deep South,” shared the long-awaited update on his Instagram page.

But then the dog, named Bear by a former owner, fled the scene.

We did get to learn more about the "box," though.

“The best way to describe it is the top of a trash can, like a public trash can that you would see out on the street or in a fast food restaurant,” City of Mobile Animal Services Director Robert Bryant said in an interview with News 5's Cherish Lombard.

“You know, you walk by, throw your trash in it, and then it closes. It was basically one of those as if the dog were going through the trash somewhere and couldn’t get the box back off his head when he stopped.”

He's safe!

That brings us to today, when the City of Mobile Animal Services Department announced on Facebook that Bear was finally safe.

"Bear has made it to COMAS!" the post said. "Bear, the Cane Corso known for previously having a box stuck on his head, was caught by our Animal Control Officers over the weekend."

The post called Bear "a sweet gentleman" and noted he's in good health.

"Thank you to our Animal Control Officers for their hard work!"

This is a developing story. News 5 will update this article as more information becomes available.
