Father of murdered trans teen Pauly Likens speaks out

SHARON, Pa., (WKBN) - A Pennsylvania community is looking to heal after one of their own was murdered late last month.

Investigators said the remains of Pauly Likens, 14, were found at the Shenango River Reservoir on June 26.

According to Pennsylvania State Police, Pauly, who identified as a transgender girl, was allegedly killed and dismembered by DaShawn Watkins, 22.

When the 14-year-old was killed, many lost a friend and — for some — a child.

Her father, Paul Likens Sr., spoke about what he's been going through since that terrible day.

"I said, 'I love you and I'll give you a call when I get home Monday after work,'" said Likens Sr.

He said that is the last thing he said to Pauly.

"It went straight to voicemail," he said. "Something told me that was wrong, something was going on."

Likens said he went to the last place Pauly was seen and continued looking.

"I knew, but I still had that hope," he said.

Now he is coping with feelings of anger, rage, sadness and sorrow.

"But if I release that out into the world, that's not going to do anyone any good," he said.

Despite all the darkness and pain in the past two weeks, the light of Pauly's pure and beautiful soul will be remembered for all the love and generosity that poured out.

"I sit with God, so God's got my back. He's been holding me up, making sure I don't fall and I'm going to make it," Likens Sr. said.

Pamela Ladner, founder of the LGBTQIA Alliance Shenango Valley, called the teen's death a tragedy and that the victim's mother wants her to be remembered for who she was.

“For her, the most important thing was that Pauly was recognized for the loving selfless person that she was. And for the hate to stop,” Ladner said. “Mom just wanted to make sure everyone knew that Pauly did identify as a transgender female. And very much so embraced that feminist side of them.”

TJ Renninger and Jason Vinkler contributed to this report.
