Save energy and money with these products

Air conditioning, refrigeration, automatic dishwashing and laundry: All these modern conveniences are irreplaceable parts of our lives, but they can also contribute to expensive energy bills. If you’re hoping to save more money, or if you’re still using an old and outdated appliance, upgrading it can significantly reduce your carbon footprint, as well as your energy costs. From smart thermostats to portable air conditioners, these energy-saving devices can help you live comfortably, use less energy and save more money.

In this article: Ecobee ecobee3 Lite Smart Thermostat, LG High-Efficiency Stackable Front-Load Washer with 6Motion Technology and Amazon Smart Plug

Energy-efficient upgrades

Upgrading to a newer appliance not only means improved features and performance, it often comes with a huge improvement in energy consumption. For example, it’s estimated that refrigerators that are at least 15 years old use twice as much energy as newer ones, while newer dishwashers use about half the water and energy compared to older ones.

Many new appliances are also Energy Star-certified, which means they meet performance requirements and energy usage criteria set by the Environmental Protection Agency. These appliances, available in 40 product categories, have been tested and reviewed by a third party to ensure they meet these criteria. Since the Energy Star program was introduced in 1992, it’s estimated to have reduced energy costs by $500 billion dollars while also reducing greenhouse gas emissions by 4 billion metric tons.

Consider these other energy-saving features as you shop for a new appliance.

  • Air fryers or air-frying toasters: These compact appliances cook food more quickly and efficiently than ovens, resulting in a lower cost to run.
  • Where your fridge’s freezer is matters: Top- and bottom-freezer refrigerators are the most energy-efficient models.
  • Induction wins: Induction ranges are more energy-efficient than electric and gas models. However, both coil and smooth-top electric ranges are more efficient than gas.
  • Install a ceiling fan: Ceiling fans use very little energy and can help you save on your energy bills in both summer and winter.

Energy-efficient tips for home

You don’t need all-new, top-of-the-line appliances to save money on your energy bill. Using your appliances wisely, monitoring your heating and cooling practices, and other steps can help you save money without having to replace every appliance in your home.

Try these energy-efficient home tricks:

  • Insulate and seal gaps: Check your home for gaps and cracks around doors and windows and in your attic and basement, if you have one.
  • Use the convection setting: When possible, run your oven on convection mode, which uses up to 20% less energy compared to the standard baking mode.
  • Launder with cold water: Washing your clothes in cold water saves energy and gets clothing just as clean, especially if you use a cold-water detergent.
  • Freeze your own ice: Automatic icemakers and through-the-door ice and water dispensers consume up to 20% more energy than refrigerators without these features.
  • Check for tax credits: You may be eligible for a tax break for switching to energy-efficient home features such as a new water heater, home insulation, windows and more.
