Where to donate blood to help American Red Cross, Helene & Milton victims

CHICAGO (WGN) — In the wake of Hurricane Helene and the lead-up to Hurricane Milton, the American Red Cross is in critical need of blood donations to help address the devastation left behind by the two hurricanes.

Since making landfall, Helene and its aftermath have led to the cancellation of more than 100 blood drives, causing thousands of blood and platelet donations to go uncollected. Blood transfusions are essential for so many people living with urgent medical care needs, and donations in the Southeast are key to keeping our nationwide network stocked. The Red Cross operates through a national inventory with the ability to move blood wherever and whenever needed.

Red Cross officials said anyone interested in donating to help hurricane recovery efforts can do so by visiting RedCrossBlood.org, calling 1-800-RED CROSS (1-800-733-2767), or by using the Red Cross Blood Donor App.

*Further instructions on how to set up a donation appointment can be found at the bottom of this article.

As the Red Cross continues to urge people to give blood or platelets as soon as possible, their teams are also on the ground working to provide vital relief − like food, water, shelter and support − in communities devastated by Helene and in the path of Milton. If you’re able to help those impacted by disasters big and small across the country, like these two hurricanes, the Red Cross asks you to consider making a gift of any amount.

According to Red Cross officials, donors outside of affected areas are encouraged to make an appointment to give now and help keep the blood supply stable in the weeks to come.

All who come to give blood, platelets or plasma Oct. 1-31 will receive a $10 Amazon.com Gift Card by email, plus be automatically entered for a chance to win one of three $5,000 gift cards. For full details, visit RedCrossBlood.org/Treat.

How to donate blood

Download the American Red Cross Blood Donor App, visit RedCrossBlood.org, call 1-800-RED CROSS (1-800-733-2767) or enable the Blood Donor Skill on any Alexa Echo device to make an appointment or for more information. All blood types are needed to ensure a reliable supply for patients. A blood donor card or driver’s license or two other forms of identification are required at check-in. Individuals who are 17 years of age in most states (16 with parental consent where allowed by state law), weigh at least 110 pounds and are in generally good health may be eligible to donate blood. High school students and other donors 18 years of age and younger also have to meet certain height and weight requirements.

Blood and platelet donors can save time at their next donation by using RapidPass® to complete their pre-donation reading and health history questionnaire online, on the day of their donation, before arriving at the blood drive. To get started, follow the instructions at RedCrossBlood.org/RapidPass or use the Blood Donor App.

Amplify your impact − volunteer!  

Another way to support the lifesaving mission of the Red Cross is to become a volunteer blood donor ambassador at Red Cross blood drives. Blood donor ambassadors help greet, check in and thank blood donors to ensure they have a positive donation experience.  

Volunteers can also serve as transportation specialists, playing a vital role in ensuring lifesaving blood products are delivered to nearby hospitals. For more information and to apply for either position, visit redcross.org/volunteertoday.
